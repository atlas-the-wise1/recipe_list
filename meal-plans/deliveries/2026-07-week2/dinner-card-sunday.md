# Dinner Card - Sunday
**Label:** Meal Prep Option
**Recipe:** Air-Fryer Chicken Breast
**Total Time:** About 25 minutes
**Energy Level:** Medium
**Cleanup:** Light
## Before Work or the Night Before
- No advance prep needed.
## Pull Out
- 1 large boneless, skinless chicken breast, about 11 ounces
- Salt
- Freshly cracked black pepper
- 2 teaspoons light brown sugar
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon low-sodium soy sauce
- 2 teaspoons olive oil
- Lime wedge, optional, for serving
## Cook
1. Heat the air fryer to 375 F, if preheating is recommended for your model.
2. Pat the chicken breast dry with a paper towel. Cut the breast in half crosswise into two pieces of equal weight, creating one shorter, thicker piece and one longer, thinner piece. Season generously with salt and pepper, then rub the brown sugar all over the chicken. If you want, season ahead and let it rest uncovered in the refrigerator for up to 12 hours. Bring the chicken to room temperature before cooking.
3. In a medium bowl, combine the rice vinegar, soy sauce, and olive oil. Toss the chicken in the marinade and let it sit for 5 minutes.
4. Transfer the chicken to the air fryer basket, setting any leftover marinade aside. Cook for 6 minutes, then flip the chicken over. Spoon the remaining marinade over the chicken and cook until lightly browned, the sauce is slightly sticky, and the internal temperature reaches 165 F, another 6 to 10 minutes depending on thickness.
5. Transfer the chicken to a cutting board and rest for at least 5 minutes. Slice against the grain and serve with the pan juices and an optional squeeze of lime.
## While It Cooks
- Storage: 3 days fridge
- Put away unused ingredients.
