# Bricklayer-Style Nachos

**Category:** L — Lunch
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#crowd-pleaser`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 820kcal |
| Protein | 45g |
| Carbs | 42g |
| Fat | 52g |
| Fiber | 4g |
| Cholesterol | 160mg |
**Health Score:** 4/10

**Prep Time:** 20 min | **Cook Time:** 30 min | **Total:** ~50 min

---

## Ingredients

- 2 pounds ripe Roma tomatoes, OR 2 (15-oz) cans fire-roasted tomatoes
- 8 oz thick-cut bacon, thinly sliced
- 8 oz fresh Mexican chorizo, casing removed, coarsely chopped
- 1½ pounds beef sirloin, excess fat removed, cut into ½-inch pieces
- Kosher or sea salt and black pepper
- 1 medium white onion, halved and slivered (about 1½ cups)
- 1–2 jalapeños or serrano chiles, halved, deseeded if desired, sliced
- 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1–1½ pounds store-bought or homemade tortilla chips
- 12 oz shredded Mexican melty cheese (Oaxaca, Asadero, quesadilla, Monterey Jack, or mozzarella) — about 3 cups
- 1 ripe avocado, halved, pitted, finely chopped
- 1 cup crumbled queso fresco
- 8 scallions, trimmed and thinly sliced

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** If using fresh tomatoes, place on a foil-lined baking sheet. Broil 8–10 minutes, turning halfway, until charred, mushy, and juices run. Once cool enough to handle, chop without discarding juices, seeds, or charred skin.

**STEP 2:** Heat a large (12-inch) skillet over medium-high. Add bacon and cook, stirring occasionally, until slightly browned and fat renders — 3 to 4 minutes.

**STEP 3:** Add chorizo to the bacon. Cook, breaking into smaller pieces with a wooden spoon, until browned and crisp — 3 to 4 minutes.

**STEP 4:** Add beef, season with salt and pepper, cook stirring a couple times until it begins to brown — about 4 minutes. Add onion and jalapeño, cook until they begin to wilt — 2 to 3 minutes. Add garlic, stir until fragrant but not browned — less than 1 minute.

**STEP 5:** Stir in chopped tomatoes with their juices. Cook over high heat, stirring occasionally, until saucy — 2 to 3 minutes. Season to taste with salt.

**STEP 6:** Set rack in middle of oven and heat to 400°F. Spread tortilla chips in an even layer on a half sheet pan. Cover chips with the bricklayer meat mixture and all its chunky salsa. Cover with shredded cheese. Bake until cheese is completely melted — 8 to 10 minutes.

**STEP 7:** Garnish with avocado, crumbled queso fresco, and scallions. Serve immediately while hot.

---

## Notes

- **Not a health-goals recipe** — this is a treat/occasion dish (bacon + chorizo + beef + cheese = calorie-dense; ~20g saturated fat per serving)
- **Lighter swap:** Sub turkey chorizo + lean ground turkey for the beef, skip bacon → cuts ~200 cal/serving, drops saturated fat significantly. See `bricklayer-nachos-healthier.md` for the full healthier variant.
- **Serves 6–8** as a meal; 10–12 as an appetizer/party spread
- **Fire-roasted canned tomatoes** are a great shortcut and add deep flavor — skip the broiling step entirely
- **Make-ahead:** Bricklayer meat mixture can be made 1–2 days ahead, refrigerated, and reheated before assembling on chips
