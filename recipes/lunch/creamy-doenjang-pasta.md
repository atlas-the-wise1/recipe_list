# Creamy Doenjang Pasta

**Category:** L — Lunch
**Tags:** `#vegetarian` `#quick`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 650kcal |
| Protein | 28g |
| Carbs | 82g |
| Fat | 23g |
| Fiber | 3g |
| Cholesterol | 230mg |
**Health Score:** 5/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 5 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~25 min

---

## Ingredients

- Salt
- 1 pound Banza chickpea linguine, bucatini, or spaghetti
- 4 large eggs, soft-boiled *(1 per serving)*
- 2 cups whole milk
- ¼ cup doenjang *(Korean soybean paste — see Tip)*
- 2 tablespoons mirin
- Freshly ground black pepper
- ¼ cup unsalted butter
- Grated Parmesan, for serving

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Bring a large, wide pot of lightly salted water to a boil over high heat. Cook pasta until about 2 minutes shy of al dente (per package instructions) — it should still be slightly uncooked in the center; it'll finish in the sauce. Reserve 1 cup pasta cooking water, drain, and return pasta to the empty pot. While pasta cooks, pour milk into a large measuring cup or bowl. Whisk in doenjang, mirin, and a generous crack of black pepper until doenjang fully dissolves.

**STEP 2:** To the pasta pot, add butter, the milk mixture, and half the reserved pasta water. Bring to a simmer over high heat. Cook, stirring constantly and vigorously with a wooden spoon, until sauce reduces and thickens significantly — coating the noodles like thin cream, 5 to 7 minutes. Taste and season with salt if needed.

**STEP 3:** While pasta finishes, soft-boil eggs: lower into boiling water, cook 6–7 minutes, transfer to ice bath, peel and halve.

**STEP 4:** Divide among plates. Top with a halved soft-boiled egg, grated Parmesan, and another crack of black pepper. Serve immediately.

---

## Tip
Doenjang (Korean soybean paste) is often labeled "soybean paste" and found at Korean or Asian supermarkets, or online — in jars or rectangular plastic tubs. Funkily pungent and deeply savory, it's similar to Japanese miso but saltier and more assertive. A small amount delivers enormous umami — fermented sourness, deep Parmesan-like depth, and unparalleled brio in soups, stews, and sauces.

---

## Notes

- **Doenjang ≠ miso** — don't sub miso 1:1; doenjang is saltier and more pungent; start with 3 tablespoons if you're new to it and adjust up
- **Stir constantly in step 2** — this isn't a passive simmer; vigorous stirring emulsifies the milk and butter into a cohesive sauce rather than breaking
- **Reserve that pasta water** — the starch is essential for thickening; don't skip it even if the sauce looks fine without it
- **Serve immediately** — the sauce tightens as it sits and Banza absorbs liquid faster than wheat pasta; don't let it wait
- **Banza note:** Banza chickpea pasta adds ~4g protein and ~3g fiber vs. standard pasta; watch cook time — Banza can go mushy faster, pull it 2-3 min early
- **Soft-boil timing:** 6 min = jammy/runny yolk, 7 min = slightly set — both work; ice bath stops the cook immediately
- **Umami upgrade:** Add a teaspoon of doenjang as a finishing stir after plating for extra punch at the table
