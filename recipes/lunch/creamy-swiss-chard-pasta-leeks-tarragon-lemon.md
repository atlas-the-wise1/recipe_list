# Creamy Swiss Chard Pasta With Leeks, Tarragon and Lemon Zest

**Category:** L — Lunch
**Tags:** `#vegetarian` `#high-protein` `#meal-prep`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 600kcal |
| Protein | 26g |
| Carbs | 52g |
| Fat | 33g |
| Fiber | 7g |
| Cholesterol | 85mg |
**Serves:** 6
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 30 min | **Total:** ~45 min

---

## Ingredients

- ¾ pound green Swiss chard (1 large bunch), washed and trimmed
- 2 large leeks (10–12 oz each)
- 5 tablespoons unsalted butter
- ½ cup panko bread crumbs
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 3 tablespoons nutritional yeast
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves
- 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 16 ounces Banza chickpea linguine
- ¾ cup finely grated Parmesan
- Fresh tarragon leaves, for garnish
- 1 lemon, for zest garnish

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Tear chard leaves off the stems. Thinly slice the stems; coarsely tear the leaves into bite-size pieces. Set aside in separate bowls.

**STEP 2:** Trim leeks — remove the bottom root end and dark green tops, keeping only the white and pale green portion. Halve lengthwise, then thinly slice crosswise. Wash, drain, and set aside.

**STEP 3:** In a large skillet, melt 2 tablespoons butter over medium. Add panko, season lightly with salt and generously with pepper, and cook, stirring occasionally, until golden and toasted, 3–4 minutes. Stir in nutritional yeast, then transfer to a paper towel-lined plate.

**STEP 4:** Wipe out the skillet. Melt remaining 3 tablespoons butter over medium-high. Add leeks, chard stems, garlic, and thyme; season with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, until leeks start to wilt and soften, about 5 minutes. Add torn chard leaves and cook, stirring, until just wilted, 2 minutes.

**STEP 5:** Add stock and cream. Boil over high heat until thickened, about 10 minutes.

**STEP 6:** Once the sauce is simmering, add pasta to the boiling water and cook until al dente. Drain.

**STEP 7:** Transfer chard mixture to the empty pasta pot. Add cooked pasta and stir to combine. Sprinkle with Parmesan and stir vigorously until melted into the sauce. Season to taste with salt and pepper. Top with bread crumbs, fresh tarragon, and a generous grating of lemon zest. Serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- **Banza note:** Pull pasta 2–3 min early — Banza absorbs liquid faster than wheat pasta and can go mushy; it'll finish in the sauce
- **Use veggie stock** to keep this fully vegetarian — chicken stock adds depth but either works
- **Nutritional yeast** adds umami and a cheesy depth without extra saturated fat — don't skip it
- **Tarragon is key** — don't sub dried; fresh tarragon has a distinct anise brightness that makes this dish. If you can't find it, fresh basil is the closest substitute
- **Lemon zest last** — grate it on right before serving; the oils fade fast once it hits hot food
- **Reheat tip:** Add a splash of stock or water when reheating — the Banza will have absorbed most of the sauce overnight
