# Brussels Sprouts Caesar Salad

**Tags:** `#heart-healthy` `#low-cholesterol` `#vegetarian` `#meal-prep`

**Health Score:** 7/10

**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

## Ingredients

- 2 pounds brussels sprouts, trimmed
- ½ cup + 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, plus more as needed
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 small baguette or chunk of sourdough (about 8 oz), preferably day-old, torn into bite-size pieces (~4 cups)
- ½ cup grated Pecorino Romano (~3 oz), plus more as needed
- 1½ tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice, plus more as needed
- 3 garlic cloves, finely grated or minced

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Heat oven to 425°F. Cut about 1¾ pounds of the brussels sprouts in half lengthwise and place on a rimmed sheet pan along with any loose leaves. Drizzle with 1 tablespoon oil, a large pinch of salt and black pepper. Toss well, spread in an even layer. Roast until golden brown and tender, 12–20 minutes, tossing halfway through.

**STEP 2:** On a second baking sheet, toss bread pieces with 2 tablespoons olive oil to coat evenly. Spread in an even layer, sprinkle with salt. Bake in the same oven until crunchy and evenly browned, 14–18 minutes, tossing halfway through. Taste a crouton and add more salt if needed. Let cool on the baking sheet.

**STEP 3:** While sprouts and croutons roast, use a food processor with slicing disk (or a chef's knife) to thinly slice the remaining ¾ pound brussels sprouts. Place shaved sprouts in a large bowl.

**STEP 4:** Make the dressing: In a small bowl, combine cheese, lemon juice, garlic, ¼ teaspoon salt, and several grinds of black pepper. Slowly whisk in remaining ½ cup olive oil until emulsified. Taste and adjust — more salt, lemon, or pepper as needed.

**STEP 5:** Pour just enough dressing over shaved raw brussels sprouts to coat. Massage dressing into the sprouts with your hands. Taste and add more dressing if needed.

**STEP 6:** Add croutons and roasted brussels sprouts to the bowl and toss well. Finish with more cheese, salt, olive oil, and lemon juice to taste. Salad should be lively in flavor and nicely coated but not oily. Serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- **Dual texture is the move** — raw shaved sprouts + roasted halves gives this real depth; don't skip either
- **Gluten-free swap:** Use GF bread or skip croutons entirely; add toasted pine nuts or pepitas for crunch
- **Boost protein:** Top with a soft-boiled egg, grilled chicken, or shaved prosciutto to turn this into a full meal (~25–35g protein)
- **Day-old bread** makes noticeably better croutons — worth planning ahead
- **No anchovy** — this Caesar skips the traditional anchovy, making it fully vegetarian; if you want that umami depth, add 1–2 anchovy fillets to the dressing
- **Make-ahead:** Roasted sprouts and croutons can be made a few hours ahead; assemble just before serving
