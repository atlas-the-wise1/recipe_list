# Frijoles Borrachos (Drunken Beans)

---

## Ingredients

- 1 pound dried pinto beans
- 5 slices thick-cut turkey bacon, thinly sliced crosswise
- 1 large yellow or white onion, coarsely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, chopped
- 2 jalapeño, serrano, or poblano peppers, seeded and coarsely chopped
- Handful of cilantro sprigs — stems finely chopped, leaves left whole
- 2 medium to large tomatoes, coarsely chopped
- Salt
- 12 oz light or dark Mexican beer (1 bottle or can)

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** In a large bowl, cover beans with plenty of water and soak 8 to 12 hours.

**STEP 2:** When ready to cook, add bacon to a large Dutch oven or pot over medium heat. Cook, stirring occasionally, until golden and crisp — 5 to 8 minutes. Use a slotted spoon to transfer bacon to a plate, leaving the fat in the pot.

**STEP 3:** Add onions, garlic, peppers, and cilantro stems to the bacon fat. Cook until softened and browned in spots — 10 to 12 minutes. Stir in tomatoes and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom. Use a slotted spoon to transfer beans to the pot. Pour in enough soaking liquid to cover beans by 1 inch (use fresh water if soaking liquid runs out). Season generously with salt. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to a simmer. Cook until beans are tender but not yet soft or falling apart — 1 to 1½ hours. Check periodically; if water level drops below beans, add soaking liquid or fresh water to cover.

**STEP 4:** Taste the bean liquid — if it tastes dull or murky, add salt. Pour in the beer and gently simmer until liquid has thickened slightly and beans are soft and creamy — 30 to 40 minutes. Taste more than one bean; they should flatten without much effort when pressed between fingers. Stir in reserved bacon, then season to taste with salt. Serve topped with reserved cilantro leaves.

**Storage:** Keeps refrigerated up to 5 days.

---

## Notes

- **Meal prep anchor** — makes a big batch, lasts all week, gets better on day 2–3 as flavors meld
- **Lighter swap:** Skip bacon entirely and use 2 tablespoons olive oil — loses some smokiness but stays rich; drops another ~40 cal/serving
- **Beer choice:** Light Mexican lager (Modelo, Pacifico) = cleaner flavor; dark (Negra Modelo) = deeper, maltier beans — both work great
- **Serving ideas:** Over rice, with warm tortillas, alongside eggs for breakfast, or as a side to grilled chicken/fish
- **Freeze well:** Portion into containers and freeze up to 3 months — reheat with a splash of water
- **Soak tip:** If short on time, quick-soak: boil beans 2 minutes, turn off heat, soak 1 hour, drain and proceed
