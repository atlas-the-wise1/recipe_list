# Buttery Lemon Pasta With Almonds and Arugula — Healthier Variant

**What changed vs. classic:** ~130 fewer calories, ~12g less fat, ~10g less saturated fat per serving. Achieves this by:
- Replacing **6 tbsp butter** with **3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil** — keeps the richness, eliminates saturated fat almost entirely
- Using **Banza chickpea linguine or spaghetti** — adds 3g fiber per serving
- **Boosting greens** from 4–5 oz to 6 oz for more volume and micronutrients
- The toasted almonds and rosemary technique remains identical — it's what makes the dish

---

## Ingredients

- Fine salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 pound **whole-wheat** linguine or spaghetti
- **3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil** (replaces 6 tbsp butter)
- 1 cup sliced almonds
- 2 fresh rosemary sprigs
- ½ teaspoon red-pepper flakes, plus more to taste
- ¼ cup freshly squeezed lemon juice, plus more to taste
- 1 tablespoon finely grated lemon zest
- **6 oz** baby or wild arugula, coarsely chopped (or baby kale or spinach) — about 6 cups
- Grated Parmesan, for serving

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a boil. Add pasta and cook until 1 minute shy of al dente. Scoop out about 1½ cups pasta water, then drain pasta.

**STEP 2:** While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add almonds, rosemary, and red-pepper flakes. Cook, stirring occasionally, until nuts are toasted and golden, **2–3 minutes**. Watch carefully — almonds toast faster in olive oil than butter.

**STEP 3:** Add about 1 cup pasta water to the skillet and bring to a simmer. Add lemon juice, zest, ½ teaspoon salt, and a large pinch of black pepper. Add drained pasta and toss well. Add arugula, tossing until it wilts. Simmer another minute if needed to thicken sauce. If mixture seems dry, add more pasta water 1 tablespoon at a time.

**STEP 4:** Taste and adjust — more salt, red-pepper flakes, and lemon juice as needed. Serve topped with grated Parmesan and more red-pepper flakes if desired.

---

## Notes

- **Olive oil vs. brown butter:** You lose the nutty brown-butter aroma, but the toasted almonds pick up the slack — you still get deep, nutty flavor. The lemon and rosemary are just as bright.
- **Whole-wheat pasta note:** Add 1–2 extra minutes to the cook time and test frequently — whole-wheat can go from al dente to mushy quickly.
- **Boost protein further:** Add grilled shrimp, white beans, or a soft-boiled egg per bowl
- **Make-ahead:** Still not great for leftovers — the pasta absorbs sauce quickly. Best served immediately.
