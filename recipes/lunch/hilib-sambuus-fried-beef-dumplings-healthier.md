# Hilib Sambuus — Leaner Turkey Version

**Category:** L — Lunch
**Healthier variant of:** `hilib-sambuus-fried-beef-dumplings.md`
**Tags:** `#meal-prep` `#low-cholesterol`
**Nutrition (per serving, ~4 sambuus):**
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 450kcal |
| Protein | 22g |
| Carbs | 35g |
| Fat | 26g |
| Fiber | 2g |
| Cholesterol | 50mg |
**Serves:** 5 (20 sambuus total)
**Prep Time:** 30 min | **Cook Time:** 25 min | **Total:** ~55 min

---

**Health Score:** 4/10

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 medium white onion, diced
- 4 to 5 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 pound ground turkey (93/7)
- 1 handful cilantro leaves, finely chopped
- 1 tablespoon ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon ground coriander
- 1½ teaspoons fine sea salt
- ½ cup / 64g all-purpose flour
- 5 burrito-size (9-inch-wide) flour tortillas
- Canola oil, as needed for frying
- Basbaas Cagaar (Green Hot Sauce), for serving *(optional)*

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** In a medium nonstick skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high. Add onion and cook until soft, stirring occasionally, 6–7 minutes. Add garlic and cook until soft, about 2 minutes. Add beef and brown, breaking up clumps, about 10 minutes. Off heat, stir in cilantro, cumin, coriander, and salt.

**STEP 2:** In a small bowl, make a paste with the flour and ½ cup water. Cut each tortilla into 4 quarters, creating 20 triangular wedges. Warm 6–7 pieces at a time in the microwave for about 10 seconds (or in a 350°F oven) to make them pliable.

**STEP 3:** On a clean surface, position one tortilla wedge with the round end closest to you. Fold the bottom-left tip up and over to meet the right edge slightly above its midpoint — forming a cone. Brush flour paste over the folded surface. Bring the bottom-right tip up to cover the flap, completing the cone. Fill the cone with 2 tablespoons of beef filling. Tuck the inner flap to seal, brush paste on top, then fold the top flap down and press to seal into a triangle. Use paste to fill any gaps. Set on a baking sheet and repeat with remaining filling.

**STEP 4:** Pour ½ inch canola oil into a large deep skillet and heat over medium-high until shimmering (a drop of flour paste should sputter). Fry 4–6 sambuus at a time until golden brown and crispy, 1–2 minutes per side. Drain on a paper towel-lined plate. Serve hot with green hot sauce if desired.

---

## Notes

- **Freeze well:** Assemble unfried sambuus and freeze on a baking sheet, then transfer to a bag — fry straight from frozen, adding 1–2 min to cook time
- **Bake option:** Brush with oil and bake at 400°F for 20–25 min, flipping halfway — less crispy but saves ~120 cal per serving
- **vs. original:** Saves ~110 cal and ~10g fat per serving vs. the beef version — same flavor, better macros for fat-loss weeks
- **Filling ahead:** The beef filling keeps refrigerated 3 days — make it Sunday, assemble and fry fresh during the week
- **Seal tight:** Any gap in the flour paste seal = filling leaks into oil; take your time pressing the edges
