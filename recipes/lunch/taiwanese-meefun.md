# Taiwanese Meefun

**Category:** L — Lunch
**Tags:** `#vegetarian` `#meal-prep` `#quick`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Carbs | Fat | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~520 | ~19g | ~46g | ~28g | ~4g | ~105mg |
**Health Score:** 6/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

## Ingredients

- 5 ounces dried rice vermicelli
- 4 dried shiitake mushrooms
- 5 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 2 eggs, lightly beaten
- 2 large shallots, thinly sliced
- Kosher salt
- 1 large carrot, cut into thin matchsticks, about 2 1/2 cups
- 1/4 small green cabbage, shredded, about 2 1/2 cups
- 8 ounces baked or smoked tofu, cut into thin matchsticks, about 2 cups
- 3 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
- 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
- Chile oil, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. In a medium bowl, cover the rice vermicelli in cold water and soak until softened, 5 to 10 minutes. Drain and set aside. Soak the dried shiitake mushrooms in warm water until hydrated and softened, about 10 minutes, then drain and slice 1/4-inch thick.
2. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a large, deep skillet over medium. Add the beaten eggs and swirl to create an even layer. Cook until set and cooked through, about 1 minute. Transfer to a cutting board, let cool slightly, then cut into matchsticks. Set aside.
3. Return the skillet to the stove and raise the heat to medium-high. Add the remaining 3 tablespoons oil, the shallots, and shiitake mushrooms. Season with salt. Cook, stirring frequently, until the edges of the shallots begin to brown, 3 to 4 minutes.
4. Add the carrot, season with salt, and cook, stirring frequently, until softened but still crisp, 1 to 2 minutes. Add the cabbage, season with salt, and cook, stirring frequently, until slightly wilted, 1 to 2 minutes.
5. Add the tofu, soy sauce, drained rice vermicelli, and 1 cup water, and cook, stirring frequently, until the noodles absorb the water, 5 to 6 minutes. Season with salt and white pepper, then stir in the reserved eggs and cilantro. Serve topped with chile oil.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 3 days fridge
- **Reheat:** Rewarm in a skillet with a splash of water or microwave covered until hot
- **Batch size:** Best at 4 servings, but can scale to 6 if you want a lighter lunch portion

---

## Notes

- This is a solid lunch-prep noodle bowl: lots of texture, decent protein from tofu and eggs, and enough vegetables to keep it from feeling like a pure starch dish.
- The score sits in the middle because the vegetable load helps, but the oil and noodle base keep calories meaningful.
- If you want a lighter version later, cut the oil first and keep the tofu, mushrooms, and cabbage.
