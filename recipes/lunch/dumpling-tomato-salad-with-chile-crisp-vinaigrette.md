# Dumpling Tomato Salad With Chile Crisp Vinaigrette

**Category:** Lunch
**Tags:** `#fat-loss`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~420 | ~18g | ~18g | ~44g | ~4g | ~35mg |
**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 10 min | **Total:** ~25 min

---

## Ingredients

- 2 1/2 pounds ripe tomatoes, cut into 1- to 2-inch pieces
- 1 garlic clove, grated
- 1/2 cup basil leaves, torn
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 1 pound frozen potsticker dumplings, not thawed
- 1 to 2 tablespoons neutral oil, such as canola or vegetable
- 1 to 2 tablespoons crispy fried shallots, optional
- 3 tablespoons chile crisp or chile oil
- 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce, plus more to taste

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Place the tomatoes in a large serving bowl or on a serving platter. Add the garlic, half the basil, 1 teaspoon salt, and a big pinch of black pepper. Toss gently and set aside.

**STEP 2:** Make the vinaigrette by combining the chile crisp, rice vinegar, and soy sauce in a small bowl. Whisk until blended. Taste and add a little more soy sauce if you want it saltier.

**STEP 3:** Heat a large 12-inch nonstick skillet or well-seasoned cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat for 1 to 2 minutes, until very hot. Add the neutral oil, then work in batches if needed and place the dumplings flat-side down in the skillet. Cook until the bottoms are golden brown, 1 to 2 minutes. Add about 1/4 cup water to the pan, cover, and cook until the water evaporates and the dumplings are heated through, 3 to 4 minutes.

**STEP 4:** Spoon the warm dumplings over the tomato salad. Drizzle with the chile crisp vinaigrette, then top with the remaining basil and the crispy fried shallots, if using. Serve warm or at room temperature.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** Best eaten fresh; 1 day fridge for leftovers
- **Reheat:** Rewarm dumplings gently in a skillet with a splash of water, then reassemble over fresh tomatoes
- **Batch size:** Scale to 4 servings for a quick lunch or light dinner

---

## Notes

- **Health score: 7/10** - lots of fresh tomatoes and basil, with a moderate dumpling portion and a light chile-crisp dressing
- **Best texture contrast** - this works because the tomatoes stay juicy while the dumplings bring the hot, crispy-soft bite
- **Optional garnish** - fried shallots add crunch, but the salad is already solid without them
