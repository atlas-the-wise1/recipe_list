# Bricklayer-Style Nachos — Healthier Variant

> 💡 **Healthier Variant** of Bricklayer-Style Nachos — see `bricklayer-nachos.md` for the classic version.

**Category:** L — Lunch
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#heart-healthy`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 550kcal |
| Protein | 42g |
| Carbs | 40g |
| Fat | 26g |
| Fiber | 6g |
| Cholesterol | 85mg |
**Health Score:** 5/10

**Prep Time:** 20 min | **Cook Time:** 25 min | **Total:** ~45 min

---

**What changed vs. classic:** ~270 fewer calories, ~26g less fat, significantly less saturated fat per serving. Achieves this by:
- **Removing bacon entirely** (saves ~180 cal and ~8g sat fat across the dish)
- Replacing Mexican chorizo with **turkey chorizo** (same smoky-spiced flavor, fraction of the fat)
- Replacing beef sirloin with **extra-lean ground turkey (93/7)** or ground chicken
- Reducing cheese volume and using **lower-fat options** (part-skim mozzarella + light queso fresco)
- Using **baked tortilla chips** instead of fried (saves ~60 cal per serving)

---

## Ingredients

- 2 pounds ripe Roma tomatoes, OR 2 (15-oz) cans fire-roasted tomatoes
- 8 oz **turkey chorizo**, casing removed, coarsely chopped
- 1½ pounds **extra-lean ground turkey (93/7)** or ground chicken
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 1 medium white onion, halved and slivered (~1½ cups)
- 1–2 jalapeños or serrano chiles, halved, deseeded if desired, sliced
- 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1 tsp olive oil
- 1–1½ pounds **baked tortilla chips** (Baked! Tostitos or similar)
- 8 oz shredded **part-skim mozzarella** (~2 cups)
- 1 ripe avocado, halved, pitted, finely chopped
- ½ cup crumbled **light queso fresco** (reduced from 1 cup)
- 8 scallions, trimmed and thinly sliced

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** If using fresh tomatoes, broil on a foil-lined baking sheet 8–10 minutes, turning halfway, until charred. Once cool, chop without discarding juices.

**STEP 2:** Heat olive oil in a large (12-inch) skillet over medium-high. Add turkey chorizo and cook, breaking into smaller pieces, until browned and crisp — 3 to 4 minutes.

**STEP 3:** Add ground turkey, season with salt and pepper, cook stirring until browned — about 5 minutes. Add onion and jalapeño, cook until they begin to wilt — 2 to 3 minutes. Add garlic, stir until fragrant — less than 1 minute.

**STEP 4:** Stir in chopped tomatoes with their juices. Cook over high heat, stirring occasionally, until saucy — 2 to 3 minutes. Season to taste with salt.

**STEP 5:** Set rack in middle of oven and heat to 400°F. Spread baked tortilla chips on a half sheet pan. Cover with turkey-chorizo mixture and all its salsa. Cover with part-skim mozzarella. Bake until cheese is completely melted — 8 to 10 minutes.

**STEP 6:** Garnish with avocado, queso fresco, and scallions. Serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- **Still a crowd-pleaser** — turkey chorizo delivers surprisingly bold flavor; most people won't notice the swap
- **Avocado is intentional** — the heart-healthy fats from avocado replace some of the saturated fat lost from removing bacon and reducing cheese
- **Baked chips caveat:** They're lighter but softer — they'll go slightly soggier under the meat mixture. Serve quickly after baking.
- **Make-ahead:** Turkey-chorizo meat mixture can be made 1–2 days ahead and refrigerated. Reheat before assembling.
