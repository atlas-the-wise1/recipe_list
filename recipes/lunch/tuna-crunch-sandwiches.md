# Tuna Crunch Sandwiches

**Category:** Lunch
**Tags:** #high-protein #quick #sandwich #meal-prep
**Servings:** 2
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 0 min | **Total:** ~15 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~520 | ~29g | ~29g | ~33g | ~3g | ~175mg |
**Health Score:** 6/10

---

## Ingredients

- 1 (5-ounce) can solid white albacore or skipjack tuna, drained well
- 1 hard-boiled egg, peeled
- 1 celery rib, finely minced
- 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 tablespoons finely minced red onion, shallot, or scallions
- 2 tablespoons finely minced fresh parsley, chives, tarragon, or a mix (optional)
- 2 tablespoons mayonnaise, plus more for spreading
- 1 tablespoon sweet pickle relish or chopped dill pickles (optional)
- 2 teaspoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
- A few large handfuls of hearty potato chips or corn chips
- Salt and black pepper
- 4 slices thick, soft sandwich bread, such as Texas toast, toasted if desired
- 2 lettuce leaves, such as iceberg, romaine, green leaf, or Bibb

## Instructions

1. Open the can of tuna, leaving the lid in place after opening. Invert the can over the sink and press the lid into the tuna to squeeze out excess water or oil, being careful of sharp edges. Transfer the tuna to a medium bowl.
2. Wash your hands well, then squeeze the boiled egg through your fingers into the bowl with the tuna. Add the celery, olive oil, onion, herbs if using, mayonnaise, relish if using, and lemon juice. Use a fork to gently fold everything together, keeping the tuna in relatively large chunks.
3. Grab a few chips and crumble them into the bowl, then fold again to combine. Season with salt and pepper and adjust with more salt, pepper, lemon juice, olive oil, or mayonnaise if needed.
4. When ready to serve, spread each slice of bread generously with mayonnaise. Place lettuce on the bottom slices, top with half the tuna salad, then add a generous handful of chips. Close the sandwiches, press firmly so the chips break into the tuna salad and bread, then cut into triangles and serve with extra chips on the side.

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 2 days refrigerated without assembled chips; add chips just before serving  
**Reheat:** Not applicable  
**Batch size:** The tuna mixture doubles easily; keep the chips separate until serving  
**Notes:** This is a classic tuna sandwich with crunch built in from both the chips and the celery. The olive oil makes it richer than a standard tuna salad, but the lemon and herbs keep it bright.
