# Quinoa and Broccoli Spoon Salad

**Category:** L — Lunch
**Tags:** `#heart-healthy` `#low-cholesterol` `#meal-prep` `#high-protein` `#fat-loss`
**Estimated Calories:** ~480 per serving (serves 4)
**Protein:** ~16g per serving
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 15 min | **Total:** ~30 min

---

## Ingredients

- Kosher salt
- 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
- 1 lemon
- 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 3 tablespoons Dijon mustard
- 2 tablespoons honey
- 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 1 large bunch broccoli (about 1½ lbs)
- 1 medium tart and crisp apple
- 4 oz sharp Cheddar
- ¼ cup toasted pecans, roughly chopped
- ½ cup dried cranberries

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Bring a medium pot of water to a boil over high heat; season aggressively with salt. Add quinoa, reduce heat to maintain a simmer, and cook until plump and tender — about 15 minutes. Transfer to a fine-mesh sieve, rinse with cool water, and drain well.

**STEP 2:** While quinoa cooks, finely grate lemon zest into a large bowl, then cut lemon in half. Add olive oil, mustard, honey, apple cider vinegar, and juice of ½ lemon. Whisk together. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

**STEP 3:** Peel the broccoli stem and trim off the dry end. Finely chop the entire broccoli (florets and stem) and add to the dressing. Core the apple, then finely chop the apple and the cheese. Add both to the broccoli and toss to combine.

**STEP 4:** Add cooked quinoa, pecans, and cranberries. Toss to combine. Taste and adjust with more salt, pepper, and lemon juice as needed.

**Storage:** Refrigerate up to 3 days.

---

## Notes

- **True meal-prep salad** — dressing coats everything and it holds well; actually better on day 2 once flavors meld
- **"Spoon salad" = finely chopped** — everything is diced small so you get all components in every bite; don't skip this
- **Lighter swap:** Skip the Cheddar or use 2 oz instead of 4 to cut ~100 cal/serving; sub pepitas for pecans for a similar crunch with more protein
- **Cranberry note:** Adds natural sweetness but also sugar — sub chopped dried apricots or just reduce to ¼ cup if watching sugar closely
- **Apple choice:** Granny Smith or Honeycrisp — tartness balances the honey-mustard dressing
- **Complete protein:** Quinoa is a full protein source — this is one of the few vegetarian lunches that holds you through the afternoon
