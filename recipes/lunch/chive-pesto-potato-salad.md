# Chive Pesto Potato Salad

**Category:** Lunch
**Tags:** `#fat-loss`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~320 | ~8g | ~18g | ~34g | ~4g | ~12mg |
**Health Score:** 6/10
**Serves:** 6
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 15 min | **Total:** ~30 min

---

## Ingredients

- 2 pounds baby potatoes, halved
- Kosher salt
- Black pepper
- 8 ounces green beans or asparagus, cut into 1-inch pieces
- 1/4 cup pine nuts
- 2 garlic cloves, peeled
- 1 cup packed parsley leaves
- 1/3 cup extra-virgin olive oil
- 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan
- 1/2 cup chopped chives
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice

---

## Instructions

1. In a large pot, combine the potatoes with enough salted water to cover by 2 inches. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to a brisk simmer and cook until completely tender in the center, 8 to 12 minutes, adding the green beans or asparagus during the last 1 to 2 minutes. Drain and return to the hot pot for about 2 minutes so the vegetables dry out a bit.
2. Meanwhile, in a food processor, pulse the pine nuts and garlic until finely chopped. Add the parsley and pulse, scraping down the sides as needed, until well combined. With the machine running, drizzle in the olive oil and puree until smooth. Transfer to a large bowl and season with salt and pepper. Stir in the Parmesan, chives, and lemon juice.
3. Add the warm potato mixture to the pesto and season again with salt and pepper. Mix well. The salad can be made a few hours ahead and refrigerated; bring it back to room temperature and toss before serving.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 3 days fridge
- **Reheat:** Serve at room temperature or warm gently
- **Batch size:** Scale to 6 servings for a lunch side or easy meal-prep base

---

## Notes

- **Health score: 6/10** - vegetables and herbs help, but potatoes, olive oil, nuts, and Parmesan make this a substantial salad
