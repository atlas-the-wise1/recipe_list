# Vegan Caesar Salad With Crisp Chickpeas

**Category:** L — Lunch
**Tags:** `#heart-healthy` `#low-cholesterol` `#high-protein` `#fat-loss` `#vegan` `#meal-prep`
**Estimated Calories:** ~480 per serving (serves 4)
**Protein:** ~18g | **Fat:** ~26g | **Carbs:** ~48g | **Fiber:** ~9g
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

## Ingredients

### Salad
- ½ small loaf crusty bread (sourdough), torn into 1-inch pieces (~2 cups)
- ¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 1 (14-oz) can chickpeas, drained, rinsed, and thoroughly dried
- 1 large bunch lacinato kale, stems removed, roughly chopped into 1-inch pieces (~4 cups)
- 1 large head romaine lettuce, roughly chopped into 1-inch pieces (~6 cups)

### Dressing
- 1 cup whole unroasted cashews
- 1 snack-size (2×3-inch) sheet roasted nori, crumbled *(optional — adds ocean/umami depth)*
- ¼ cup nutritional yeast, plus more for serving
- ¾ cup fresh lemon juice
- 3 garlic cloves
- 2 teaspoons brine from a jar of capers
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
- 1 teaspoon white miso paste
- ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
- ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- ½ cup water

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Heat oven to 400°F. On a medium baking sheet, toss bread with 2 tablespoons olive oil; season generously with salt and pepper. On a second baking sheet, toss chickpeas with remaining 2 tablespoons oil; season generously with salt and pepper. Bake both until browned and crisp, stirring halfway through — about 10 minutes for croutons, 20 minutes for chickpeas. Set aside.

**STEP 2:** While chickpeas and croutons roast, make the dressing: Add all dressing ingredients plus ¾ teaspoon kosher salt, ½ teaspoon pepper, and ½ cup water to a high-speed blender. Blend until completely smooth — 3 to 4 minutes, scraping down the sides with a spatula as needed. You should have about 1½ cups dressing.

**STEP 3:** In a large bowl, add kale and dressing. Massage kale with your hands until slightly softened. Add lettuce, croutons, and half the roasted chickpeas; toss to coat. Season to taste with salt and pepper. Sprinkle with additional nutritional yeast. Top with remaining roasted chickpeas, grind black pepper over, and serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- **Cashew dressing is the star** — blends into a creamy, rich Caesar without any dairy or anchovy; nori adds the oceanic umami that makes it taste "fishy" in a good way
- **Dry your chickpeas well** — pat them thoroughly before roasting; moisture = steamed, not crispy
- **Gluten-free:** Skip the croutons and add extra chickpeas or toasted pepitas
- **Dressing makes ~1½ cups** — use what you need, store the rest refrigerated up to 5 days; great on grain bowls or as a dip
- **Kale massage is non-negotiable** — raw lacinato kale is tough; massaging the dressing in breaks it down and makes it tender
- **High-speed blender required** for a smooth cashew dressing (Vitamix, Ninja, etc.) — a standard blender will leave it grainy
