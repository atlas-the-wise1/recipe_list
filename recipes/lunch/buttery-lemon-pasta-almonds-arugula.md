# Buttery Lemon Pasta With Almonds and Arugula

**Prep Time:** 5 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~25 min

---

## Ingredients

- Fine salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 pound Banza chickpea linguine or spaghetti
- 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 1 cup sliced almonds
- 2 fresh rosemary sprigs
- ½ teaspoon red-pepper flakes, plus more to taste
- ¼ cup freshly squeezed lemon juice, plus more to taste
- 1 tablespoon finely grated lemon zest
- 4–5 oz baby or wild arugula, coarsely chopped (or baby kale or spinach) — about 4–5 cups
- Grated Parmesan, for serving

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a boil. Add pasta and cook until 1 minute shy of al dente (usually 1–2 minutes less than package instructions). Scoop out about 1½ cups pasta water, then drain pasta.

**STEP 2:** While pasta cooks, melt butter in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat. Cook, swirling occasionally, until foam subsides, milk solids turn golden-brown, and butter smells rich and toasty — 3 to 4 minutes. Watch carefully so it doesn't burn.

**STEP 3:** Stir in almonds, rosemary, and red-pepper flakes. Continue stirring occasionally until nuts are toasted and slightly darkened — about 1 minute.

**STEP 4:** Add about 1 cup pasta water to the skillet and bring to a simmer. Add lemon juice, zest, ½ teaspoon salt, and a large pinch of black pepper. Add drained pasta and toss well. Add arugula, tossing until it wilts. Simmer another minute if needed to thicken sauce until thick and glossy. If mixture seems dry, add more pasta water 1 tablespoon at a time.

**STEP 5:** Taste and adjust — more salt, red-pepper flakes, and lemon juice as needed. Serve topped with grated Parmesan and more red-pepper flakes if desired.

---

## Notes

- **Quick weeknight win** — 25 minutes start to finish, one skillet after pasta
- **Brown butter is the key** — don't rush step 2; that nutty, toasty butter is what makes the dish
- **Higher in sat fat** — 6 tbsp butter = ~14g saturated fat per serving; not #heart-healthy as written (see Healthier Variant)
- **Lighter swap:** Sub 3 tablespoons olive oil for the butter to cut saturated fat and drop ~100 cal/serving; loses some richness but still great
- **Boost protein:** Add grilled chicken, shrimp, or a soft-boiled egg per bowl; or stir in ½ cup white beans with the pasta water
- **Not keto** — pasta-based; could swap for zucchini noodles or hearts of palm pasta for a low-carb version
- **Greens flexibility:** Baby kale holds up slightly better if making ahead; arugula is peppery and bright; spinach is mildest
- **Make-ahead:** Not great for leftovers — pasta absorbs sauce quickly; best served immediately
