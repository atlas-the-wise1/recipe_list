# Goat Cheese and Dill Dutch Baby

**Category:** B — Breakfast
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#brunch` `#vegetarian`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~370 | ~16g | ~31g | ~18g | ~1g | ~180mg |
**Health Score:** 4/10

**Servings:** 6
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 22 min | **Total:** ~37 min

---

## Ingredients

- 1 cup / 148g crumbled plain goat cheese
- 1/2 cup chopped fresh dill, plus picked fronds for garnish
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 lemon
- Coarse kosher salt, such as Morton
- Ground black pepper
- 1 cup / 135g all-purpose flour
- 8 large eggs
- 3/4 cup / 190ml whole milk
- 4 tablespoons / 57g unsalted butter
- 2 cups watercress leaves with tender stems, or other tender greens such as spinach or arugula
- 1 tablespoon honey

---

## Instructions

1. In a small bowl, combine the goat cheese, 1/4 cup dill, and 1 tablespoon olive oil. Zest the lemon over the mixture, season with a pinch each of salt and pepper, and let it sit to marinate.
2. Heat oven to 425 F with a rack in the lower third. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and 1/4 teaspoon black pepper. Add the remaining 1/4 cup dill and stir. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk, then whisk the wet ingredients into the dry until just combined.
3. Melt the butter in a heavy 12-inch ovenproof skillet over medium-high heat. Let it cook until nutty and browned, about 5 minutes, swirling the pan so the butter coats the bottom and sides.
4. Pour the batter into the hot buttered skillet and spoon half of the marinated goat cheese into the center of the batter. Bake until puffed and golden, 20 to 22 minutes.
5. Cool slightly in the pan, then top with the watercress and dill fronds. Spoon the remaining goat cheese on top, drizzle with honey and the remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil, squeeze over lemon juice, and serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- Strong brunch-style breakfast with good protein, but it is still rich from eggs, butter, flour, and goat cheese.
- The greens help the balance, but this is better as an occasional wedding-diet breakfast than an everyday cut.
- If you want a lighter version later, I would reduce the butter first and then trim the cheese.
