# Gyeran Bap (Egg Rice)

**Category:** B — Breakfast
**Tags:** `#quick` `#vegetarian`
**Nutrition (per serving):**

**Serves:** 1
**Prep Time:** 5 min | **Cook Time:** 5 min | **Total:** ~10 min

## Ingredients

- 1/2 tablespoon unsalted butter
- 2 large eggs
- 1 teaspoon soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil
- 1 cup steamed white rice, preferably short- or medium-grain
- 1 (5-gram) packet roasted, salted seaweed, such as gim, optional

## Instructions

1. Melt the butter in a small nonstick skillet over medium heat. Continue cooking, stirring occasionally with a rubber spatula, until the melted butter starts to darken from yellow to light brown, 1 to 1 1/2 minutes.
2. Crack in the eggs and drizzle the soy sauce and sesame oil on top. Cook until the whites puff up slightly around the edges and the translucent parts around the yolks turn opaque, 2 to 2 1/2 minutes. Watch that the soy sauce doesn’t burn; remove the pan from the heat if needed.
3. Scoop the rice into a medium bowl and top with the fried eggs, including all of the buttery soy sauce drippings from the pan. Crush the seaweed over the eggs, pile it high, then mix everything together with a spoon before eating.

## Notes

- The browned butter gives the rice a nutty base before the soy and sesame finish it
- Gim is optional, but it adds the right salty, savory crunch
- Best eaten right away while the eggs are still hot and the rice can absorb the sauce
