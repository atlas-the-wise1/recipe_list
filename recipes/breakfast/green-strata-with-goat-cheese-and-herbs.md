# Green Strata With Goat Cheese and Herbs

**Category:** B — Breakfast
**Tags:** `#vegetarian` `#brunch` `#meal-prep`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~560 | ~29g | ~32g | ~34g | ~2g | ~400mg |
**Health Score:** 5/10
**Servings:** 6
**Prep Time:** 20 min | **Cook Time:** 45-50 min + at least 4 hr chill | **Total:** ~5 hr 10 min

---

## Ingredients

- 2 cups whole milk
- 1/2 cup heavy cream
- 5 ounces baby braising greens, such as kale, mustard greens, chard, or a mix, about 5 cups
- 3/4 cup mixed soft herbs, such as parsley, cilantro, dill, mint, or chives
- 1/4 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more as needed
- Black pepper, as needed
- 12 large eggs
- 1 pound day-old brioche or white bread, cut into 2-inch cubes, about 6 cups
- 6 ounces cold goat cheese, sliced into 1/4-inch-thick rounds
- Aleppo or Turkish pepper, for serving, optional

---

## Instructions

1. In a medium pot, bring the milk and cream to a simmer.
2. Meanwhile, place the greens, herbs, Parmigiano-Reggiano, 1 teaspoon salt, and black pepper in a food processor fitted with the blade attachment. Once the hot milk mixture comes to a simmer, pour it over the greens and puree until smooth. Pour into a bowl and let cool completely, then whisk in 6 eggs.
3. Lightly oil a 9-by-13-inch baking pan. Scatter the bread cubes in the bottom of the pan. Pour the custard over the bread and press down so the bread absorbs the mixture. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours. If you can, stir the bread cubes after about an hour to encourage even distribution of the custard.
4. When ready to bake, heat the oven to 350 F. Tuck the goat cheese rounds into and on top of the strata. Transfer the pan to the oven and bake until the top is beginning to firm up but is still slightly wet underneath, about 25 minutes.
5. Remove the pan from the oven and use a spoon to make 6 evenly spaced indentations on the surface. Crack an egg into each hole and season with salt and pepper. Return the pan to the oven and continue baking until the strata is cooked through and the eggs are just set, 20 to 25 minutes more. Sprinkle with Aleppo or Turkish pepper if desired.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 3 days fridge
- **Reheat:** Warm covered in a 300 F oven until heated through, or reheat individual slices gently in the microwave
- **Batch size:** Best scaled to 6 servings for a brunch or breakfast prep block

---

## Notes

- This is a strong make-ahead breakfast: the greens and herbs keep it feeling fresher than a standard bread-and-egg bake.
- The score lands in the middle because the dish has real vegetable volume, but it is still rich from cream, 12 eggs, bread, and goat cheese.
- If you want a lighter version later, trim the cream first and then reduce the goat cheese.
