## Ingredients

- 5 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 pound ground chicken
- Salt and black pepper
- 4 garlic cloves, roughly chopped
- 1 1/2 teaspoons ground coriander
- 1/2 teaspoon celery seeds (optional)
- 2 celery stalks, halved lengthwise, then sliced 1/2-inch thick
- 1 large carrot, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch cubes
- 1 large shallot, finely chopped
- 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
- 7 to 8 cups chicken stock, plus more as needed
- 8 ounces dried egg noodles, cavatelli or other small shaped pasta
- Creme fraiche, chopped fresh parsley and dill, and lemon zest, for garnish

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** In a large pot, heat 3 tablespoons oil over medium-high. Add the chicken, season generously with salt and pepper, and cook, breaking up with a wooden spoon, until starting to crumble, about 3 minutes. Add the garlic, coriander and celery seeds (if using), and cook, stirring frequently, until chicken is cooked through, about 2 minutes. Using a slotted spoon, scoop the chicken into a small lidded bowl; cover and set aside.

**STEP 2:** Add the celery, carrot, shallot, thyme and the remaining 2 tablespoons oil to the pot, season with salt and pepper, and cook, stirring, until slightly softened, about 3 minutes. Add the stock, bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer.

**STEP 3:** Add noodles and cook over medium-high until al dente according to package directions. If the noodles absorb a lot of liquid, add more broth according to taste. Add the reserved chicken and any accumulated juices and heat until warmed. Divide among bowls and top with creme fraiche, fresh herbs and lemon zest.

---

## Notes

- Best eaten hot and fresh
- Add more stock if the noodles drink up the broth
- The garnishes make it feel bright instead of heavy
