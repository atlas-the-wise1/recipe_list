# Miso-Butter Roasted Broccoli

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#vegetarian` `#vegetable-forward`
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 15 min | **Total:** ~25 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~130 | ~4g | ~11g | ~8g | ~3g | ~5mg |
**Health Score:** 8/10

---

## Ingredients

- 1 large head broccoli (about 1 to 1 1/4 pounds), florets and tender stems cut into bite-size pieces (6 to 8 cups)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- Kosher salt
- 1 tablespoon unsalted butter or ghee, at room temperature
- 2 teaspoons white miso
- 1 lime, halved

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Heat the oven to 425°F.

**STEP 2:** Add the broccoli to a large sheet pan, along with any tiny stray pieces from the cutting board. Drizzle with the olive oil, season with 1/4 teaspoon salt, and toss to coat. Spread out the broccoli and roast until the underside is charred and the thickest stalks are easily pierced with a fork, about 15 minutes.

**STEP 3:** Meanwhile, in a small bowl, use a fork to mash the butter and miso together. Add the mixture to the roasted broccoli, then squeeze half the lime juice over it and stir it all together, scraping up any brown bits on the bottom of the sheet pan. Season as needed with salt and the remaining lime juice. Serve warm or at room temperature.

---

## Notes

- **Sheet-pan browning matters** — spread the broccoli out so it chars instead of steaming
- **Miso-butter goes on after roasting** so the miso stays savory and glossy instead of scorching
- **Lime at the end** brightens the whole dish and keeps the butter from feeling heavy
- **Good side dish energy** — rich enough to feel special, but still very vegetable-forward
