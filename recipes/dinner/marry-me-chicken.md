# Marry Me Chicken

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#low-cholesterol`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~280 | ~30g | ~11g | ~12g | ~2g | ~85mg |
**Health Score:** 7/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~30 min

---

## Ingredients

- 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (6 oz each)
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- ½ cup sun-dried tomatoes (oil-packed), chopped
- ½ cup chicken broth
- ½ cup heavy cream
- 1 tsp Italian seasoning
- 1 cup fresh spinach, roughly chopped
- ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese
- 1 tbsp butter
- ½ tsp red pepper flakes (optional)

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Season chicken breasts generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
**STEP 2:** Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear chicken for 4–5 minutes per side until golden brown. Transfer to a plate.
**STEP 3:** In the same skillet, add minced garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant, stirring constantly.
**STEP 4:** Add sun-dried tomatoes, chicken broth, Italian seasoning, and red pepper flakes. Stir well.
**STEP 5:** Return chicken to the skillet, nestling it into the sauce. Reduce heat to medium and simmer for 8–10 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F internally.
**STEP 6:** Stir in heavy cream, spinach, and Parmesan cheese. Simmer for 2–3 minutes until spinach wilts and sauce is creamy.
**STEP 7:** Swirl in butter at the end for richness. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
**STEP 8:** Serve immediately with the creamy sauce spooned over the chicken.

---

## Notes

- **Keto-friendly:** Serve over cauliflower rice, zucchini noodles, or with roasted vegetables to keep carbs minimal.
- **Cream stability:** Use full-fat heavy cream (not light cream) to prevent curdling when simmered. Add it at the end, off heat, for extra insurance.
- **Make ahead:** Prepare the sauce and chicken up to 2 days in advance; store separately and combine gently when reheating.
- **Sun-dried tomato swap:** If unavailable, use 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes plus 1 tsp tomato paste for similar depth.
- **Spinach freshness:** Add spinach at the very end to preserve color and texture; overcooked spinach turns dark and mushy.
- **Parmesan quality matters:** Freshly grated Parm melts better than pre-shredded; it also prevents the sauce from becoming grainy.
- **Timing precision:** Don't overcook chicken—an instant-read thermometer at 165°F is your friend; carryover heat will push it slightly higher as it rests.
