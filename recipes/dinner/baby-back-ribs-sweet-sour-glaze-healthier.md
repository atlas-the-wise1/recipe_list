# Baby Back Ribs With Sweet and Sour Glaze — Healthier Variant

> 💡 **Healthier Variant** of Baby Back Ribs With Sweet and Sour Glaze — see `baby-back-ribs-sweet-sour-glaze.md` for the classic version.

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #high-protein #meal-prep #heart-healthy
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 2.5 hrs | **Total:** ~2 hrs 45 min

---

|---|---|---|---|---|---|

**Health Score:** 7/10

**What changed vs. classic:** ~200 fewer calories, ~22g less fat, ~55mg less cholesterol per serving. Achieves this by:
- Switching to **St. Louis-cut spare ribs** (meatier, less fat marbling than baby backs) trimmed of the fat cap
- **Removing the membrane** (non-optional here — reduces rendered fat significantly)
- **Draining rendered fat** from the foil packet before broiling
- **Halving the glaze sugar** (less jelly, add orange zest for brightness)
- Serving with a fresh slaw instead of eating plain — adds fiber and volume

---

## Ingredients

### Ribs
- 1 rack St. Louis-cut spare ribs (~3 lbs), fat cap trimmed, membrane removed
- 1½ tsp coarse kosher salt
- 1 tsp onion powder
- ½ tsp smoked paprika
- ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper

### Lighter Glaze
- 4 garlic cloves, crushed
- 2 tbsp Concord grape jelly (half the original)
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp soy sauce (low-sodium)
- 1 tsp orange zest
- ½ tsp Dijon mustard

### Quick Apple-Cabbage Slaw (Serving Side)
- 2 cups shredded green cabbage
- 1 medium apple, julienned or grated
- 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
- 1 tsp olive oil
- Pinch of salt and black pepper

---

## Instructions

1. Position an oven rack 6 inches from the broiler and another on the bottom of the oven. Heat oven to 325°F.
2. **Mandatory:** Remove the membrane from the bony underside of the ribs — use a small knife to release one corner, pull off with your hands and discard.
3. In a small bowl, stir together salt, onion powder, smoked paprika, and black pepper. Season ribs on both sides.
4. Wrap ribs tightly in aluminum foil, place on a sheet pan flesh-side down, and bake on the bottom rack until meat is tender and pulls away easily from bones, **2 to 2½ hours**.
5. Remove ribs from oven. Carefully open the foil — **drain off and discard the rendered fat** before proceeding. Heat the broiler.
6. Transfer ribs flesh-side up to the sheet pan. Tip any remaining juices (post-drain) into a skillet. Add garlic, jelly, vinegar, soy sauce, orange zest, and Dijon. Bring to a simmer over medium-high. Cook whisking until syrupy and reduced by half, **4–5 minutes**.
7. Brush or spoon half the glaze over the ribs. Broil until charred in spots, **1–3 minutes**. Watch closely.
8. Transfer ribs to cutting board, spoon remaining glaze over top. Cut between bones and serve.
9. **Slaw:** Toss cabbage, apple, vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper together. Serve alongside.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 4 days fridge / 3 months freezer
**Reheat:** Wrapped in foil at 300°F for 15 min, or microwave covered
**Batch size:** Double the rack for 8 servings — glaze scales easily
**Notes:** The fat drain in step 5 is the biggest lever here — you can see 2–4 tbsp of rendered fat come out of the packet. Discarding it shaves significant calories without changing the rib texture. The slaw adds fiber and a refreshing contrast to the rich glaze.
