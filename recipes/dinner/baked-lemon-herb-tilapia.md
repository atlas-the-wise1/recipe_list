# Baked Lemon Herb Tilapia

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#keto` `#heart-healthy` `#low-cholesterol` `#high-protein` `#fat-loss`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~244 | ~35g | ~10g | ~4g | ~1g | ~85mg |
**Health Score:** 9/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 14 min | **Total:** ~24 min

---

## Ingredients

- 4 tilapia fillets (5–6 oz each)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Zest of 2 lemons
- 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- 1 tbsp capers (drained and rinsed)
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- ½ tsp dried thyme
- ¼ tsp dried oregano
- Salt and white pepper to taste
- Lemon slices for garnish
- Fresh dill or additional parsley for serving

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or lightly coat with cooking spray.
**STEP 2:** Pat tilapia fillets dry with paper towels. Arrange them on the prepared baking sheet without overlapping.
**STEP 3:** In a small bowl, combine olive oil, lemon zest, lemon juice, minced garlic, parsley, capes, Dijon mustard, thyme, and oregano. Whisk until combined.
**STEP 4:** Brush this mixture evenly over each tilapia fillet, making sure to coat both sides and the edges.
**STEP 5:** Season lightly with salt and white pepper.
**STEP 6:** Lay 2–3 lemon slices on top of each fillet for visual appeal and additional citrus flavor.
**STEP 7:** Bake for 12–15 minutes until fish flakes easily with a fork and is just opaque throughout (don't overcook or tilapia will dry out).
**STEP 8:** Remove from oven. Garnish with fresh dill or additional chopped parsley.
**STEP 9:** Serve immediately with extra lemon wedges on the side.

---

## Notes

- **Tilapia texture:** Tilapia is a delicate, mild white fish. It cooks quickly—watch it carefully to avoid overcooking. A fillet is done when it flakes easily but is still moist in the center.
- **Parchment power:** Baking on parchment paper ensures easy cleanup and helps the fish steam slightly while baking, keeping it tender and moist.
- **Flavor add-ins:** This recipe is a canvas—feel free to add sun-dried tomatoes, roasted red peppers, or even caramelized shallots under the herb topping.
- **Keto superstar:** At just 220 calories and 3g carbs per serving, this meal is perfect for keto eating. Serve with roasted cauliflower or zucchini for a complete meal.
- **Meal-prep friendly:** Prepare fillets with topping up to 8 hours ahead (cover loosely with plastic wrap). Bake fresh just before serving.
- **Mustard magic:** Dijon mustard adds subtle tang and richness while helping the herb mixture adhere to the delicate fish.
- **Cook time varies:** Thicker fillets (¾+ inch) may need an extra 2–3 minutes. Thinner fillets (½ inch) may be done in 10–12 minutes. Use an instant-read thermometer—fish is done at 145°F.
