# Soor iyo Dalac Bilaash (Grits and Tomato Stew)

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #vegetarian #gluten-free #comfort-food #one-pot
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 40 min | **Total:** ~50 min

## Ingredients

### For the Soor

- 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
- 1 cup fine white cornmeal

### For the Dalac Bilaash

- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 medium red onion, sliced
- 5 Roma tomatoes, roughly chopped
- 1 small jalapeño, optional, destemmed and halved
- 4 to 5 garlic cloves, peeled
- 1 handful cilantro leaves, roughly chopped
- 2 1/2 teaspoons all-purpose seasoning, preferably Vegeta
- 1/2 small green bell pepper, halved and sliced

## Instructions

1. Make the soor: In a medium saucepan with a lid, add 3 cups cold water and the salt. Turn heat to medium-high and whisk in the cornmeal slowly to reduce lumps. Cook, whisking constantly, until the mixture thickens, about **8 minutes**. Cover, reduce heat to medium-low, and cook for **25 more minutes**, stirring occasionally, until thick. If it sticks to the bottom, stir in **1/4 cup water**. Set aside, covered.
2. Prepare the dalac bilaash: While the soor cooks, warm the olive oil in a medium pot or skillet over medium-high heat. When hot, stir in the onion and cook until translucent, about **5 minutes**.
3. Add the tomatoes and jalapeño, if using, then cover and cook, occasionally mashing the tomatoes as they break down, about **10 minutes**. Meanwhile, smash the garlic in a mortar and pestle. Add the cilantro and pound until it becomes a chunky paste.
4. Once the tomatoes have broken down completely, add the cilantro paste, all-purpose seasoning, and green bell pepper. Stir to combine, cover, and cook over medium heat until the peppers are soft but not mushy, about **15 minutes**.
5. Divide the soor among plates and top with the dalac bilaash.

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 3 days refrigerated  
**Reheat:** Warm the soor with a splash of water or milk on the stovetop; rewarm the stew separately over low heat  
**Batch size:** Doubles well for a larger pot, but the cornmeal thickens fast, so keep extra water nearby  
**Notes:** This is a simple, satisfying cornmeal-and-tomato bowl with a bright savory topping. Vegeta gives it a deep, seasoned backbone, but you can substitute a mix of salt, black pepper, a pinch of sugar, and a little nutmeg if needed.
