# Sheet-Pan Gnocchi With Asparagus, Leeks and Peas

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#heart-healthy` `#high-protein` `#vegetarian`
**Nutrition (per serving, with white beans + egg):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~620 | ~28g | ~26g | ~80g | ~10g | ~215mg |
**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 25 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

**Health Score:** 6/10

## Ingredients

- 2 (12-oz) packages gnocchi (preferably fresh, refrigerated, precooked)
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 1 bunch asparagus (about 1 lb), trimmed and cut into thirds
- 2 small leeks, trimmed, halved lengthwise, then sliced ½-inch thick (~2 cups)
- 2 shallots, trimmed and sliced lengthwise ¼-inch thick
- 1 cup frozen peas, thawed (or fresh)
- 1 (15-oz) can white beans (cannellini), drained and rinsed
- 1 lemon, scrubbed
- ½ cup grated Parmesan
- ¼ cup sliced fresh chives or parsley *(optional)*
- 4 soft-boiled eggs, halved *(optional but recommended for protein)*

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Heat oven to 425°F. On a large rimmed baking sheet, toss gnocchi with 2 tablespoons oil, 1 tablespoon butter, and ½ teaspoon salt.

**STEP 2:** On a second rimmed baking sheet, toss asparagus, leeks, and shallots with remaining 1 tablespoon oil and 1 tablespoon butter. Season with ½ teaspoon salt and several generous grinds of pepper.

**STEP 3:** Put both pans in the oven — gnocchi on the bottom rack, vegetables above. Stir both after 5 minutes to distribute the butter. Stir vegetables once or twice more, but leave gnocchi undisturbed. Roast until vegetables are golden and crisping at the edges (~15 minutes total) and gnocchi are golden brown on one side (20–25 minutes total). Stir peas and white beans into vegetables in the last 5 minutes of cooking.

**STEP 4:** Combine gnocchi and vegetables on one tray. Grate lemon zest over the top. Sprinkle with half the Parmesan and chives (if using); save the rest for serving. Season with salt and pepper. Toss, transfer to a serving bowl or platter. Finish with remaining Parmesan and chives. Top each portion with a halved soft-boiled egg if using. Serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- **Fresh/refrigerated gnocchi is essential** — shelf-stable shelf gnocchi won't crisp the same way; look for it in the refrigerated pasta section
- **Leave gnocchi undisturbed after the first stir** — one flat side pressed against the hot pan = golden crust; flipping it around = no crust
- **Two pans, two racks** — don't try to cram everything onto one sheet; crowding kills crispiness on both the gnocchi and vegetables
- **Peas go in last 5 minutes** — they just need to warm through; adding earlier makes them mushy
- **Lemon zest while hot** — grate directly over the warm tray so the oils bloom into everything
- **Protein additions baked in:** White beans added to the vegetable tray in the last 5 minutes (+7g protein, +4g fiber per serving); soft-boiled egg on top adds another +6g protein
- **Spring-forward dish** — asparagus + leeks + peas is a classic spring combination; sub broccolini and mushrooms in fall/winter
- **Not a meal-prep dish** — gnocchi loses its crispiness quickly; best eaten immediately after cooking
