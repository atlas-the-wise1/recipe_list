# Iwuk Edesi (One-Pot Rice With Chicken)

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #high-protein #meal-prep
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 20 min | **Cook Time:** 45 min | **Total:** ~65 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~680 | ~52g | ~30g | ~48g | ~3g | ~145mg |
**Health Score:** 7/10

---

## Ingredients

### Pepper Paste
- 1–2 yellow or red Scotch bonnet chiles or red habanero chiles
- 1 tbsp whole crayfish or dried shrimp, soaked in warm water 10 min until plump then drained (or ½ tbsp ground crayfish)
- ¼ tsp fresh or dried irú, soaked in warm water 10 min and drained (or substitute fish sauce or fermented black beans)

### Chicken and Rice
- ¼ cup neutral oil (canola or grapeseed)
- 3 lbs bone-in, skin-on chicken parts
- Fine salt, to taste
- 1 tbsp grated ginger
- 2 garlic cloves, smashed
- ½ cup red palm oil
- 2 cups long-grain white rice
- 2¼ cups chicken stock
- 4 cups torn hearty greens — ugwu, mature spinach, collards, or kale
- ½ small red onion, thinly sliced

---

## Instructions

1. **Make the pepper paste:** Using a mortar and pestle (or food processor), pound Scotch bonnet chiles and crayfish to a coarse paste. Add irú and pound until smooth. *(Pepper paste keeps refrigerated 1 week or frozen 1 month.)*
2. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat neutral oil over medium until shimmering, **1–2 minutes**. Pat chicken dry and season generously with salt on both sides. Working in batches if needed, sear chicken skin-side down until deep golden brown on both sides, **~12 minutes total**. Chicken will not be cooked through. Transfer to a plate.
3. Drain all but 2 tbsp oil from the pot. Add ginger and garlic; stir until fragrant. Stir in palm oil and pepper paste, then add rice. Stir to coat every grain in the seasoned oil. Add stock and scrape the bottom to loosen any stuck bits. Season with salt and bring to a simmer.
4. Return chicken to the pot skin-side up along with any resting juices. Reduce heat to low. Cover and cook until liquid is absorbed, rice is tender, and chicken is cooked through, **18–20 minutes**. Stir in greens and allow to wilt, **~2 minutes**. Remove from heat and let sit covered **10 more minutes**. Fluff rice gently with a fork.
5. Divide rice and chicken among plates, sprinkle with sliced red onion, and serve warm.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 4 days fridge / 2 months freezer
**Reheat:** Stovetop over medium-low with a splash of stock or water. Microwave covered with a damp paper towel.
**Batch size:** Doubles well in a larger Dutch oven — make a full 6 lb batch for the week
**Specialty ingredient notes:**
- **Crayfish/dried shrimp:** Find at African, Asian, or Caribbean grocery stores. Ground crayfish is easiest.
- **Irú (locust beans):** West African fermented seasoning — fish sauce or fermented black beans are solid substitutes
- **Red palm oil:** African grocery stores or online. Has a distinct flavor and color — do not substitute with regular palm oil
- **Scotch bonnets/habaneros:** Very hot — use 1 chile for moderate heat, 2 for bold
**Notes:** A deeply flavorful West African classic. Red palm oil is rich in vitamin E and beta-carotene — nutritionally distinct from other oils. Higher in fat due to palm oil and skin-on chicken — remove skin before eating to reduce fat and cholesterol if desired. The pepper paste is the soul of the dish — worth making a double batch and freezing. See `iwuk-edesi-one-pot-rice-chicken-healthier.md` for a skinless version with reduced palm oil that earns #low-cholesterol.
