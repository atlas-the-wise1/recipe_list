# Chicken Teriyaki Bowl

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#meal-prep` `#heart-healthy` `#low-cholesterol`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~480 | ~38g | ~14g | ~48g | ~6g | ~95mg |

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 25 min | **Total:** ~40 min

---

## Ingredients

**For the chicken:**
- 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**For the teriyaki sauce:**
- ¼ cup low-sodium soy sauce
- 2 tbsp honey or maple syrup
- 2 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- ½ tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
- 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 1 tbsp water

**For serving:**
- 2 cups cooked brown rice
- 3 cups steamed broccoli florets
- 1 tbsp sesame seeds
- 2 scallions, sliced

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Season chicken with salt and pepper.
**STEP 2:** Cook chicken until golden brown and cooked through, about 10 minutes. Transfer to a plate.
**STEP 3:** In the same skillet, combine soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, sesame oil, ginger, garlic, and red pepper flakes. Bring to a simmer.
**STEP 4:** Add cornstarch slurry and stir constantly for 1–2 minutes until sauce thickens and becomes glossy.
**STEP 5:** Return chicken to the skillet and toss until well coated. Simmer for 3–4 minutes to allow flavors to meld.
**STEP 6:** Divide brown rice and broccoli among four bowls. Top with teriyaki chicken and sauce.
**STEP 7:** Garnish with sesame seeds and fresh scallions before serving.

---

## Notes

- **Make-ahead friendly:** Prepare teriyaki chicken up to 3 days in advance; store in an airtight container. Reheat gently and serve over fresh or reheated rice.
- **Sauce depth:** Use aged soy sauce (tamari) for deeper umami and a slightly lower sodium option.
- **Rice alternative:** Cauliflower rice reduces carbs to ~20g per serving while keeping protein and satiety high.
- **Protein preference:** Swap chicken thighs for breast meat if leaner protein is preferred (reduce cooking time to 8 minutes).
- **Batch cooking:** This recipe scales beautifully—triple the batch for four days of lunches.
- **Garnish matters:** Sesame seeds and scallions add texture and freshness; don't skip them.
- **Temperature tip:** Don't boil the sauce after adding the cornstarch slurry; gentle simmering prevents it from breaking down.
