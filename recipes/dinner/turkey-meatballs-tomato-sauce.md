# Turkey Meatballs in Tomato Sauce

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#heart-healthy` `#low-cholesterol` `#meal-prep`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~380 | ~38g | ~18g | ~16g | ~2g | ~125mg |
**Serves:** 4 (28 meatballs)
**Prep Time:** 20 min + optional 1h chill | **Cook Time:** 30 min | **Total:** ~50 min

---

## Ingredients

- ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese, plus more for serving
- ½ cup panko or other plain dried bread crumbs
- ¼ cup minced onion
- ¼ cup chopped chives or basil
- 2 garlic cloves, grated or minced
- 1½ teaspoons kosher salt
- ½ teaspoon black pepper
- 1½ teaspoons dried oregano
- Pinch red-pepper flakes *(optional)*
- 1½ pounds ground turkey, very cold
- 1 large egg, beaten
- 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, plus more as needed
- 3 cups marinara sauce, plus more to taste

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** In a large bowl, combine Parmesan, bread crumbs, onion, chives, garlic, salt, pepper, oregano, and red-pepper flakes (if using). Mix well. Add turkey and egg; blend with your hands until well mixed. If time allows, cover and chill for 1 hour or up to 24 hours — cold mixture is much easier to roll. Form into 28 meatballs, each about 1¼ inches in diameter.

**STEP 2:** Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a large sauté pan over medium-high. When hot, add enough meatballs to fit in one layer without crowding. Brown on all sides — 5 to 8 minutes. Transfer to a plate. Add another tablespoon of oil and brown the next batch. Repeat until all meatballs are browned, adding more oil as needed.

**STEP 3:** Add marinara sauce to the pan and bring to a simmer, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Return meatballs and their juices to the pan. Shake pan to coat meatballs with sauce. Lower heat, partly cover, and simmer until cooked through — 15 to 20 minutes.

**STEP 4:** Serve hot, drizzled with more olive oil and sprinkled with Parmesan if desired.

---

## Notes

- **Different from the roasted turkey meatballs in the repo** — this version is pan-seared and simmered in marinara; the other is oven-roasted with mozzarella. Both are worth keeping.
- **Cold turkey = better meatballs** — don't skip the chill; warm ground turkey is sticky and harder to roll into tight balls
- **Don't crowd the pan** — brown in batches; crowding steams instead of sears and you lose the crust
- **Meal-prep powerhouse:** Meatballs + sauce keep 5 days refrigerated or freeze 3 months; reheat gently in sauce
- **Serve over:** Banza chickpea spaghetti, zucchini noodles, polenta, or with crusty bread
- **Lighten it up:** Use ground turkey breast (99% lean) instead of regular ground turkey to drop ~40 cal and ~5g fat per serving
- **Marinara shortcut:** Store-bought is fine — Rao's is the gold standard for a jarred option
