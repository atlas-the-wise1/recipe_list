# Spaghetti with Meat Sauce

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #high-protein #meal-prep
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 25 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~575 | ~46g | ~12g | ~62g | ~8g | ~85mg |
**Health Score:** 8/10

> **With ground beef:** ~680 cal | ~40g protein | ~72g carbs | ~22g fat | ~110mg cholesterol
> **With ground chicken:** ~540 cal | ~44g protein | ~72g carbs | ~8g fat | ~75mg cholesterol

---

## Ingredients

- 1 lb ground beef, turkey, or chicken
- 1 onion, chopped
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 jar (24 oz) tomato or marinara sauce
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1 lb Banza chickpea spaghetti
- Grated Parmesan, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. Cook Banza chickpea spaghetti in salted boiling water per package directions. Drain and set aside.
2. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium. Add onion and cook until soft, **~5 minutes**.
3. Add ground meat and cook, breaking it up, until fully browned, **~7 minutes**. Drain excess fat if using beef.
4. Stir in tomato sauce, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer over medium-low, **10–15 minutes**.
5. Toss with cooked Banza spaghetti. Serve topped with grated Parmesan.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** Sauce 5 days fridge / 3 months freezer. Store sauce and pasta separately for best texture.
**Reheat:** Sauce on stovetop over medium-low. Pasta reheated in boiling water for 60 seconds or microwaved with a splash of water.
**Batch size:** Doubles easily — make a double batch of sauce and freeze half for a future week
**Health swaps:**
- Ground turkey or chicken over beef — cuts fat and cholesterol significantly
- Whole wheat or chickpea pasta — adds fiber and protein, lowers glycemic impact
- Zucchini noodles (zoodles) — cuts carbs dramatically for keto-friendly version
**Notes:** Classic weeknight staple and one of the best meal prep dinners in the book. Ground turkey is the best balance of health goals and flavor. Sauce gets better after a day in the fridge — make it Sunday, eat it Monday and Tuesday.
