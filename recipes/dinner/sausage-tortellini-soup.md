# Sausage Tortellini Soup

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#quick` `#comfort-food` `#weeknight`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~470 | ~22g | ~24g | ~38g | ~4g | ~95mg |
**Health Score:** 5/10

**Serves:** 6
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

## Ingredients

- 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 12 to 16 ounces fully cooked chicken sausage, any flavor, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
- 1 large fennel bulb, coarsely chopped, stalks thinly sliced, fronds finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, chopped
- Salt and pepper
- 6 quarts chicken broth
- About 10 ounces fresh or frozen cheese, spinach, or mushroom tortellini
- 2 cups chopped vegetables, such as snap or snow peas, green beans, asparagus, kale, spinach, or escarole, or frozen peas
- Grated Parmesan, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. Heat the oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high. Add the sausage and cook, stirring once or twice, until browned and crisp all over, 6 to 8 minutes. Add the chopped fennel bulb and garlic, season with salt and pepper, and cook, scraping up the browned bits as you go, until softened, 5 to 7 minutes.
2. Add the chicken broth and bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the broth is flavorful, 5 to 7 minutes. Scrape the browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Taste and season with salt and pepper.
3. Add the tortellini and cook according to package directions. In the last 3 minutes of the tortellini cooking, add the chopped vegetables and up to 1/2 cup sliced fennel stalks. For some tortellini, that might mean adding the vegetables with the pasta.
4. Remove from heat and stir in up to 1/4 cup chopped fennel fronds. Serve in bowls topped with Parmesan and more black pepper.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 3 days refrigerated
- **Reheat:** Warm gently over medium-low heat; add broth if the tortellini absorb too much liquid
- **Batch size:** Best fresh, but still makes good leftovers if you keep extra broth around

---

## Notes

- This is a classic fast comfort soup: sausage, fennel, broth, and tortellini do most of the work.
- The tortellini and sausage make it more substantial than a light broth soup, so the calorie count sits in the middle.
- If you want it lighter later, increase the vegetables and keep the Parmesan modest.
