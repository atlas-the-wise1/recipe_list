# Chorizo Taquitos

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#spicy` `#quick` `#weeknight`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~430 | ~18g | ~26g | ~28g | ~2g | ~125mg |
**Health Score:** 4/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 15 min | **Total:** ~30 min

---

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon neutral oil, such as canola
- 1 small onion, diced
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 jalapeño, diced
- 1/2 pound raw chorizo, casings removed if necessary
- 1/4 teaspoon chili powder
- 1/4 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
- 2 large eggs, beaten
- 4 to 6 6-inch flour tortillas
- 1/2 to 1 cup shredded pepper Jack cheese
- Salsa roja or salsa verde, optional, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. Heat a medium skillet over medium. Add the oil, then stir in the onion and garlic and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, about 4 minutes. Stir in the jalapeño and cook until softened, about 2 minutes more.
2. Crumble the chorizo into the pan, sprinkle with the chili powder, cumin, and black pepper, and stir to combine. Cook, stirring occasionally and breaking the chorizo into small chunks, until cooked through, 4 to 5 minutes.
3. Add the eggs, pouring them gradually into the pan in a circular motion. Stir into the chorizo mixture and cook until the eggs are just set. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting to keep the filling warm while you heat the tortillas.
4. Heat a separate pan, or a comal if you have one, over medium. Add the tortillas one by one and warm through, flipping each tortilla when it begins to puff on each side.
5. Fill each tortilla with the chorizo mixture, add cheese to taste, and roll it into a taquito. Repeat with the remaining filling and tortillas. Serve with salsa, if you like.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** Best eaten fresh, but leftovers keep 2 days refrigerated
- **Reheat:** Warm in a dry skillet or toaster oven so the tortillas stay a little crisp
- **Batch size:** Good as a small dinner, brunch spread, or party snack

---

## Notes

- This lands more like a savory brunch or dinner snack than a lean main course.
- Chorizo, eggs, oil, and cheese push the fat and cholesterol up quickly, so the health score stays low.
- If you want it lighter, reduce the cheese first and use 4 tortillas instead of 6.
