# Green Curry Salmon With Coconut Rice

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #heart-healthy #low-cholesterol
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 45 min | **Total:** ~55 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~620 | ~28g | ~34g | ~51g | ~1g | ~70mg |
**Health Score:** 5/10

---

## Ingredients

- 1¼ to 1½ lbs skinless salmon fillets
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 4 tbsp green curry paste
- 2 tbsp virgin coconut oil (or neutral oil)
- 1 bunch scallions, whites and greens, thinly sliced
- 2 garlic cloves, finely grated or minced
- 1 (14¾ oz) can unsweetened full-fat coconut milk
- 1¾ cups sushi rice or other short-grain rice, rinsed well
- 2 cups chopped baby spinach
- 1 cup chopped cilantro leaves and tender stems
- Lime wedges, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. Lightly season salmon with salt and pepper. Spread 1 tbsp curry paste all over the fillets. Set aside.
2. Heat coconut oil in a 5- or 6-quart Dutch oven over medium. Add scallion whites and most of the greens (reserve 2 tbsp scallion greens for garnish). Cook until golden brown, **~3 minutes**. Stir in garlic and a pinch of salt; cook until fragrant, **~1 minute**.
3. Stir in coconut milk, remaining 3 tbsp green curry paste, 1½ cups water, and 1½ tsp salt. Stir in rinsed rice and reduce heat to low. Simmer covered, stirring occasionally, **20 minutes**. Fold in spinach, cilantro, and a pinch of salt. Add 2–4 tbsp more water if rice looks dry or threatens to stick.
4. Place salmon fillets on top of rice. Raise heat to medium-low, cover, and cook until salmon is just cooked through, **12–20 minutes** depending on thickness.
5. If salmon finishes before rice, gently remove with a metal spatula, transfer to a plate, and tent to keep warm. Continue cooking rice until tender, adding water if needed. Season with salt.
6. Squeeze a lime wedge over salmon. Serve immediately garnished with reserved scallions and more lime wedges.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 3 days fridge — rice absorbs coconut milk as it sits; add a splash of water when reheating
**Reheat:** Stovetop over medium-low with a splash of water. Microwave covered with a damp paper towel.
**Batch size:** Scales well — use a larger Dutch oven for 6–8 servings
**Make it lighter:** Light coconut milk cuts ~80 cal and ~10g fat per serving with minimal flavor loss
**Salmon tip:** Check salmon at 12 min — thinner fillets cook fast. Pull it just before fully opaque; residual heat finishes it.
**Notes:** One of the most indulgent-feeling recipes in the book that still hits the heart-healthy and low-cholesterol marks. The coconut rice absorbs all the green curry and salmon juices — extraordinary flavor payoff for a one-pot meal. Higher calorie than most dinners due to coconut milk and rice — portion the rice carefully if fat loss is the priority that week.
