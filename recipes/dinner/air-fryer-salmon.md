# Air-Fryer Salmon

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #heart-healthy #low-cholesterol #high-protein #fat-loss #keto
**Servings:** 2
**Prep Time:** 5 min | **Cook Time:** 12 min | **Total:** ~17 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~370 | ~42g | ~20g | ~4g | ~1g | ~95mg |

---

## Ingredients
- 2 (6-oz) salmon fillets — skin-on or skinless (frozen works, see notes)
- Olive oil, as needed
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- 1 lemon (optional)
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes (optional)

---

## Instructions

1. Heat air fryer to 400°F (if preheating is recommended for your model).
2. Pat salmon fillets dry with paper towels. Brush olive oil all over salmon; season with salt and pepper.
3. **If using lemon and tomatoes:** Thinly slice half the lemon crosswise; set remaining half aside. In a small bowl, toss cherry tomatoes and lemon slices with 1 tbsp olive oil and a pinch of salt.
4. Place salmon fillets in the air fryer basket skin-side down. Top with lemon slices and scatter tomatoes around the basket.
5. Cook until skin is crisped and lightly browned, **5–7 minutes**. Flip fillets and cook another **4–6 minutes** until salmon is cooked through and flakes evenly.
6. Squeeze remaining lemon half over fillets and serve.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 3 days fridge (salmon doesn't freeze well once cooked)
**Reheat:** Air fryer at 300°F for 3–4 min — avoids drying out. Avoid microwave if possible.
**Batch size:** Scale up fillets as needed — cook in batches to avoid overcrowding the basket
**Frozen tip:** Thaw overnight in fridge, or run under cold water 10–15 min before cooking. Pat extra dry before oiling.
**Notes:** One of the best recipes for your health goals — high protein, heart-healthy omega-3s, low cholesterol, keto-friendly, and under 20 min start to finish. Strong weekly staple candidate.
