# Lemon Herb Grilled Chicken Breast

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 12 min | **Total:** ~27 min

---

## Ingredients

- 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (6 oz each)
- 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp black pepper
- ½ tsp lemon zest

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** In a small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, minced garlic, olive oil, oregano, thyme, salt, pepper, and lemon zest.
**STEP 2:** Place chicken breasts in a ziplock bag or shallow dish and pour marinade over them. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (or up to 4 hours for deeper flavor).
**STEP 3:** Preheat grill to medium-high heat (about 400°F). Oil grates to prevent sticking.
**STEP 4:** Remove chicken from marinade and place on hot grill. Reserve excess marinade.
**STEP 5:** Grill for 5–6 minutes per side until internal temperature reaches 165°F and juices run clear.
**STEP 6:** Transfer to a plate and brush with reserved marinade. Let rest for 3 minutes before serving.

---

## Notes

- **Oven option:** If grilling isn't available, bake at 400°F for 18–20 minutes until cooked through.
- **Marinate longer:** Overnight marination intensifies flavor without affecting texture (acid doesn't over-tenderize at this ratio).
- **Meal prep friendly:** Grilled chicken stores well in the fridge for 4 days; reheat gently with a splash of water or lemon juice to keep it moist.
- **Flavor swap:** Add minced rosemary (1 tsp) or Italian seasoning (1 tsp) to customize the herb profile.
- **Serving ideas:** Pair with roasted asparagus, cauliflower rice, or a crisp green salad with olive oil vinaigrette.
- **Batch cooking:** This recipe doubles easily—grill extra for weekday lunches or protein bowls.
- **Prevent drying:** Don't skip the rest period after grilling; carryover cooking will complete cooking without overdrying the meat.
