# Ginger-Dill Salmon

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#heart-healthy` `#low-cholesterol` `#high-protein` `#keto` `#meal-prep`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~420 | ~42g | ~24g | ~12g | ~5g | ~95mg |
**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 15–20 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

## Ingredients

- 1 (1½-pound) salmon fillet, skin-on or skinless
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 6 tablespoons finely chopped dill
- 1 (2-inch) piece ginger, scrubbed and finely grated (no need to peel)
- 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for serving
- 1 grapefruit
- 2 oranges
- 6 small radishes, cut into thin wedges
- 1 avocado
- Flaky sea salt, for finishing *(optional)*

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Heat oven to 325°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper. Pat salmon dry, place skin-side down (if skin-on), and season with salt and pepper.

**STEP 2:** In a medium bowl, stir together dill, ginger, and olive oil until combined. Season with salt and pepper. Spread half the dill-ginger mixture over the top of the salmon; reserve the rest. Bake until cooked through — 15 to 20 minutes. *(Done when fish flakes or instant-read thermometer reads 120°F at the thickest part.)*

**STEP 3:** As salmon cooks, cut off the top and bottom of the grapefruit and set it on one of the cut sides. Follow the curve to cut away the peel and pith. Squeeze the peels over the reserved dill-ginger mixture to extract juice. Cut fruit in half top to bottom, then slice into ¼-inch half-moons; remove seeds. Halve any especially large pieces. Transfer fruit and any cutting board juices to the bowl. Repeat with the oranges. Add radishes, season generously with salt, and stir gently to combine.

**STEP 4:** Break salmon into large pieces and divide across plates with the citrus salad. Peel, pit, and quarter the avocado lengthwise; add to plates. Season with salt. Spoon bowl juices over everything. Finish with black pepper, a drizzle of olive oil, and flaky salt if using.

---

## Tip
Both the salad and salmon can be made up to two days ahead — everything is good at room temperature or cold. To embellish: baby greens, thinly sliced cucumbers or fennel, roasted beets, soba noodles, tostadas, furikake, or chile oil.

---

## Notes

- **Low and slow at 325°F** — salmon stays silky and just barely cooked through; don't rush it at high heat
- **120°F internal = perfect** — it'll look slightly translucent in the center; that's right. Pull it at 120, not 145.
- **Squeeze the peels** — don't discard after cutting the citrus; squeeze every bit of juice into the dill-ginger bowl — it's the base of the dressing
- **Meal-prep friendly:** Salmon and citrus salad both keep 2 days refrigerated; prep avocado day-of to prevent browning
- **Hits every health goal** — omega-3 rich salmon + citrus + avocado = heart-healthy trifecta; low carb, high protein, anti-inflammatory
- **Serve cold too** — works beautifully as a cold lunch the next day over greens or with tostadas
- **Keto note:** Citrus adds some carbs (~12g/serving); sub radishes + cucumber + avocado only for strict keto (~4g net carbs)
