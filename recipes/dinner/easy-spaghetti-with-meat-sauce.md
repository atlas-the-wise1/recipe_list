# Easy Spaghetti With Meat Sauce

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#quick` `#meal-prep` `#classic`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~600 | ~30g | ~18g | ~70g | ~4g | ~85mg |
**Health Score:** 6/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 15 min | **Total:** ~25 min

---

## Ingredients

- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped
- 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1/2 pound ground beef, preferably 20 percent fat, pork, or dark meat turkey
- 12 ounces spaghetti, pappardelle, or other long pasta
- 1/4 cup tomato paste
- 2 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce
- Grated Parmesan, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium-high. Add the onion, garlic, and 1/2 teaspoon salt and cook, stirring often, until beginning to soften, 3 minutes. Add beef and cook, breaking it up with a wooden spoon, until no longer pink, about 3 minutes.
2. Add pasta to the pot and cook according to package instructions until al dente.
3. Meanwhile, add tomato paste to the skillet and cook, stirring, until darkened, about 1 minute. Add 1 cup water, Worcestershire sauce, 1/2 teaspoon pepper, and 1/2 teaspoon salt and simmer until slightly reduced, about 5 minutes.
4. Using tongs, transfer pasta directly from the pot to the skillet along with 1 cup pasta water. Increase heat to high and simmer vigorously, tossing, until sauce reduces and coats the pasta, about 2 minutes. Season to taste with salt and pepper. Serve with Parmesan.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 4 to 5 days refrigerated
- **Reheat:** Warm gently in a skillet with a splash of water or microwave in short bursts
- **Batch size:** Easy to double; sauce can be made ahead and held separately

---

## Notes

- This is a streamlined meat-sauce version that leans on tomato paste and Worcestershire for quick depth.
- Ground beef gives the most classic flavor, while dark meat turkey keeps it a little lighter.
- If you want it richer later, add extra Parmesan and a drizzle of olive oil at serving.
