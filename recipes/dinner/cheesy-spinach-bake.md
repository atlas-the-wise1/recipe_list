# Cheesy Spinach Bake

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#vegetarian` `#meal-prep` `#high-fiber`
**Nutrition (per serving):**

**Health Score:** 6/10
**Serves:** 8
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 25 min | **Total:** ~40 min

---

## Ingredients

- 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 1 large yellow onion or 2 shallots, finely chopped
- 3 garlic cloves, coarsely chopped
- Salt and pepper
- 1/2 cup panko or fresh coarse bread crumbs
- 1 cup / 3 ounces finely grated Parmesan
- 5 (10-ounce) packages frozen chopped spinach, thawed and squeezed dry
- 2 cups (1 pint) half-and-half
- 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg (optional)
- 1/2 cup / 1 1/2 ounces grated Gruyere or extra-sharp Cheddar

---

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to 400°F. In a large ovenproof skillet, melt the butter. Spoon 1 tablespoon of the butter into a medium bowl. Add the onion and garlic to the skillet, season with salt and pepper, and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened but not browned, 7 to 10 minutes.
2. While the onions are sauteing, stir the bread crumbs into the bowl of butter until coated. Season with salt and pepper, then stir in half the Parmesan.
3. Add the spinach to the skillet and stir until warm and dry, 2 to 4 minutes. Turn off the heat. Stir in the half-and-half, remaining Parmesan, and nutmeg, if using, then taste and adjust the seasoning. If you want to bake this in a 2 1/2-quart or larger casserole dish, transfer the spinach now.
4. Top evenly with the Gruyere, then the panko mixture. Bake until golden on top and bubbling around the edges, 20 to 25 minutes.

---

## Notes

- Rich, vegetable-forward side dish with good fiber from spinach, but it is still butter- and cheese-heavy.
- Best fit for a dinner side rather than a standalone lunch.
- If you want a lighter version later, I would cut the butter and half-and-half first.
