# Baked Fish With Olives and Ginger

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#heart-healthy` `#low-cholesterol`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~430 | ~34g | ~30g | ~6g | ~2g | ~70mg |
**Health Score:** 8/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~30 min

---

## Ingredients

- 1/2 cup olive oil
- 1 cup pitted green olives, preferably Castelvetrano, roughly chopped
- 1 tablespoon drained capers
- 1 lemon, halved, 1/2 thinly sliced
- 4 6-ounce skinless mild white fish fillets, about 1 inch thick
- Salt and black pepper
- 1 1/2-inch piece fresh ginger, finely grated
- 1/4 teaspoon crushed red pepper
- 2 packed tablespoons torn or chopped fresh parsley or cilantro leaves and tender stems

---

## Instructions

1. Heat the oven to 400 F. On a rimmed sheet pan or baking dish, combine the olive oil, olives, capers, and lemon slices. Turn the fish fillets in the oil to coat and space them evenly apart. Season the exposed parts with salt, then the grated ginger, rubbing it in. Top each fillet with a grind or two of black pepper and the crushed red pepper.
2. Cover with foil and bake until the center of each fish fillet is opaque and the fish flakes easily with a fork, about 20 minutes. Thinner fillets will cook more quickly.
3. Carefully remove the foil, then squeeze the remaining lemon half over everything. Spoon the olive and olive oil mixture over the fish. Garnish with the parsley or cilantro and serve.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 2 days refrigerated
- **Reheat:** Warm gently in a low oven or covered skillet so the fish stays tender
- **Batch size:** Best as a fresh dinner, but the olive-ginger topping can be made ahead

---

## Notes

- This is a bright, Mediterranean-style fish dinner that leans on olives, capers, lemon, and ginger for punch.
- The olive oil makes it richer than a bare baked fish, but it stays squarely in the heart-healthy lane.
- If you want it lighter later, reduce the oil a bit and serve with extra vegetables instead of starch.
