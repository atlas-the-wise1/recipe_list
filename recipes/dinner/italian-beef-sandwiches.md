# Italian Beef Sandwiches

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#meal-prep`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~650 | ~38g | ~34g | ~40g | ~2g | ~115mg |
**Health Score:** 5/10
**Serves:** 8
**Prep Time:** 20 min | **Cook Time:** 8 hr | **Total:** ~8 hr 20 min

---

## Ingredients

- 2 1/2 to 3 pounds boneless chuck roast, trimmed
- 1 tablespoon kosher salt
- Black pepper
- 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 yellow onion, halved
- 1 head garlic, halved crosswise
- 2 carrots, peeled
- 2 celery stalks
- 1/2 teaspoon red-pepper flakes
- 6 cups beef broth
- 2 tablespoons tamari or soy sauce
- 6 to 8 soft hoagie rolls, soft French rolls, or ciabatta rolls
- 1/4 pound provolone, thinly sliced
- About 2 cups giardiniera, preferably Chicago-style
- 1 cup pickled green hot peppers
- Fresh parsley, optional, leaves torn

---

## Instructions

1. Season the meat generously all over with salt and pepper. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the meat and sear on one side, undisturbed, until deeply browned, about 5 minutes. Flip and brown lightly on all remaining sides, about 2 minutes per side, lowering the heat as necessary to avoid scorching the oil. Using tongs, remove the meat and place it in a 6- to 8-quart slow cooker. Add the onion, garlic, carrots, celery, red-pepper flakes, and beef broth.
2. Cover and cook on low for 8 hours, until the beef is very tender. You can also cook, covered, in an ovenproof Dutch oven or pot in a 300 F oven until very tender, about 3 1/2 to 4 hours. When the beef is ready, it should be juicy and flavorful and pull apart easily with a fork. Using tongs and a slotted spoon, remove the beef to a cutting board and set the vegetables aside. Slice the beef thinly across the grain, then return it to the broth. Stir in the tamari or soy sauce and cover to keep warm.
3. Heat a broiler to low. Split the rolls in half. Top each sandwich with some beef and jus, and a layer of provolone over the top. Broil just to melt the cheese, being careful not to overdo it. The roll should stay soft but be slightly warm. Stuff the sandwich with giardiniera, pickled peppers, and fresh parsley leaves, if using.
4. Serve warm, with broth for dipping if desired, or drizzle some jus over the sandwich while warm.

---

## Notes

- Hearty dinner sandwich with strong protein, but the bread, cheese, and salt make it more of a satisfying comfort-food meal than a lean one.
- Best fit for batch cooking or weekend prep if you portion the beef and keep the toppings separate.
- Health score reflects the high sodium and refined-roll structure, balanced by the relatively high protein and a reasonable amount of vegetables in the braise.
- Macros assume 8 sandwiches from the full batch.
