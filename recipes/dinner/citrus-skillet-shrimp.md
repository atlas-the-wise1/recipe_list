# Citrus Skillet Shrimp With Shallots and Jalapeños

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #high-protein #fat-loss
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 10 min | **Total:** ~20 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~245 | ~35g | ~8g | ~11g | ~2g | ~215mg |
**Health Score:** 7/10

---

## Ingredients

- 1 navel orange, zested and juiced
- 2 limes, zested and juiced
- 1 medium shallot, cut into thin rings
- 1 jalapeño, seeded and finely chopped
- ¼ tsp ground cumin
- Kosher salt
- ½ packed cup fresh cilantro leaves and tender stems
- 1½ lbs large peeled, deveined shrimp (tails on or off)
- ½ tsp smoked paprika
- 2 tbsp olive oil

---

## Instructions

1. In a small bowl, combine orange and lime zest and juice with shallot, jalapeño, cumin, and ½ tsp salt. Roughly chop two-thirds of the cilantro and add to the bowl. Toss to combine and set aside.
2. Pat shrimp dry. Season all over with salt and smoked paprika.
3. Heat a 12-inch skillet over medium-high until hot. Add oil and tilt skillet to coat the bottom evenly. Spread shrimp in an even layer (slightly snug is fine).
4. Cook undisturbed for **3 minutes** until just pink underneath. Flip and cook until fully pink all over with no grey spots, **1–3 more minutes** depending on size.
5. Remove from heat onto an empty burner. Immediately pour the citrus mixture into the skillet and gently toss to coat, **~1 minute**. *(Shrimp continue cooking in residual heat — undercook by 30 seconds if needed.)*
6. Tear or chop remaining cilantro, sprinkle on top, and serve.

---

## Tips
- **Juicing hack:** Insert a fork into the orange or lime half and move it up and down like a lever while squeezing. No press needed, pulp welcome.
- Don't overcook shrimp — they go from perfect to rubbery fast. Pull them just before you think they're done.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 2 days fridge (shrimp don't hold as long as other proteins)
**Reheat:** Briefly in a warm skillet over low — 1–2 min max. Do not microwave.
**Batch size:** Scales easily — use a larger skillet or cook in batches to avoid steaming
**Pairs well with:** Cauliflower rice (keto), brown rice, or alongside the Endive Salad
**Notes:** One of the lightest and fastest recipes in the book — 20 min start to finish, 220 calories, 35g protein. Hits every health goal. Strong weekly staple candidate, especially paired with a side.
