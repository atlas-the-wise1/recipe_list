# Lemon-Dill Meatballs With Orzo

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #heart-healthy #high-protein #meal-prep
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~480 | ~42g | ~16g | ~38g | ~3g | ~105mg |
**Health Score:** 7/10

---

## Ingredients

- 1½ cups loosely packed chopped dill (~3 oz), plus more for serving
- 1 cup full-fat Greek yogurt
- 1 large garlic clove, finely grated
- 1 lemon, zested and halved
- 1 lb ground turkey or chicken
- 1 tsp fennel seeds (optional)
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 cup orzo
- 3 anchovy fillets
- 4 cups chopped escarole, spinach, mustard greens, or baby kale (~3 oz)

---

## Instructions

1. In a medium bowl, stir together dill, Greek yogurt, garlic, and lemon zest. Transfer ½ cup of this yogurt mixture to a large bowl. Add ground turkey, fennel seeds (if using), and 1 tsp salt. Mix lightly with hands to combine. Roll into **12 meatballs** (~3 tbsp each) and refrigerate until step 3. *(Can refrigerate up to 1 hour ahead.)*
2. To the remaining yogurt in the medium bowl, squeeze in juice from the lemon halves. Season with salt until flavorful and slightly puckery. Set aside as the serving sauce.
3. In a medium skillet, melt butter over medium. Add orzo, anchovies, and ½ tsp each salt and pepper. Stir until orzo is golden, **2–4 minutes**. Add 2½ cups water and bring to a boil over medium-high. Reduce to a simmer over medium-low, then add meatballs in a single layer. Cover and cook without stirring until orzo is tender and meatballs are cooked through, **12–14 minutes**.
4. Turn off heat. Add greens on top, season with salt and pepper. Cover and let sit until greens are wilted, **3–5 minutes**. Taste orzo and adjust seasoning.
5. Serve with a generous spoonful of the lemon-dill yogurt sauce, extra dill, and black pepper.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 4 days fridge / 2 months freezer (meatballs freeze well; store orzo and meatballs together)
**Reheat:** Stovetop over medium-low with a splash of water to loosen the orzo. Microwave covered works well.
**Batch size:** Doubles easily — use a larger skillet for the orzo or cook in two batches
**Anchovy note:** Don't skip the anchovies — they dissolve completely and add a deep savory backbone, not fishiness. Nobody will know they're there.
**Pairs well with:** A simple green salad or the Endive Salad as a side
**Notes:** One of the most complete one-pan meals in the book — meatballs, starchy orzo, and greens all cooked together. The lemon-dill yogurt sauce ties everything together and doubles as extra protein. The meatballs and yogurt sauce can both be made ahead, making this genuinely fast on a weeknight. Strong weekly rotation candidate.
