# Cajun Shrimp and Grits

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#meal-prep`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~540 | ~38g | ~26g | ~38g | ~2g | ~200mg |
**Health Score:** 4/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 30 min | **Total:** ~45 min

---

## Ingredients

**For the creamy grits:**
- 1 cup stone-ground cornmeal (or quick grits)
- 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
- ½ cup heavy cream (or 2% milk for lighter option)
- 2 tbsp butter
- ½ cup sharp cheddar cheese, grated
- Salt and white pepper to taste

**For the Cajun shrimp:**
- 1.5 lbs large shrimp (16/20 count), peeled and deveined
- 4 oz andouille sausage (or turkey sausage), diced
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 1 green bell pepper, diced
- ½ yellow onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced

**For the Cajun spice & sauce:**
- 1 tbsp paprika
- 1 tsp cayenne pepper
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- ½ tsp dried oregano
- ½ tsp garlic powder
- ½ tsp onion powder
- ¼ tsp black pepper
- ¼ tsp salt
- ½ cup chicken broth
- 2 tbsp heavy cream (optional, for sauce richness)
- 1 tbsp butter
- 2 scallions, sliced
- Fresh parsley for garnish

---

## Preparation

**For the grits:**

**STEP 1:** Bring chicken broth to a boil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat.
**STEP 2:** Whisk in cornmeal slowly, stirring constantly to prevent lumps. Reduce heat to medium-low.
**STEP 3:** Stir frequently for 20–25 minutes until the mixture thickens and becomes creamy (it should pull away from the sides of the pan).
**STEP 4:** Stir in butter, heavy cream, and cheddar cheese. Season with salt and white pepper to taste. Keep warm on low heat.

**For the shrimp:**

**STEP 5:** While grits cook, combine all Cajun spices in a small bowl.
**STEP 6:** In a large skillet over medium-high heat, cook andouille sausage, stirring occasionally, until browned and fat renders (about 5 minutes). Remove to a plate.
**STEP 7:** Add olive oil to the same skillet. Season shrimp with salt and half the Cajun spice mixture. Sear shrimp in a single layer for 1–2 minutes per side until pink and just cooked through. Remove to a plate.
**STEP 8:** Add remaining olive oil if needed. Sauté diced peppers and onion for 4–5 minutes until softened.
**STEP 9:** Add minced garlic and remaining Cajun spices. Cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
**STEP 10:** Return cooked sausage and shrimp to the skillet. Add chicken broth and optional heavy cream. Simmer for 2–3 minutes until flavors meld and sauce thickens slightly.
**STEP 11:** Swirl in 1 tbsp butter. Taste and adjust seasoning.

**To serve:**

**STEP 12:** Spoon creamy grits into four shallow bowls. Top with Cajun shrimp and sausage mixture.
**STEP 13:** Garnish with sliced scallions and fresh parsley. Serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- **Sausage swap:** Use smoked turkey sausage for a leaner protein option (~10g fat vs. 15g in traditional andouille). Cajun flavor profile remains bold and satisfying.
- **Grits texture:** Constant stirring prevents lumps and ensures creamy results. Don't skip this step. If grits seem too thick, thin with a splash of warm broth.
- **Make-ahead strategy:** Prepare grits and shrimp sauce separately up to 1 day ahead. Reheat gently (grits need medium heat and constant stirring to prevent seizing). Combine fresh at serving time.
- **Heat level:** Cayenne is prominent here—reduce to ½ tsp for milder versions, or serve extra hot sauce on the side for those who want more.
- **Cheese variation:** Swap sharp cheddar for smoked Gouda or Gruyère for different flavor depths.
- **Vegetables flexible:** Add diced tomatoes, zucchini, or mushrooms to the pepper/onion mix for additional vegetables and flavor.
- **Quick sausage hack:** Pre-cooked sausage from the store can be used—just warm it through with the other ingredients, saving you 5 minutes of active cooking.
