# Mayo-Marinated Chicken With Chimichurri

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#fat-loss` `#keto`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~380 | ~42g | ~22g | ~2g | ~0g | ~115mg |
**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min + optional 4–24h marinate | **Cook Time:** 5 min | **Total:** ~15 min active

---

## Ingredients

- 4 chicken breast cutlets (4–5 oz each), pounded to about ¼-inch thick
- Kosher salt and ground black pepper
- ⅓ cup store-bought or homemade mayonnaise
- 1 cup chimichurri *(store-bought or homemade)*

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Season chicken cutlets on both sides with salt and pepper. Set aside.

**STEP 2:** Whisk together mayonnaise and ¼ cup chimichurri in a large bowl. Reserve remaining ¾ cup chimichurri. Add chicken cutlets and turn to coat. Cook immediately, or for better flavor, transfer to a sealed container and refrigerate 4 to 24 hours.

**STEP 3 — Grill method:** Heat a gas or charcoal grill over high heat for 10 minutes. Cook chicken directly over high heat, turning and flipping occasionally, until just cooked through and lightly charred — 4 to 5 minutes. Transfer to a platter. Spoon some reserved chimichurri over top; serve the rest on the side.

**STEP 4 — Skillet method:** Heat a large (12-inch) cast-iron or nonstick skillet over medium-high until a drop of water balls up and dances across the surface. Add chicken in a single layer and cook, swirling and flipping occasionally, until browned all over and just cooked through — about 4 minutes. Transfer to a platter. Spoon chimichurri over top; serve the rest on the side.

---

## Notes

- **Mayo = moisture insurance** — the fat in mayo prevents the lean chicken breast from drying out at high heat; this is the whole trick, and it works better than any other marinade for cutlets
- **Longer marinate = better** — 24 hours in the fridge transforms the texture and flavor; even 4 hours makes a noticeable difference over cooking immediately
- **Pound to ¼-inch** — don't skip this; even thickness = even cooking; thick spots overcook before thin spots are done
- **Hot pan or hot grill only** — the mayo crust needs high heat to brown properly; medium heat = steam, not sear
- **Chimichurri:** Use store-bought in a pinch (Trader Joe's makes a solid one) or blend fresh parsley, garlic, olive oil, red wine vinegar, oregano, and red pepper flakes
- **Keto-friendly:** <2g carbs per serving as written
- **Serve with:** Roasted vegetables, a simple green salad, or over arugula with extra chimichurri as the dressing
- **Meal-prep:** Marinate up to 24h in advance; cook fresh in under 5 minutes — ideal weeknight setup
