# Asparagus, Spinach and Leek Soup

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#heart-healthy` `#vegetarian` `#low-cholesterol`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~170 | ~5g | ~10g | ~16g | ~5g | ~0mg |
**Health Score:** 8/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 25 min | **Total:** ~40 min

---

## Ingredients

- 2 1/2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 3 leeks, white and light green parts, thinly sliced
- 1/4 teaspoon red-pepper flakes
- 2 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more to taste
- 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, plus more to taste
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 large fennel bulb with fronds, trimmed and diced
- 2 medium zucchini, about 12 ounces, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
- 1 bunch asparagus, about 1 pound, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
- 4 cups vegetable stock
- 1 cup cilantro, mint, or parsley leaves and tender stems, chopped
- 1 cup dill fronds and tender stems, chopped
- 10 ounces baby spinach
- 1 to 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice

---

## Instructions

1. In a soup pot over medium-high heat, add 2 tablespoons oil. Let heat for 30 seconds, then add the leeks, red-pepper flakes, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and 1/4 teaspoon black pepper. Sauté leeks until golden at the edges, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, 1 minute. Stir in the fennel, zucchini, 1 teaspoon salt, and remaining 1/4 teaspoon pepper.
2. Set aside the asparagus tips and add the stems to the pot. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables turn bright green, about 5 minutes.
3. Pour in the vegetable stock and bring to a simmer. Reduce heat and simmer for 10 to 15 minutes, or until the vegetables are very tender.
4. While the soup is simmering, heat a small skillet over medium-high heat. Add the remaining 1/2 tablespoon oil. Once hot, add the asparagus tips and a pinch of salt, and cook until just tender, 2 to 3 minutes. Set aside for garnish.
5. To the soup pot, add the herbs and 1 cup reserved fennel fronds. Working in batches, stir in the spinach until wilted. Add the remaining 1/2 teaspoon salt.
6. Use an immersion blender to purée until smooth, or purée in batches in a regular blender. You may need to add the remaining 1/2 cup broth for a looser texture.
7. Stir in lemon juice, and taste for salt and pepper, adding more if needed. Serve garnished with fennel fronds and asparagus tips.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 4 days refrigerated
- **Reheat:** Warm gently over medium-low heat; add a splash of stock or water if it thickens
- **Batch size:** Good for a light dinner or lunch; doubles well if you want more soup for the week

---

## Notes

- This is a very green, vegetable-forward blended soup with excellent texture from the leek-fennel-asparagus base.
- Olive oil keeps it silky without needing cream, and the lemon finish brightens the whole pot.
- If you want it richer later, serve with crusty bread or a little grated Parmesan.
