# Rotisserie Chicken and Greens Pasta — Healthier Variant

> 💡 **Healthier Variant** of Rotisserie Chicken and Greens Pasta — see `rotisserie-chicken-greens-pasta.md` for the classic version.

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #high-protein #heart-healthy #low-cholesterol #meal-prep #fat-loss
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 25 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

## Macros (per serving, estimated)
| Calories | Protein | Carbs | Fat | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~480 | ~52g | ~60g | ~7g | ~8g | ~90mg |

**What changed vs. classic:** ~80 fewer calories, ~5g less fat, ~15mg less cholesterol per serving. Achieves this by:
- Using **whole-wheat rigatoni** instead of regular (adds ~3g fiber per serving, lower glycemic impact)
- **Removing chicken skin entirely** (discard, don't chop and add — saves ~4g sat fat per serving)
- Reducing butter from **3 tbsp to 1 tbsp** (use the saved pasta water and stock to maintain body without butter)
- Bumping greens from **8–10 oz to 12 oz** for more fiber and volume

---

## Ingredients

- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 lb **whole-wheat** rigatoni, penne rigate, or ziti
- 1 small rotisserie chicken (~1¾ lbs) or ~2½ cups shredded cooked chicken
- **1 tbsp** unsalted butter (reduced from 3 tbsp)
- 1 medium yellow onion, thinly sliced (~2 packed cups)
- 5 large garlic cloves, finely chopped or grated
- 2 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 cup low-sodium chicken stock
- ¼ cup plain Greek yogurt (full-fat or 2%)
- ½ cup reserved pasta water (plus more as needed)
- **12 oz** baby kale, spinach, or chopped chard (increased from 8–10 oz)
- 1 juicy lemon, zested then halved
- Parmesan, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook pasta until al dente. **Reserve at least 1 cup pasta water before draining.** Drain and set aside.
2. Prep the chicken: Separate skin from meat, then meat from bones. **Discard skin and bones.** Shred meat into bite-size pieces and season with 1½ tsp pepper and salt to taste. Set aside.
3. Place a Dutch oven or large heavy pot over medium heat. Add butter. Once melted and bubbling, add onions, season with salt and pepper, and cook, pressing often with a spatula, until very soft, translucent, and jammy with golden brown edges, **~10 minutes**. Stir in garlic and mustard and cook until fragrant, **~2 minutes**.
4. Raise heat to high. Add ¾ cup stock and ¼ cup pasta water. Scrape up any browned bits. Bring to a simmer, then stir in pasta and seasoned chicken. Add greens in small handfuls, stirring until wilted and tender.
5. Turn off heat. Add lemon zest and squeeze lemon halves over the top. Stir in Greek yogurt quickly until fully incorporated and creamy. Add remaining ¼ cup stock or more pasta water for sauciness.
6. Serve topped with grated Parmesan and extra pepper.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 4 days fridge — whole-wheat pasta holds up slightly better than regular pasta as leftovers
**Reheat:** Stovetop over medium-low with a splash of stock or water. Microwave covered works well.
**Batch size:** Doubles easily
**Greek yogurt tip:** Always stir in off heat — adding while the pan is still on the burner can cause it to break and curdle.
**Notes:** The whole-wheat pasta swap is barely noticeable in this dish given the bold Dijon-lemon-yogurt sauce. Dropping the chicken skin and two tablespoons of butter cuts significant saturated fat without losing richness — the pasta water and Greek yogurt provide all the body you need.
