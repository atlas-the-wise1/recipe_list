# Winter Squash and Rice Soup with Pancetta

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#heart-healthy` `#meal-prep`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~420 | ~18g | ~22g | ~42g | ~4g | ~35mg |
**Serves:** 4–6
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 55 min | **Total:** ~70 min

---

## Ingredients

- 1 small butternut squash (1½–2 lbs) OR 1 medium delicata squash (~2 lbs), peeled, halved, seeded, cut into ¾-inch chunks *(no need to peel delicata)*
- 3 tablespoons granulated sugar
- 4 tablespoons olive oil
- Salt and pepper
- 8 oz pancetta or bacon, diced
- 1 large onion, chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 5 fresh thyme sprigs
- ¾ cup jasmine rice (or other long-grain), rinsed
- 8 cups chicken broth (preferably homemade or low-sodium)
- Chopped fresh parsley, for serving

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Heat oven to 425°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment. Toss squash with sugar and 2 tablespoons olive oil; season with salt and pepper. Roast, tossing once halfway through, until cooked through but not too soft — golden-brown caramelization on the edges, about 30 minutes. Turn off oven; set aside.

**STEP 2:** Meanwhile, heat remaining 2 tablespoons olive oil in a Dutch oven or large pot over medium-high. Add diced pancetta and cook, stirring occasionally, until golden-brown and crisp — about 7 minutes. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate. Discard all but 2 tablespoons of fat from the pot.

**STEP 3:** Add onion and garlic to the pot; season with salt. Cook, stirring frequently, until softened and lightly browned — 3 to 5 minutes. Add thyme sprigs; stir until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Add rice and stir to coat in the fat.

**STEP 4:** Add broth and bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer. Cook until rice is slightly al dente — 20 to 25 minutes. *(Rice will continue to cook in the hot broth as it sits.)* Discard thyme sprigs. Taste and season with salt if needed.

**STEP 5:** Wait to add squash, pancetta, and garnishes until just before serving — this preserves the distinct textures. Ladle brothy rice into bowls; top with roasted squash, crispy pancetta, fresh parsley, and more black pepper.

---

## Tip
If using delicata squash, no need to peel — the skin softens during roasting and is edible.

---

## Notes

- **The sugar on the squash is intentional** — it accelerates caramelization and creates a slightly candied edge that contrasts beautifully with the savory broth
- **Add toppings at the table, not the pot** — squash and pancetta added to the pot will lose their texture fast; keep separate until serving
- **Meal-prep:** Store broth + rice separately from squash and pancetta; combine at serving. Broth keeps 4 days refrigerated; note rice will absorb liquid overnight — add more broth when reheating
- **Lighter swap:** Sub turkey bacon for pancetta to cut ~80 cal and significant saturated fat per serving
- **Delicata vs butternut:** Delicata is easier (no peeling), slightly nuttier; butternut is sweeter and more widely available — both work great
- **Low-sodium broth matters:** 8 cups is a lot of liquid; start low-sodium and season at the end rather than fighting saltiness
