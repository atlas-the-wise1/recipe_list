# White Bean, Rice, and Dill Soup

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #heart-healthy #low-cholesterol #fat-loss #meal-prep
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 40 min | **Total:** ~55 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~380 | ~14g | ~14g | ~58g | ~12g | ~0mg |
**Health Score:** 6/10

---

## Ingredients

- ¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil
- 6 garlic cloves, roughly chopped
- 2 large carrots, scrubbed and finely chopped
- 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
- 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- Red-pepper flakes (optional), to taste
- ½ cup white jasmine rice, rinsed
- 2 (15 oz) cans navy beans or cannellini beans, rinsed
- 1 tbsp dried dill or ¼ cup chopped fresh dill
- Lemon (optional), for serving

---

## Instructions

1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium. Add garlic, carrots, celery, and onion. Season with a good pinch of salt and cook, stirring occasionally, until vegetables have softened, **~10 minutes**.
2. Add turmeric and red-pepper flakes (if using). Cook until fragrant, **~1 minute**. Add rice, stir, and cook **1 minute**. Add beans and dill; season well with salt (~1½ tbsp) and black pepper to taste. Stir and cook **1 minute**.
3. Add 6 cups water, stir, and partially cover. Raise heat to high and bring to a boil. Cover completely, reduce heat to low, and simmer, stirring occasionally, until rice is completely soft and beans are creamy, **~30 minutes**. Taste for seasoning as it simmers.
4. Serve with a squeeze of lemon if desired.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 5 days fridge / 3 months freezer — note that rice continues to absorb liquid as it sits; add a splash of water when reheating to loosen
**Reheat:** Stovetop over medium-low with a splash of water. Microwave works well covered.
**Batch size:** Doubles easily — great Sunday batch cook for the week
**Notes:** Zero cholesterol, high fiber, fully plant-based. Turmeric adds anti-inflammatory benefits. One of the most hands-off soups in the book — set it and simmer. Protein is moderate at 14g; stir in a handful of shredded rotisserie chicken when serving to boost protein without changing the recipe.
