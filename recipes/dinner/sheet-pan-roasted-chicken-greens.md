# Sheet Pan Roasted Chicken With Greens

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #heart-healthy #high-protein #low-cholesterol #fat-loss #meal-prep #keto
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 50 min | **Total:** ~65 min (+ up to 24 hr dry brine optional)

## Ingredients

- 1 whole chicken (~3½ to 4½ lbs)
- Salt (Diamond Crystal kosher — ~½ tsp per lb of chicken)
- 2 large or 3 medium bunches dark leafy greens — kale, collard greens, mustard greens, dandelion greens, chard, or broccoli rabe (preferably a mix)
- ¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil
- ½ tsp red-pepper flakes
- 6 garlic cloves, smashed and peeled
- Lemon wedges, for serving (optional)

---

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to **425°F**.
2. **Butterfly the chicken:** Place breast-side down on a cutting board. Using sharp kitchen shears, cut along both sides of the backbone to remove it. *(Freeze backbone for stock.)* Flip breast-side up and press firmly in the center to flatten — you should hear a crack.
3. Pat chicken dry. Season both sides with salt (~½ tsp per lb). *Optional: Refrigerate uncovered up to 24 hours for crispier skin and juicier meat.*
4. Using scissors or a knife, cut tough ends off greens then cut in half crosswise. Transfer to a sheet pan with 3 tbsp olive oil, red-pepper flakes, and a generous pinch of salt. Toss and squeeze greens until coated.
5. Spread greens on the sheet pan. Pour ½ cup water over greens. Place garlic cloves in the center, then set the chicken on top of the garlic. Rub remaining 1 tbsp oil onto the chicken skin.
6. Roast until chicken is browned and thickest part of thigh reaches **165°F**, **45–50 minutes**.
7. Transfer chicken to a cutting board and rest at least **10 minutes**. Stir greens, scraping up any browned bits. Carve chicken and pour resting juices over greens. Serve with lemon if desired.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 4 days fridge / 3 months freezer. Store chicken and greens together — the juices keep the greens flavorful.
**Reheat:** Oven at 350°F for 10–12 min to keep skin crisp. Microwave works for the greens.
**Batch size:** One chicken feeds 4 — scale up by using two sheet pans
**Dry brine tip:** Salting the night before makes a noticeable difference — worth the extra planning
**Backbone note:** Save and freeze for homemade stock (pairs with the Wonton Soup recipe)
**Notes:** One of the most complete meals in the book — whole protein + vegetables on one pan, one hour, minimal cleanup. Hits every health goal. The garlic roasts under the chicken and becomes sweet and spreadable — don't skip it. Strong Sunday meal prep anchor.
