# Sheet-Pan Roast Chicken With Tangy Greens

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #high-protein #one-pan #weeknight #meal-prep
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 40 min | **Total:** ~55 min

---

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~470 | ~31g | ~32g | ~7g | ~2g | ~155mg |
**Health Score:** 6/10

---

## Ingredients

- 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for drizzling
- 6 garlic cloves
- 1 1/2 tablespoons curry powder
- 2 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more for finishing
- 1 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/4 teaspoon red-pepper flakes
- 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken leg quarters (2 1/2 to 3 pounds)
- 10 ounces chopped fresh kale leaves or other braising greens (about 6 packed cups)
- 1/2 cup pitted or unpitted green olives (optional)
- 2 tablespoons bread-and-butter pickle brine or apple cider vinegar

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to **425°F** and line a large baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a large bowl or heavy-duty resealable plastic bag, combine the olive oil, garlic, curry powder, salt, pepper, and red-pepper flakes. Add the chicken and toss well to coat. If time allows, refrigerate the chicken in the marinade overnight for deeper flavor. Add 1 more tablespoon olive oil if the chicken seems lean.
3. Transfer the chicken to the prepared baking sheet, skin-side up, and scatter the garlic among the pieces. Roast until golden brown and cooked through, **30 to 40 minutes**, turning the pan front to back about halfway through for even browning. The thickest part of the thigh should read **165°F**.
4. Transfer the chicken to a platter or four plates to rest. Add the greens, olives if using, and the brine to the baking sheet. Toss to coat in the cooking juices, then roast until wilted, bright green, and slightly crisp on the edges, **about 5 minutes**. Taste and season with more salt, if needed.
5. Serve the greens with the chicken, scraping any excess juices from the pan over the top. Finish with a light drizzle of olive oil if desired.

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 4 days refrigerated  
**Reheat:** 350°F oven for 10 to 12 minutes, or warm gently in a skillet  
**Batch size:** Scales cleanly to 8 servings with 2 sheet pans  
**Notes:** Curry powder plus the pickle brine gives this a sharp, savory-sour edge that keeps the chicken from feeling heavy. The greens soak up the pan juices, and the optional olives add a briny pop if you want more saltiness.
