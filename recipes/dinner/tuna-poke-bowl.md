# Tuna Poke Bowl

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#low-cholesterol` `#heart-healthy` `#fat-loss`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~560 | ~40g | ~20g | ~52g | ~6g | ~50mg |

**Serves:** 2
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Chill Time:** 15 min | **Total:** ~30 min

---

## Ingredients

**For the tuna marinade:**
- 1 lb sushi-grade ahi tuna, cubed (¾-inch pieces)
- 3 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp sriracha (or to taste)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, minced
- 1 scallion, thinly sliced
- ½ tsp sesame seeds

**For the bowls:**
- 1.5 cups cooked sushi rice
- 1 cucumber, julienned or cut into matchsticks
- 1 avocado, sliced
- ½ cup edamame (cooked, shelled)
- 2 tbsp pickled ginger
- 2 tbsp sesame seeds
- 2 sheets nori (seaweed), sliced into strips
- 1 tbsp fresh cilantro (optional)

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, sriracha, minced garlic, ginger, scallion, and sesame seeds.
**STEP 2:** Place cubed tuna in a medium glass bowl. Pour marinade over the tuna and gently fold to coat evenly.
**STEP 3:** Cover and refrigerate for 15 minutes. (Don't marinate longer than 30 minutes—the acid will begin to "cook" the tuna.)
**STEP 4:** While tuna marinates, prepare sushi rice and distribute between two bowls.
**STEP 5:** Arrange cucumber, avocado, and edamame around each rice bowl, leaving room in the center for the tuna.
**STEP 6:** Remove tuna from the marinade and spoon it into the center of each bowl. Drizzle with remaining marinade.
**STEP 7:** Top with pickled ginger, extra sesame seeds, nori strips, and fresh cilantro if desired.
**STEP 8:** Serve immediately with soy sauce and extra sriracha on the side for those who want more heat.

---

## Notes

- **Tuna sourcing:** Purchase tuna from reputable fishmongers who specifically label it "sushi-grade" or "sashimi-grade." Don't use regular market tuna for raw preparations—food safety is paramount.
- **Cubing technique:** Cut against the grain for more tender bites. Use a very sharp, clean knife and keep tuna cold while prepping.
- **Marinate timing:** 15 minutes is ideal—long enough for flavor, short enough to maintain raw texture. Longer marination will cure the tuna (interesting variant, but different dish).
- **Rice temperature:** Sushi rice can be served at room temperature, warm, or even chilled. Room temperature is traditional and most flavorful.
- **Avocado hack:** Slice avocado just before assembly and toss lightly with lemon juice to prevent browning.
- **Make-ahead parts:** Prepare sushi rice, vegetables, and pickled ginger up to 1 day ahead. Marinate tuna fresh just before serving to maintain texture and quality.
- **Lower-carb option:** Use cauliflower rice instead of sushi rice to drop carbs to ~28g per serving. Toss with a touch of sesame oil and rice vinegar for seasoning.
