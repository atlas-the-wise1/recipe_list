# Blackened Cod Fish Tacos

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#heart-healthy` `#low-cholesterol` `#fat-loss`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~278 | ~29g | ~5g | ~31g | ~5g | ~62mg |
**Health Score:** 7/10

**Serves:** 4 (2 tacos per person)
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 10 min | **Total:** ~25 min

---

## Ingredients

**For the blackening spice:**
- 1 tbsp paprika
- 1 tsp cayenne pepper
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- ½ tsp onion powder
- ½ tsp dried thyme
- ½ tsp dried oregano
- ¼ tsp black pepper
- ¼ tsp salt

**For the fish:**
- 1.5 lbs cod fillets (6 fillets, about 4 oz each)
- 1 tbsp olive oil

**For the lime crema:**
- ½ cup Greek yogurt (0% or 2%)
- 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- Salt and pepper to taste

**For serving:**
- 8 small corn tortillas
- 3 cups shredded purple cabbage
- 1 avocado, sliced
- 2 limes, cut into wedges
- Fresh cilantro
- Diced tomatoes (optional)

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Mix all blackening spice ingredients in a small bowl. Pat cod fillets dry with paper towels.
**STEP 2:** Sprinkle blackening spice evenly on both sides of each fillet, pressing gently so it adheres.
**STEP 3:** Prepare the lime crema: Whisk together Greek yogurt, lime juice, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Set aside.
**STEP 4:** Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, carefully place cod fillets in the pan.
**STEP 5:** Sear for 3–4 minutes per side until the spice crust is dark and fragrant and fish flakes easily with a fork. Don't move the fish around; let it develop that blackened crust.
**STEP 6:** Warm corn tortillas in a dry skillet over medium heat for about 30 seconds per side, or wrap them in foil and warm in a 350°F oven for 3–4 minutes.
**STEP 7:** Assemble tacos: Place one cod fillet piece in each tortilla. Top with shredded cabbage, avocado slice, and a dollop of lime crema.
**STEP 8:** Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with lime wedges for extra brightness.

---

## Notes

- **Cabbage slaw hack:** Toss shredded cabbage with a splash of lime juice and pinch of salt 5 minutes before serving for a quick, tangy slaw that adds crunch.
- **Fish choice flexibility:** Tilapia, mahi-mahi, or halibut work beautifully with this blackening spice; adjust cooking time slightly based on thickness (4 oz fillets cook in 5–7 minutes total).
- **Lime crema make-ahead:** Prepare this sauce up to 2 days in advance. It actually improves as flavors meld.
- **Low-cholesterol win:** Cod is naturally lean and low in cholesterol; this meal fits perfectly into heart-healthy eating patterns.
- **Tortilla swap:** Use butter lettuce cups or whole wheat tortillas for different carb levels (lettuce drops carbs to ~16g per serving).
- **Spice heat:** The cayenne is noticeable but not overwhelming; reduce to ½ tsp if you prefer milder heat. Lime juice balances and cools the spice beautifully.
- **Don't flip too early:** Let the fish develop that dark crust for 3–4 minutes undisturbed; moving it too early prevents proper searing and breaks apart delicate fillets.
