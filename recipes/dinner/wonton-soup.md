# Wonton Soup

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #heart-healthy #low-cholesterol #fat-loss
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 25 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~320 | ~18g | ~8g | ~42g | ~3g | ~55mg |
**Health Score:** 7/10

---

## Ingredients

- Salt
- 2 quarts chicken stock (preferably homemade — see below)
- 12 baby bok choy, cut in halves or quarters lengthwise
- 8 oz fresh wonton noodles (optional)
- 20 fresh or unthawed frozen wontons
- 1 scallion, thinly sliced
- Soy sauce, red vinegar, chile oil or chile crisp, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. In a separate large saucepan, bring chicken stock to a boil.
2. Add bok choy to the stock, immersing fully, then immediately reduce heat to lowest setting. Let stand until ready to serve, **5–10 minutes**. Season broth with salt to taste.
3. **If using noodles:** Cook noodles in the boiling water per package directions. Transfer to serving bowls with tongs or chopsticks. Bring water back to a boil.
4. Drop wontons into the boiling water and gently stir to prevent sticking. When water returns to a rapid boil, add 2 cups cold water and adjust heat to maintain a steady simmer. Once wontons float, continue to simmer **2 more minutes** — dough should look slightly translucent.
5. Using a spider or slotted spoon, divide wontons among serving bowls. Divide bok choy and broth among bowls.
6. Top with sliced scallion and serve immediately with soy sauce, red vinegar, chile oil or chile crisp on the side.

## Homemade Chicken Stock (Optional but Worth It)

**Full version (~40 min):**
Combine 8 oz chicken wings, 8 oz ground pork, 3 slices ginger, 2 cut-up scallions, and 7 cups water in a large pot. Add 1 oz dried Chinese mushrooms or dried scallops and 1 whole star anise if available. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then immediately reduce to a bare simmer. Simmer skimming foam for **35 minutes**. Strain and discard solids. Season to taste.

**Quick version (~15 min):**
Simmer 5 cups store-bought chicken stock with 2 ginger slices and 2 cut-up scallions for 15 minutes. Strain and use.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** Broth and bok choy 4 days fridge. Wontons best cooked fresh — store uncooked wontons in freezer up to 3 months.
**Reheat:** Broth on stovetop over medium. Re-cook wontons fresh from frozen directly in simmering broth — don't reheat already-cooked wontons.
**Batch size:** Doubles easily — scale stock and wontons proportionally
**Make it lighter:** Skip the noodles to save ~100 cal and 20g carbs per serving
**Notes:** Light, warming, and satisfying. Bok choy adds vitamins C and K plus fiber. Low cholesterol from the store-bought wontons — homemade stock cuts sodium significantly vs. store-bought. Great weeknight dinner, especially in cooler months. See `wonton-soup-healthier.md` for a version with homemade shrimp wontons, low-sodium stock, and added vegetables that earns #low-cholesterol and #heart-healthy.
