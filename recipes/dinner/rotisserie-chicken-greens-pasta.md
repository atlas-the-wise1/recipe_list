# Rotisserie Chicken and Greens Pasta

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #high-protein #heart-healthy #low-cholesterol #meal-prep
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 25 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~555 | ~56g | ~12g | ~64g | ~8g | ~105mg |
**Health Score:** 9/10

---

## Ingredients

- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 lb Banza chickpea pasta (rigatoni or penne)
- 1 small rotisserie chicken (~1¾ lbs) or ~2½ cups shredded cooked chicken
- 3 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 medium yellow onion, thinly sliced (~2 packed cups)
- 5 large garlic cloves, finely chopped or grated
- 2 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 cup chicken stock (or reserved pasta water — see tip)
- ¼ cup plain Greek yogurt (full-fat or 2%)
- ½ cup reserved pasta water (plus more as needed)
- 8–10 oz baby kale, spinach, or chopped chard
- 1 juicy lemon, zested then halved
- Parmesan, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook pasta until al dente. **Reserve at least 1 cup pasta water before draining.** Drain and set aside.
2. Prep the chicken: Separate skin from meat, then meat from bones. Discard bones. Shred meat into bite-size pieces and season with 1½ tsp pepper and salt to taste. Gather skin on cutting board, slice thinly, then finely chop. Set aside.
3. Place a Dutch oven or large heavy pot over medium heat. Add butter. Once melted and bubbling, add onions, season with salt and pepper, and cook, pressing often with a spatula, until very soft, translucent, and jammy with golden brown edges, **~10 minutes**. Stir in garlic and mustard and cook until fragrant, **~2 minutes**.
4. Raise heat to high. Add ¾ cup stock and ¼ cup pasta water. Scrape up any browned bits. Bring to a simmer, then stir in pasta, seasoned chicken, and chopped skin. Add greens in small handfuls, stirring until wilted and tender.
5. Turn off heat. Add lemon zest and squeeze lemon halves over the top. Stir in Greek yogurt quickly until fully incorporated and creamy. Add remaining ¼ cup stock or a splash more pasta water if more sauciness is desired.
6. Serve topped with grated Parmesan and extra pepper.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 4 days fridge — pasta absorbs liquid as it sits; add a splash of stock or water when reheating
**Reheat:** Stovetop over medium-low with a splash of stock or water. Microwave covered works well.
**Batch size:** Doubles easily — great for a large family or full week of lunches
**Health swaps:**
- Whole wheat or chickpea pasta adds fiber and protein
- Skip the chicken skin to reduce fat further
**Greek yogurt tip:** Always stir in off heat — adding it while the pan is still on the burner can cause it to break and curdle. Stir quickly and thoroughly.
**Notes:** Fast, deeply satisfying weeknight pasta. Greek yogurt replaces cream with more protein, less fat, and a subtle tang that plays beautifully with the Dijon and lemon. The pasta water adds silky starch-based body. Rotisserie chicken makes this a true 35-minute dinner. Already heart-healthy as written — skip the chicken skin to drop sat fat further.
