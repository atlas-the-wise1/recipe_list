# Rotisserie Chicken and Greens Pasta

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #high-protein #meal-prep
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 25 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

## Macros (per serving, estimated — with cream)
| Calories | Protein | Carbs | Fat | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~680 | ~48g | ~72g | ~24g | ~5g | ~130mg |

> **Without cream:** ~580 cal | ~48g protein | ~72g carbs | ~14g fat | ~105mg cholesterol

---

## Ingredients

- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 lb rigatoni, penne rigate, penne, or ziti
- 1 small rotisserie chicken (~1¾ lbs) or ~2½ cups shredded cooked chicken
- 3 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 medium yellow onion, thinly sliced (~2 packed cups)
- 5 large garlic cloves, finely chopped or grated
- 2 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 cup chicken stock (or reserved pasta water — see tip)
- ½ cup heavy cream (optional)
- 8–10 oz baby kale, spinach, or chopped chard
- 1 juicy lemon, zested then halved
- Parmesan, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook pasta until al dente. *(Reserve 1 cup pasta water before draining if using instead of stock.)* Drain and set aside.
2. Prep the chicken: Separate skin from meat, then meat from bones. Discard bones. Shred meat into bite-size pieces and season with 1½ tsp pepper and salt to taste. Gather skin on cutting board, slice thinly, then finely chop. Set aside.
3. Place a Dutch oven or large heavy pot over medium heat. Add butter. Once melted and bubbling, add onions, season with salt and pepper, and cook, pressing often with a spatula, until very soft, translucent, and jammy with golden brown edges, **~10 minutes**. Stir in garlic and mustard and cook until fragrant, **~2 minutes**.
4. Raise heat to high. Add ¾ cup stock and heavy cream (if using). Scrape up any browned bits. Bring to a simmer, then stir in pasta, seasoned chicken, and chopped skin. Add greens in small handfuls, stirring until wilted and tender.
5. Turn off heat. Add lemon zest and squeeze lemon halves over the top. Stir. Add remaining ¼ cup stock if more sauciness is desired.
6. Serve topped with grated Parmesan and extra pepper.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 4 days fridge — pasta absorbs liquid as it sits; add a splash of stock or water when reheating
**Reheat:** Stovetop over medium-low with a splash of stock or water. Microwave covered works well.
**Batch size:** Doubles easily — great for a large family or full week of lunches
**Health swaps:**
- Skip the cream to save ~100 cal and 10g fat per serving — barely noticeable with jammy onions providing richness
- Whole wheat or chickpea pasta adds fiber and protein
- Skip the chicken skin to reduce fat further
**Tip:** If no chicken stock on hand, reserve 1 cup pasta cooking water before draining — works perfectly as a substitute
**Notes:** Fast, deeply satisfying weeknight pasta. The jammy onions + Dijon + lemon combo builds enormous flavor without heavy cream. Rotisserie chicken makes this a true 35-minute dinner. Skipping the cream is the easiest swap for your health goals with minimal flavor sacrifice.
