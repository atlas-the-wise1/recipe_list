# Bengali-Style Mustard Oil Fish

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #high-protein #gluten-free #global #weeknight
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 15 min | **Total:** ~30 min (+ 30 min marinating)

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~360 | ~32g | ~16g | ~18g | ~1g | ~85mg |
**Health Score:** 7/10

---

## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 teaspoons black mustard seeds
- 1 fresh hot green chile (such as serrano or Thai bird's eye), finely chopped
- 2 tablespoons unsweetened grated dried coconut
- 2 tablespoons finely chopped yellow onion
- 3/4 teaspoon coarse kosher salt
- 1/4 teaspoon ground turmeric
- 1/4 teaspoon red chile powder (such as cayenne or Kashmiri)
- 3 teaspoons mustard oil, plus more if needed
- 1 1/2 pounds cod or haddock, divided into 4 fillets
- 4 pieces aluminum foil, each large enough to make a loose packet around a fish fillet
- Cooked rice, for serving

## Instructions

1. In a mortar and pestle, coarsely grind the black mustard seeds, then add the chile, coconut, and onion and grind again into a paste. Transfer to a medium bowl and mix in the salt, turmeric, red chile powder, and mustard oil until incorporated. If you do not have a mortar and pestle, combine everything in a mini food processor and pulse until it forms a paste, then stir in the mustard oil.
2. Add the fish to the bowl and toss to evenly coat with the paste. Cover and refrigerate for **30 minutes**. Meanwhile, heat the oven to **450°F**.
3. Place each fillet on one half of a piece of foil and fold the other half over the fish. Fold each side over a few times to create a pouch, then place the pouches on a baking sheet. Bake for **10 to 15 minutes**, depending on thickness, until the fish is just opaque throughout.
4. Divide the pouches among plates or shallow bowls and serve with rice. Spoon the juices from the foil over the top of the fish for extra flavor.

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 2 days refrigerated  
**Reheat:** Gently in a 300°F oven or low skillet heat to avoid drying the fish out  
**Batch size:** Works best fresh, but the mustard paste can be mixed ahead and refrigerated for 2 days  
**Notes:** Mustard oil gives the dish its signature bite, while the black mustard seeds and chile bring heat. The foil-packet method keeps the fish tender and makes cleanup easy. If you can find mustard oil labeled for cooking, it is worth using here for the most authentic flavor.
