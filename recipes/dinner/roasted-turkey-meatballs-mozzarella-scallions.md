# Roasted Turkey Meatballs With Mozzarella and Scallions

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #high-protein #heart-healthy #low-cholesterol #fat-loss #meal-prep
**Servings:** 4 (3 meatballs each)
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 17 min | **Total:** ~32 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~300 | ~32g | ~15g | ~10g | ~1g | ~110mg |
**Health Score:** 9/10

---

## Ingredients

- ½ cup crushed buttery crackers (like Ritz)
- 1 large egg
- 1½ tsp ground cumin
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 6 scallions, coarsely chopped (reserve some for garnish)
- 2 jalapeños (optional), seeded if desired, coarsely chopped (reserve some for garnish)
- 1 lb ground turkey
- 2 oz chopped mozzarella, preferably fresh (~½ cup)

---

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to **425°F**. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. In a large bowl, stir together cracker crumbs, egg, cumin, 1 tsp salt, and a few grinds of pepper until a paste forms. Stir in scallions and jalapeños (if using), reserving a little of each for garnish.
3. Add ground turkey and mix with your hands until combined. Add mozzarella and mix just until incorporated. Mixture will be loose and sticky.
4. Using wet hands, roll into **12 meatballs** (about 3 tbsp each) and place on the prepared sheet pan.
5. Bake until cooked through and bottoms are golden, **13–17 minutes**.
6. Garnish with reserved scallions and jalapeños if desired. Serve warm.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 4 days fridge / 3 months freezer — freeze on sheet pan first then transfer to a bag
**Reheat:** Oven at 375°F for 8–10 min. Air fryer at 375°F for 4–5 min for best texture. Microwave works in a pinch.
**Batch size:** Doubles easily — make 24 meatballs and freeze half for a future week
**Pairs well with:** Marinara sauce over zucchini noodles (keto), spaghetti (use with the Meat Sauce recipe base), or stuffed into a pita with Greek yogurt sauce
**Serve as:** Dinner, meal prep protein, or party appetizer
**Notes:** One of the fastest and most versatile recipes in the book — 32 minutes, one bowl, one pan. Low cholesterol, high protein, heart-healthy turkey. The mozzarella pockets inside make these far more interesting than standard meatballs. Excellent freezer staple — make a double batch and you have protein ready for two future meals with zero effort.
