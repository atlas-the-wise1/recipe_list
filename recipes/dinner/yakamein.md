# Yakamein

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#meal-prep`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~580 | ~48g | ~22g | ~48g | ~3g | ~285mg |
**Health Score:** 4/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min + 30 min marinate | **Cook Time:** 1h–1h15 | **Total:** ~1h45

---

## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
- 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 pound chuck roast, thinly sliced *(see Tip)*
- 4 tablespoons neutral oil (canola or vegetable)
- 1 cup chopped celery (from about 2 ribs)
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced
- 1 medium green bell pepper, seeded and diced
- 1½ teaspoons fine salt
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1½ teaspoons black pepper
- ¼ teaspoon cayenne powder
- ¼ teaspoon ground ginger
- 4 cups beef stock (preferably unsalted or low-sodium)
- 8 oz Banza chickpea spaghetti
- 4 hard-boiled eggs, cooled and peeled
- ½ cup sliced scallions, for serving
- Ketchup, soy sauce, or hot sauce for serving *(optional)*

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** In a medium bowl, mix together Worcestershire sauce, Cajun seasoning, and garlic. Toss beef with the marinade and let sit at least 30 minutes at room temperature.

**STEP 2:** As beef marinates, heat 3 tablespoons oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high. Add celery, onion, bell pepper, and salt. Sauté until translucent — about 5 minutes. Add onion powder, garlic powder, paprika, pepper, cayenne, and ginger; sauté another minute. Transfer vegetables to a small bowl.

**STEP 3:** Add remaining 1 tablespoon oil to the pot. Working in batches, sauté the beef over medium-high heat — about 4 minutes per side — until a nice crust forms. Add vegetables and beef stock back to the pot with the beef. Stir well and bring to a boil.

**STEP 4:** Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer uncovered, stirring occasionally, until beef is tender — 40 minutes to 1 hour. Taste and adjust seasoning.

**STEP 5:** About 10 minutes before serving, cook Banza spaghetti in a separate pot of salted boiling water per package directions. Drain.

**STEP 6:** Serve in bowls — each bowl gets noodles, broth, and beef. Garnish with halved hard-boiled eggs and chopped scallions. Add ketchup, soy sauce, or hot sauce if desired.

---

## Tip
Chuck roast cut by a butcher may have uneven strips. Watch the smaller pieces as they cook — pull them out and set aside if needed, then add back at the end to reheat before serving.

---

## Notes

- **New Orleans soul food classic** — known as "Old Sober," traditionally served as hangover cure or late-night street food; rich, spiced broth with noodles, beef, and egg
- **Low-sodium stock matters** — the Cajun seasoning + Worcestershire + soy sauce condiments add plenty of salt; start with unsalted or low-sodium stock and adjust at the end
- **Meal-prep friendly:** Broth and beef keep 4–5 days refrigerated; store noodles separately to prevent them soaking up all the broth
- **Banza swap:** Standard recipe calls for spaghetti — using Banza chickpea spaghetti adds ~4g protein and ~3g fiber per serving while keeping the dish authentic
- **Healthier swap:** Sub lean sirloin for chuck roast to drop ~80 cal and significant saturated fat; loses some richness but still great
- **Hard-boiled egg tip:** Cook eggs while beef simmers — 10 min boil, ice bath, peel when cool
- **Condiment bar:** Set out ketchup, soy sauce, and hot sauce and let everyone customize — this is traditional to the dish
