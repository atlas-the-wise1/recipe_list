# Moroccan-Spiced Chicken Meatballs

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#meal-prep`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~380 | ~29g | ~21g | ~15g | ~1g | ~120mg |
**Health Score:** 6/10
**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 15 min | **Total:** ~30 min

---

## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 cups plain whole-milk yogurt
- 1 tablespoon plus 1 teaspoon minced or grated garlic
- 2 teaspoons lemon juice
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 1 pound ground chicken, not 100 percent breast meat
- 1/2 cup panko bread crumbs
- 1 large egg, lightly beaten
- 3 tablespoons minced parsley, plus more for serving
- 1 tablespoon olive oil, plus more for frying
- 1 teaspoon light brown sugar or granulated sugar
- 1 teaspoon sweet paprika
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
- 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 3 tablespoons toasted pine nuts, optional

---

## Instructions

1. Make the yogurt sauce by combining 1 cup of the yogurt, 1 teaspoon of the garlic, the lemon juice, 1/4 teaspoon salt, and black pepper to taste in a small bowl. Mix well and chill until ready to use.
2. In a large bowl, combine the remaining 1/2 cup yogurt, remaining garlic, ground chicken, panko, egg, parsley, olive oil, sugar, paprika, cumin, ginger, cinnamon, 1 1/2 teaspoons salt, and a few grinds of black pepper. Mix gently until just combined. Shape into meatballs about 1 1/2 inches in diameter and place on a plate or sheet pan.
3. In a large skillet, heat 1/2 inch of oil over medium-high heat. Fry the meatballs in batches, turning occasionally, until browned all over and cooked through, 4 to 5 minutes. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate.
4. To serve, spread the yogurt sauce on a platter or plate. Arrange the meatballs on top and finish with chopped parsley and the pine nuts, if using.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 3 days fridge
- **Reheat:** Warm gently in a skillet or low oven to avoid drying out
- **Batch size:** Scale to 4 servings for a full dinner, or serve with rice and vegetables for a bigger meal

---
