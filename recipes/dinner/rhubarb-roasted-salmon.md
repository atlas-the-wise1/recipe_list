# Rhubarb Roasted Salmon

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#heart-healthy` `#high-protein` `#quick`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~390 | ~33g | ~21g | ~13g | ~2g | ~90mg |
**Health Score:** 7/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 15 min | **Total:** ~30 min

---

## Ingredients

- 3 scallions, thinly sliced
- 2 tablespoons granulated sugar, more to taste
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar, more for serving
- 1/2 teaspoon grated fresh ginger, more to taste
- Salt
- 6 ounces rhubarb, trimmed and sliced 1-inch-thick (about 1 1/3 cups)
- 4 6- to 8-ounce skin-on salmon fillets
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, cubed
- Red-pepper flakes, for serving, optional

---

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to 350 F. Line a rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper, if you like, for easier cleanup.
2. Set aside about 2 tablespoons of the scallion greens for serving. In a medium saucepan, combine the remaining scallions, sugar, vinegar, ginger, and a pinch of salt. Bring to a simmer over medium heat and simmer until the sugar has dissolved.
3. Add the rhubarb. Cover the pan, and cook, stirring occasionally, until the rhubarb is just tender, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat and, using a spoon or fork, mash the mixture until it falls apart into a chunky puree. Taste and add more sugar, vinegar, ginger, and salt if needed. It should taste balanced between sweet, tangy, and salty.
4. Arrange salmon skin-side down on the prepared baking sheet. Season lightly with salt and pepper. Spread half of the rhubarb mixture on top of the fillets, saving the remaining rhubarb for serving. Top fillets evenly with cubed butter. Roast for 8 to 13 minutes, or until the salmon is just cooked through.
5. Garnish the fillets with the reserved scallions, red-pepper flakes, if you like, and a drizzle of rice wine vinegar. Serve with the remaining rhubarb sauce.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 2 days refrigerated
- **Reheat:** Warm gently at low oven heat or in a skillet so the salmon stays tender
- **Batch size:** Best as a fresh dinner, but the rhubarb sauce can be made ahead and held separately

---

## Notes

- The rhubarb gives the salmon a sweet-tart, savory glaze that feels bright and seasonal.
- Butter keeps it richer than a lean baked fish dinner, but the salmon still makes it a strong protein-forward option.
- If you want it lighter later, reduce the butter slightly and serve with extra vegetables instead of rice.
