# Crispy Mushroom Tacos

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #heart-healthy #low-cholesterol #meal-prep
**Servings:** 4 (3 tacos each)
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 30 min | **Total:** ~45 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~510 | ~16g | ~25g | ~57g | ~14g | ~0mg |

---

## Ingredients

### Pico de Gallo
- 1 large beefsteak tomato, cored and cut into ¼-inch dice
- 1 large ripe Hass avocado, pitted, peeled and cut into ¼-inch dice
- 2 jalapeños, stemmed and finely chopped (seeded if desired)
- ½ cup cilantro leaves, finely chopped
- 3 tbsp fresh lime juice (from 1–2 limes)
- Coarse kosher salt

### Mushroom Tacos
- 18 oz oyster mushrooms (from 2–3 medium clusters)
- 1 (15 oz) can black beans, drained and rinsed
- ½ cup neutral oil (canola or similar)
- ¼ tsp ground cumin
- 1½ tsp coarse kosher salt
- Black pepper
- 12 corn tortillas

---

## Instructions

1. **Make the pico de gallo:** Combine tomato, avocado, jalapeños, cilantro, and lime juice in a bowl. Season with salt and gently fold together. Set aside to let flavors marry while mushrooms cook.
2. **Cook the mushrooms:** Tear oyster mushrooms into bite-size pieces, leaving some tops whole. Cook in two batches to ensure crispiness:
   - Heat a large cast-iron or heavy skillet over medium-high. Coat with half the oil.
   - Once hot, add half the mushrooms in a single layer without crowding. Cook, stirring every few minutes, until deep golden brown and crisp, **10–15 minutes**. Transfer to a plate.
   - Repeat with remaining oil and mushrooms.
   - Return first batch to skillet. Add cumin, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
   - Add black beans to the skillet and stir to combine, heating through for **1–2 minutes**. Remove from heat.
3. **Warm the tortillas:** Heat on a comal or heavy skillet over medium-high until soft and pliable. Wrap in a dish towel to keep warm.
4. Divide mushrooms and pico de gallo among warm tortillas. Serve immediately.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** Mushrooms 3 days fridge. Pico de gallo 2 days fridge (avocado browns — press plastic wrap directly on surface). Tortillas store separately.
**Reheat:** Mushrooms in a dry cast-iron skillet over high heat 2–3 min to re-crisp. Avoid microwave.
**Batch size:** Scales easily — cook mushrooms in more batches, double the pico.
**Pairs well with:** A side salad or extra guacamole
**Notes:** Zero cholesterol, very high fiber (14g), fully plant-based. Black beans added directly to the skillet with the mushrooms — doubles the protein and adds a satisfying heartiness. The two-batch method for mushrooms is non-negotiable for crispiness — don't skip it.
