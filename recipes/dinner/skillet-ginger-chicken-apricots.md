# Skillet Ginger Chicken With Apricots

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #heart-healthy #high-protein #low-cholesterol #fat-loss
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 25 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~380 | ~38g | ~12g | ~28g | ~3g | ~110mg |
**Health Score:** 7/10

---

## Ingredients

- 1 tbsp + 1 tsp grated or minced fresh ginger (unpeeled)
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- Kosher salt
- 1½ lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 2-inch pieces
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 small red onion, halved and thinly sliced
- 1 cup dried Turkish apricots, halved
- ¼ cup white wine
- 2–3 packed cups baby spinach
- 2 packed tbsp fresh mint or cilantro leaves

---

## Instructions

1. In a medium bowl, combine 1 tbsp ginger with cumin, coriander, and 1 tsp salt. Pat chicken dry, add to bowl, and toss to coat.
2. Heat oil in a 12-inch skillet over medium-high until shimmering. Add onion, season with salt, and cook stirring occasionally until just softened, **3–4 minutes**. Push onion to the sides.
3. Add chicken so each piece contacts the pan (crowding is OK). Cook undisturbed until bottom is browned and releases easily, **7–8 minutes**. Flip chicken and scatter apricots around the skillet.
4. Cook until the other side of chicken is no longer pink, **~2 minutes**. Pour in wine and remaining 1 tsp ginger. Stir and scrape up any browned bits. Continue stirring to coat everything in the liquid until mostly absorbed with a little pooling at the bottom, chicken is cooked through, and apricots are softened, **~3 minutes**.
5. Add spinach and mix until wilted, **~2 minutes**. Season with salt.
6. Sprinkle with mint or cilantro and serve.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 4 days fridge / 2 months freezer
**Reheat:** Skillet over medium-low with a splash of water or chicken broth. Microwave works well covered.
**Batch size:** Doubles easily in a larger skillet or two pans simultaneously
**Pairs well with:** Couscous, brown rice, or cauliflower rice (keto)
**Notes:** One of the most well-rounded recipes in the book — high protein, heart-healthy, low-fat, done in 35 minutes. The apricots add natural sweetness that balances the ginger and cumin beautifully. Spinach wilts directly into the pan for a built-in vegetable. Strong weekly staple candidate.
