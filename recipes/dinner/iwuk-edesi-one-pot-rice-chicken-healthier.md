# Iwuk Edesi (One-Pot Rice With Chicken) — Healthier Variant

> 💡 **Healthier Variant** of Iwuk Edesi (One-Pot Rice With Chicken) — see `iwuk-edesi-one-pot-rice-chicken.md` for the classic version.

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #high-protein #heart-healthy #low-cholesterol #meal-prep
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 20 min | **Cook Time:** 45 min | **Total:** ~65 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~510 | ~50g | ~14g | ~48g | ~4g | ~90mg |
**Health Score:** 8/10

**What changed vs. classic:** ~170 fewer calories, ~16g less fat, ~55mg less cholesterol per serving. Achieves this by:
- Using **boneless, skinless chicken thighs** instead of skin-on (eliminates chicken skin fat and cholesterol)
- **Reducing red palm oil** from ½ cup to 3 tbsp — preserves the authentic flavor and color while cutting ~280 calories across the dish
- Using **2 tbsp neutral oil** for searing instead of ¼ cup (just enough to sear)
- Boosting **greens volume** from 4 cups to 6 cups for more fiber and micronutrients

---

## Ingredients

### Pepper Paste
- 1–2 yellow or red Scotch bonnet chiles or red habanero chiles
- 1 tbsp whole crayfish or dried shrimp, soaked in warm water 10 min then drained (or ½ tbsp ground crayfish)
- ¼ tsp fresh or dried irú, soaked in warm water 10 min and drained (or substitute fish sauce or fermented black beans)

### Chicken and Rice
- 2 tbsp neutral oil (canola or grapeseed)
- 3 lbs **boneless, skinless** chicken thighs
- Fine salt, to taste
- 1 tbsp grated ginger
- 2 garlic cloves, smashed
- **3 tbsp red palm oil** (reduced from ½ cup)
- 2 cups long-grain white rice
- 2¼ cups low-sodium chicken stock
- **6 cups** torn hearty greens — ugwu, mature spinach, collards, or kale (increased for more fiber)
- ½ small red onion, thinly sliced

---

## Instructions

1. **Make the pepper paste:** Using a mortar and pestle (or food processor), pound Scotch bonnet chiles and crayfish to a coarse paste. Add irú and pound until smooth.
2. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat neutral oil over medium until shimmering. Pat chicken dry and season generously with salt on both sides. Working in batches if needed, sear chicken on both sides until deep golden brown, **~10 minutes total**. (Skinless chicken won't take as long as skin-on — watch carefully.) Transfer to a plate.
3. Drain all but 1 tbsp oil from the pot. Add ginger and garlic; stir until fragrant. Stir in palm oil and pepper paste, then add rice. Stir to coat every grain in the seasoned oil. Add stock and scrape the bottom to loosen any stuck bits. Season with salt and bring to a simmer.
4. Return chicken to the pot along with any resting juices. Reduce heat to low. Cover and cook until liquid is absorbed, rice is tender, and chicken is cooked through, **18–20 minutes**. Stir in greens and allow to wilt, **~2–3 minutes**. Remove from heat and let sit covered **10 more minutes**. Fluff rice gently with a fork.
5. Divide rice and chicken among plates, sprinkle with sliced red onion, and serve warm.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 4 days fridge / 2 months freezer
**Reheat:** Stovetop over medium-low with a splash of stock or water. Microwave covered works well.
**Batch size:** Doubles well in a larger Dutch oven
**Notes:** Skinless chicken thighs are the single biggest health lever — they're still incredibly juicy and absorb the pepper paste and palm oil flavors beautifully. The reduced palm oil still delivers the signature golden-orange color and earthy richness that defines this dish. Increasing greens to 6 cups bumps fiber to ~4g and adds iron and vitamins without changing the eating experience.
