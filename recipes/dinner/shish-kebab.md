# Shish Kebab

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#meal-prep` `#heart-healthy`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~470 | ~45g | ~31g | ~4g | ~0g | ~110mg |
**Health Score:** 6/10
**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 20 min | **Cook Time:** 12-15 min | **Total:** ~2 hr 35 min to 24 hr 35 min including marinating

---

## Ingredients

- 1/4 cup plain yogurt
- 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, plus extra for serving
- 2 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 1 tablespoon kosher salt, such as Diamond Crystal
- 1 teaspoon Aleppo pepper, or crushed red pepper of choice, plus extra for serving
- 1 teaspoon sweet or smoked paprika
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano, crushed between your fingers
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 2 pounds boneless lamb leg or shoulder, beef top loin or sirloin, or a combination, excess fat trimmed and meat cut into 1 1/2- to 2-inch cubes
- Lemon or lime wedges
- Flatbread, such as pita, for serving
- Grilled bell pepper and onion, optional, for serving
- Fresh herbs, such as mint and basil, optional, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. In a medium bowl, combine the yogurt, olive oil, garlic, tomato paste, salt, Aleppo pepper, paprika, cumin, oregano, and black pepper. Add the meat and toss well to coat. Cover and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours and up to 24 hours.
2. Remove the meat from the fridge 30 minutes before grilling. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes. Heat a gas grill to medium-high, about 400 F, or prepare a hot charcoal grill and oil the grates. Thread the meat onto skewers and grill, turning a few times, until cooked to your liking, about 12 minutes for medium-rare to 15 minutes for medium on a gas grill, or 10 to 12 minutes on a charcoal grill. Grill vegetables alongside, if using, and warm the bread on the grill just before serving.
3. Place the bread on a serving platter, set the kebabs on top, squeeze lemon or lime juice over the meat, drizzle with a little olive oil, and add an extra sprinkling of Aleppo pepper if you want. Serve with fresh herbs.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 3 days fridge
- **Reheat:** Warm gently in a skillet or low oven so the meat stays tender
- **Batch size:** Scale to 4 servings for a full dinner, or stretch with more vegetables and bread for a larger crowd

---

## Notes

- Rich, high-protein grilled dinner with solid meal-prep potential if you batch the marinade and skewer work ahead of time.
- Health score stays moderate because the recipe uses red meat, olive oil, and salty seasoning, but the yogurt marinade and optional vegetables help balance it.
- Best version for wedding-diet use: leaner cut of lamb leg or sirloin, extra grilled vegetables, and a lighter hand with the oil and bread.
