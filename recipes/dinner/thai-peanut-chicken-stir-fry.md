# Thai Peanut Chicken Stir Fry

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#meal-prep` `#heart-healthy`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~440 | ~38g | ~18g | ~32g | ~5g | ~85mg |

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 15 min | **Total:** ~30 min

---

## Ingredients

**For the chicken & vegetables:**
- 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized strips
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 red bell peppers, sliced thin
- 2 cups snap peas
- 1 cup shredded carrots
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
- Salt and pepper to taste

**For the peanut sauce:**
- ⅓ cup natural peanut butter (or almond butter)
- 3 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
- 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
- 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- ¼ tsp red pepper flakes
- 3 tbsp warm water

**For serving:**
- 2 cups cooked brown rice OR 4 cups cauliflower rice
- ¼ cup chopped roasted peanuts (optional)
- 2 scallions, sliced
- Fresh cilantro for garnish

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** In a small bowl, whisk together peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, honey, sesame oil, red pepper flakes, and warm water until smooth. Set sauce aside.
**STEP 2:** Heat 1 tbsp of olive oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Season chicken with salt and pepper.
**STEP 3:** Stir-fry chicken until golden and cooked through, about 8–10 minutes. Transfer to a plate.
**STEP 4:** Add remaining olive oil to the skillet. Add garlic and ginger, stirring constantly for 30 seconds until fragrant.
**STEP 5:** Add bell peppers, snap peas, and carrots. Stir-fry for 4–5 minutes until vegetables are tender-crisp but still vibrant.
**STEP 6:** Return chicken to the skillet. Pour peanut sauce over everything and toss until well coated. Simmer for 2–3 minutes to allow flavors to meld.
**STEP 7:** Taste and adjust seasoning (add lime juice for brightness, soy sauce for saltiness, or honey for sweetness).
**STEP 8:** Divide rice or cauliflower rice among four bowls. Top with stir-fry mixture.
**STEP 9:** Garnish with chopped peanuts, sliced scallions, and fresh cilantro. Serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- **Meal-prep superstar:** This dish stores beautifully for 4 days in the fridge. Pack stir-fry and rice separately; combine and reheat gently before serving.
- **Cauliflower rice hack:** Using cauliflower rice instead of brown rice cuts carbs to ~18g per serving while keeping protein and fiber high—perfect for keto-curious eating.
- **Nut butter swap:** Almond butter works just as well as peanut butter and offers a slightly lighter flavor profile.
- **Vegetable flexibility:** Substitute broccoli, bok choy, or green beans for any of the vegetables listed; keep the ratio roughly 3 cups veggies per serving.
- **Sauce texture:** If sauce seems too thick, thin with 1 tbsp water at a time. If too thin, add 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with water and simmer briefly.
- **Speed tip:** Buy pre-cut vegetables from the produce section to save 10 minutes of prep time without sacrificing quality.
- **Heat level:** Red pepper flakes can be adjusted; start with ¼ tsp and add more to taste. Serve with extra lime wedges for those who want brightness.
