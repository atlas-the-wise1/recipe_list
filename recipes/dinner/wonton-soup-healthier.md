# Wonton Soup — Healthier Variant

> 💡 **Healthier Variant** of Wonton Soup — see `wonton-soup.md` for the classic version.

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #heart-healthy #low-cholesterol #fat-loss #meal-prep
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 20 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~40 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~240 | ~22g | ~6g | ~22g | ~4g | ~75mg |
**Health Score:** 8/10

**What changed vs. classic:** ~80 fewer calories, significantly less sodium, more fiber, more protein. Achieves this by:
- Using **homemade shrimp wontons** instead of store-bought (controls sodium and fat — store-bought wontons average 700–900mg sodium/serving)
- **Skipping the noodles** (saves ~100 cal and 20g carbs per serving)
- Using **homemade or low-sodium stock** (store-bought cuts sodium to ~400mg/serving vs. 1,000mg+)
- Adding **extra vegetables** (snap peas, shiitake mushrooms) alongside bok choy for more fiber and volume

---

## Ingredients

### Homemade Shrimp Wontons (~20 wontons)
- 8 oz medium shrimp, peeled and deveined, roughly chopped (not pureed — keep texture)
- 2 tbsp finely minced scallions
- 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
- 1 tsp low-sodium soy sauce
- ½ tsp sesame oil
- ¼ tsp white pepper
- 20 wonton wrappers

### Soup
- 2 quarts **homemade or low-sodium chicken stock** (see original recipe for quick homemade version)
- 12 baby bok choy, halved or quartered lengthwise
- 1 cup snap peas, strings removed
- 4 dried shiitake mushrooms, soaked in hot water 20 min, stems removed, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp low-sodium soy sauce (to season broth)
- 1 scallion, thinly sliced

### Condiments (optional)
- Red vinegar, chile oil, sesame oil — serve on the side, use sparingly

---

## Instructions

1. **Make the wontons:** Combine shrimp, scallions, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, and white pepper. Mix well. Place a wonton wrapper on a clean surface, add ~1 tsp filling to the center. Dampen edges with water, fold into a triangle, pressing to seal. Bring the two base corners together and press to seal (classic nurse-cap shape). Place on a parchment-lined sheet. *(Freeze extras for up to 3 months.)*
2. In a large saucepan, bring low-sodium stock to a boil. Add shiitake mushrooms and simmer 5 minutes. Add bok choy and snap peas; reduce to lowest setting and let stand 3–5 minutes until just tender. Season broth with soy sauce and salt if needed.
3. Bring a separate large pot of water to a boil. Drop wontons in gently. When water returns to a boil, add 1 cup cold water. Once wontons float, simmer **2 more minutes** until cooked through.
4. Using a spider or slotted spoon, divide wontons among serving bowls. Ladle hot broth with vegetables over top. Garnish with sliced scallion.
5. Serve immediately with condiments on the side.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** Broth + vegetables 4 days fridge. Freeze uncooked wontons on a sheet pan, then transfer to a bag — cook directly from frozen (add 1–2 min to cook time).
**Reheat:** Broth on stovetop over medium. Always cook wontons fresh — do not reheat cooked wontons in broth.
**Batch size:** Make a double batch of wontons and freeze — the soup comes together in 20 minutes once wontons are ready.
**Sodium note:** Low-sodium stock + homemade wontons brings sodium down to ~550mg/serving vs. ~1,400mg+ with store-bought components. A significant improvement for heart health.
**Notes:** Homemade shrimp wontons are easier than they look — 20 wontons take about 15 minutes once you have a rhythm. The filling is simple (shrimp, scallion, ginger) and deeply flavorful. Adding shiitake mushrooms to the broth amplifies umami without adding sodium.
