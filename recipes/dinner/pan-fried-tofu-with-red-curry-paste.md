# Pan-Fried Tofu With Red Curry Paste

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#vegetarian` `#quick`
**Nutrition (per serving):**

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 15 min | **Total:** ~30 min

## Ingredients

- 1 pound extra-firm tofu
- 2 tablespoons canola oil
- 2 tablespoons store-bought or homemade red curry paste
- 4 small baby eggplants, stemmed and quartered
- 1 medium red or white potato, peeled and cut into 1-inch cubes
- 1 small red bell pepper, stemmed, seeded and cut into 1-inch cubes
- 1/2 cup sliced bamboo shoots, drained if using canned
- 1 (14-ounce) can unsweetened coconut milk
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce or soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh Thai or sweet basil
- 3 cups cooked jasmine rice
- 1 cup uncooked rice

## Instructions

1. Press the tofu between paper towels, then cut into 1-inch cubes.
2. Heat the oil in a Dutch oven or large saucepan over medium heat. Add the tofu cubes and stir-fry until they turn light brown along the sides; transfer to a plate.
3. Add the curry paste to the same oil and stir-fry until fragrant, 1 to 2 minutes.
4. Add the eggplant, potato, bell pepper and bamboo shoots. Shake the coconut milk, pour it over the vegetables and scrape the bottom of the pot.
5. Add the tofu and fish sauce, then bring the curry to a boil. Cover and cook, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes and eggplant are fork-tender, 10 to 12 minutes. Uncover for the last few minutes to thicken slightly.
6. Serve sprinkled with basil alongside jasmine rice.

## Notes

- Let the curry paste fry briefly so it blooms before the coconut milk goes in
- Baby eggplants hold their shape well, but any small eggplant works
- Sweet basil is fine if you can’t find Thai basil
