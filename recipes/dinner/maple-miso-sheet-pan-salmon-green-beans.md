# Maple and Miso Sheet-Pan Salmon With Green Beans

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #heart-healthy #high-protein #low-cholesterol #keto
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 12 min | **Total:** ~25 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~420 | ~42g | ~22g | ~12g | ~3g | ~95mg |
**Health Score:** 8/10

> **With white rice (1 cup cooked):** ~620 cal | ~46g protein | ~52g carbs | ~22g fat

---

## Ingredients

- 4 (6 oz) skin-on salmon fillets, ~1-inch thick
- Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 4 tsp maple syrup
- 1 tbsp white or brown miso
- 1 tbsp rice wine vinegar
- 2 tsp soy sauce
- 1 garlic clove, grated
- 1 lb green beans, trimmed
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Pinch of red-pepper flakes (optional)
- ¼ tsp toasted sesame oil (optional)
- ¼ cup roughly chopped cilantro (leaves and tender stems)
- 4 lime wedges, for serving
- Flaky sea salt, for serving (optional)
- Cooked white rice, for serving (optional)

---

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to **400°F**. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper or foil.
2. Season salmon fillets well with salt and pepper. Place on a plate or in a shallow bowl.
3. In a small bowl, whisk together maple syrup, miso, rice wine vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic. Pour over salmon and gently massage marinade all over the fish. Let marinate while oven comes to temperature.
4. In a medium bowl, toss green beans with olive oil, red-pepper flakes, and sesame oil (if using). Season generously with salt and pepper.
5. Arrange salmon skin-side down on the sheet pan and spread green beans out around the fillets.
6. Bake until salmon is opaque and green beans are cooked through, **~12 minutes**.
7. Scatter cilantro over the top, squeeze lime over everything, and finish with flaky sea salt. Serve with rice if desired.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 3 days fridge. Green beans hold better than the salmon — store separately if possible.
**Reheat:** Oven at 300°F for 6–8 min to avoid drying out the salmon. Air fryer at 300°F for 4 min works great.
**Batch size:** Scales easily — use a larger sheet pan or two pans for 6–8 fillets
**Make it keto:** Skip the rice and serve over cauliflower rice — maple syrup adds minimal carbs per serving
**Notes:** One of the fastest sheet-pan dinners in the book at 25 minutes total. The maple-miso glaze is exceptional — sweet, savory, umami-forward. Salmon's omega-3s make this a standout heart-healthy option. Pairs beautifully with the Air-Fryer Salmon as a weekly rotation to keep salmon interesting without repetition.
