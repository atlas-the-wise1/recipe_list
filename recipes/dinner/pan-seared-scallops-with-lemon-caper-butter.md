# Pan-Seared Scallops with Lemon Caper Butter

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#low-cholesterol` `#heart-healthy` `#keto`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~320 | ~30g | ~16g | ~8g | ~2g | ~40mg |

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 8 min | **Total:** ~18 min

---

## Ingredients

**For the scallops:**
- 1.25 lbs large sea scallops (U/10 or U/15 count)
- 2 tbsp butter, divided
- Salt and white pepper to taste
- 1 tbsp olive oil

**For the lemon caper butter sauce:**
- 2 tbsp butter
- 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 2 tbsp capers (drained and rinsed)
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- ¼ tsp red pepper flakes
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- Salt and white pepper to taste

**For serving:**
- Cauliflower purée or risotto (see notes for recipe ideas)
- Lemon wedges
- Extra parsley for garnish

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Pat scallops thoroughly dry with paper towels. This is crucial for achieving a golden crust. Season lightly with salt and white pepper on both sides.
**STEP 2:** Heat 1 tbsp butter and 1 tbsp olive oil in a large cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat. The pan should be very hot but not smoking.
**STEP 3:** Carefully place scallops in the skillet, leaving space between each one. Don't crowd the pan.
**STEP 4:** Let scallops cook undisturbed for 2–3 minutes until a golden crust forms on the bottom. Flip gently and cook for another 1–2 minutes on the other side until just opaque in the center.
**STEP 5:** Transfer scallops to a warm plate. Reduce heat to medium.
**STEP 6:** Add 2 tbsp butter to the skillet. Once melted, add minced garlic and cook for 20 seconds until fragrant.
**STEP 7:** Add capers, red pepper flakes, and fresh lemon juice. Swirl until combined, about 30 seconds.
**STEP 8:** Return scallops to the skillet (or spoon sauce directly over plated scallops). Add fresh parsley. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and white pepper.
**STEP 9:** Serve immediately over cauliflower purée or risotto with lemon wedges on the side.

---

## Notes

- **Cauliflower purée recipe:** Steam 4 cups cauliflower florets until very tender (12–15 minutes). Blend with 2 tbsp butter, 2 tbsp Greek yogurt or cream, salt, and white pepper until silky. This pairs perfectly and keeps the meal keto-friendly (~8g carbs).
- **Scallop quality:** Look for "dry" scallops (not treated with sodium tripolyphosphate/STP), which sear better and have superior texture. Ask your fishmonger.
- **Don't overcook:** Scallops go from tender to rubbery in seconds. Aim for just opaque in the center—an instant-read thermometer should read 120–125°F.
- **Keto-approved:** This entire dish (with cauliflower purée) is naturally low-carb and fits perfectly into ketogenic eating patterns.
- **Cast iron essential:** A well-seasoned cast-iron skillet provides the dry heat needed for a proper golden crust. Don't use non-stick for this—it prevents good browning.
- **Make-ahead:** Scallops must be cooked fresh, but you can prepare the lemon caper sauce up to 1 day ahead. Reheat gently just before serving.
- **Risotto pairing:** For a more indulgent option, serve over creamy risotto made with white wine, Parmesan, and butter—still beautiful and restaurant-worthy.
