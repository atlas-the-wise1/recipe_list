# Gingery Meatballs in Tomato Sauce

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#meal-prep`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~400 | ~25g | ~24g | ~14g | ~2g | ~70mg |
**Health Score:** 6/10
**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons finely grated or minced fresh ginger
- 3 garlic cloves, finely grated or minced
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin, more for serving
- 1 teaspoon fine sea or table salt, plus more as needed
- 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
- 1 pound ground pork, or turkey, chicken, beef, lamb, or vegan meat
- 1/2 cup panko bread crumbs, or plain breadcrumbs
- 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh cilantro leaves and tender stems, more for serving
- 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 cups diced fresh ripe or overripe tomatoes
- 1 tablespoon fish sauce or soy sauce
- 4 scallions, thinly sliced
- 1 lime, halved

---

## Instructions

1. In a large bowl, mix together 1 tablespoon of the ginger, the garlic, cumin, salt, and coriander. Add the pork, panko, and cilantro. Using your hands, gently mix everything together without overworking it, then form into 1 1/4-inch balls.
2. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat, then add the olive oil and let it warm until it thins out. Add the meatballs in one layer and cook, turning and shaking the pan, until browned all over, 5 to 7 minutes.
3. Move the meatballs to one side of the pan, scraping up any browned bits. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon ginger to the empty side and sauté for 1 minute. Add the tomatoes, fish sauce or soy sauce, and a pinch of salt to the empty side of the pan. Once the tomatoes are simmering, cover the pan, lower the heat to medium, and cook until the meatballs are no longer pink in the center, about 5 to 8 minutes longer.
4. Uncover the pan. Mix the scallions into the sauce, squeeze lime juice over everything, and stir together. Taste and add more salt or lime juice as needed. Serve the meatballs sprinkled with more cumin and topped with cilantro.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 3 days fridge
- **Reheat:** Warm gently in a skillet or low oven so the meatballs stay tender
- **Batch size:** Scale to 4 servings for dinner, or pair with rice and greens to stretch it further
