# One-Pot Mushroom and Ginger Rice

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#vegetarian` `#one-pot` `#meal-prep`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~310 | ~8g | ~10g | ~48g | ~3g | ~0mg |
**Health Score:** 7/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~30 min

---

## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 cups medium-grain white rice
- 2 cups vegetable stock, store-bought or homemade
- 10 ounces mushrooms, such as shiitake, cremini, button, or blue oysters (or a mix), halved or quartered
- 2 teaspoons cornstarch
- 6 to 7 teaspoons tamari soy sauce
- 2 teaspoons Shaoxing wine, optional
- 3 teaspoons sesame oil
- Salt and white or black pepper
- 1 (1-inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and thinly sliced
- 2 scallions, thinly sliced
- Toasted white sesame seeds, optional

---

## Instructions

1. Place the rice into a Dutch oven and rinse it 3 times until the water runs almost clear. Pour the stock over the rice and leave to soak for 10 minutes while you prepare the mushrooms.
2. Place the mushrooms into a bowl and add the cornstarch and toss to coat. Add 4 teaspoons soy sauce, the Shaoxing wine, 1 teaspoon sesame oil, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and a pinch of white or black pepper; toss to coat.
3. Place the pot with the rice over medium-high heat, add the ginger slices, and bring to a boil. When bubbling, cover, reduce to low, and cook for 5 minutes.
4. Uncover, add the mushrooms, and use chopsticks or a large spoon to stir the mushrooms through the rice. Cover again and cook on low until the mushrooms are cooked and rice is tender, 10 to 12 minutes.
5. Remove the lid, increase heat to medium-high, and cook for 4 to 5 minutes, until you can hear it sizzling aggressively, which indicates the bottom of the rice is becoming crispy. Skip this step if you don’t care for the crispy rice.
6. Turn off heat and drizzle with 2 to 3 teaspoons soy sauce, the remaining 2 teaspoons sesame oil, and top with scallions and sesame seeds, if using.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 3 days refrigerated
- **Reheat:** Warm in a covered skillet or microwave with a splash of stock or water
- **Batch size:** Best as a fresh one-pot dinner, but leftovers hold up well for lunch

---

## Notes

- This is a simple, satisfying rice dish with a lot of savory mushroom depth and a little ginger lift.
- The crispy-bottom step gives it extra texture, but it still works if you prefer a softer pilaf.
- If you want it lighter later, reduce the sesame oil slightly and add more mushrooms.
