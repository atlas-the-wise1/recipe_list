# One-Pan Salmon Niçoise With Orzo

**Category:** D — Dinner
**Tags:** `#heart-healthy` `#low-cholesterol` `#high-protein` `#fat-loss`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~480 | ~38g | ~18g | ~42g | ~4g | ~80mg |
**Health Score:** 8/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 25 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

## Ingredients

- 1 large shallot, coarsely chopped
- 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 cup orzo
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- ½ cup pitted kalamata olives
- 1 pint cherry or other small tomatoes, halved (8–10 oz)
- 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- Handful of basil leaves or pinch of thyme leaves *(optional)*
- 4 (4–5 oz) salmon fillets, skin-on or skinless
- 8 oz green beans, trimmed and cut into 1-inch lengths

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Transfer 1 tablespoon of the chopped shallot to a medium bowl; add the remaining shallot to a large (12-inch) skillet. Add 1 tablespoon oil to the skillet and heat over medium-high. When sizzling, add orzo, season with ½ teaspoon each salt and pepper, and stir constantly until light golden — 2 to 4 minutes.

**STEP 2:** Add olives and 2¼ cups water; bring to a boil, then cover the skillet with a lid, baking sheet, or foil. Reduce heat to medium-low and cook per package directions until orzo is al dente.

**STEP 3:** Meanwhile, add tomatoes, red wine vinegar, mustard, herbs (if using), and remaining 1 tablespoon oil to the reserved shallot in the bowl. Season with salt and pepper; set aside. Season salmon with salt and pepper.

**STEP 4:** When orzo is al dente, add green beans and stir to combine. If orzo looks dry, add ½ cup water — you want it wet but not soupy. Nestle salmon fillets in a single layer into the orzo, skin-side up if applicable. Cover and cook until salmon and orzo are cooked through — 6 to 8 minutes. Remove from heat and let sit covered for 2 minutes. If skin-on, peel off and discard. Top with the tomato vinaigrette.

---

## Notes

- **True one-pan, 35 minutes** — orzo toasted in the same skillet, salmon nestled in to finish; minimal cleanup
- **Toast the orzo** — stirring until golden before adding water adds a nutty depth that makes this taste much more complex than the effort suggests
- **Wet but not soupy** — check orzo before adding salmon; add that ½ cup water if needed, the salmon releases liquid and you want the orzo to absorb it without drying out
- **Sit covered 2 minutes** — don't skip this; carryover heat finishes the salmon gently and lets the orzo absorb remaining liquid
- **Tomato vinaigrette is served raw/fresh** — don't cook it; the contrast of warm orzo + salmon against the bright cold vinaigrette is the whole point
- **Meal-prep note:** Best eaten same day — orzo absorbs liquid overnight; if reheating, add a splash of water and reheat gently
- **Boost it:** Add a handful of arugula under the tomato vinaigrette for peppery freshness; or top with a soft-boiled egg per person to push protein over 45g
