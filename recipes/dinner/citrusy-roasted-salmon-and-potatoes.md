# Citrusy Roasted Salmon and Potatoes

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#heart-healthy` `#low-cholesterol` `#high-protein` `#fat-loss`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~710 | ~36g | ~49g | ~29g | ~4g | ~95mg |
**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 30-40 min | **Total:** ~45-55 min

---

## Ingredients

- 1/2 cup extra-virgin olive oil
- 1/3 cup fresh clementine or mandarin juice
- 1 clementine or mandarin, sliced into 1/4-inch-thick rounds, for garnish
- 1/4 cup fresh lime juice
- 1/4 cup chopped cilantro leaves and tender stems
- 1 shallot, chopped
- 2 large garlic cloves, finely grated
- 2 large Yukon Gold potatoes (about 1 pound)
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- Red-pepper flakes, to taste
- 1 1/2 pounds skin-on salmon fillet (preferably 1 single center-cut piece)

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Set a rack in the center of the oven and heat the oven to 425 F.

**STEP 2:** In a medium bowl, combine the olive oil, clementine or mandarin juice, lime juice, cilantro, shallot, and garlic. Set aside as the citrus sauce.

**STEP 3:** Scrub the potatoes and slice them into 1/4-inch rounds. In a large 12-inch cast-iron skillet or other oven-safe pan, fan out the potato rounds, overlapping slightly to cover the surface. Season well with salt, black pepper, and red-pepper flakes. Stir the citrus sauce again, then spoon half over the potatoes. Roast for 20 minutes, until the edges start to turn golden.

**STEP 4:** Remove the skillet from the oven. Season the salmon well with salt and black pepper, then place it on top of the potatoes, skin-side down. Sprinkle with more red-pepper flakes. Spoon the remaining citrus sauce over the salmon and top with the citrus slices. Return the skillet to the oven and roast until the salmon is cooked through, 10 to 20 minutes depending on thickness.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 2 days fridge
- **Reheat:** Cover and warm gently at 300 F, or reheat in a skillet over low heat so the salmon stays tender
- **Batch size:** Scale to 4 servings for a full dinner, or 6 if you want smaller portions for lighter meal planning

---

## Notes

- **Health score: 7/10** - strong protein and omega-3s from salmon, with potatoes and citrus keeping it balanced and useful for meal planning
- **Oil-heavy but still solid** - the 1/2 cup olive oil makes this richer than a lean fish dinner, so it is healthy but not low-cal
- **Great wedding-diet staple** - filling, high quality protein, and easy to pair with a green side if you want to tighten the calories
