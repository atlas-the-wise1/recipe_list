# Caldo Verde (Potato and Greens Soup With Sausage)

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #heart-healthy #high-protein #meal-prep
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 40 min | **Total:** ~55 min

---

## Macros (per serving, estimated)
| Calories | Protein | Carbs | Fat | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~380 | ~18g | ~35g | ~18g | ~4g | ~45mg |

---

## Ingredients

- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for serving
- 1 medium yellow onion, halved and thinly sliced
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 5 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1½ lbs Yukon Gold or yellow potatoes, peeled and cut into ½-inch cubes
- 1 (3–5 oz) chouriço, linguiça, or any smoked garlic sausage link (whole)
- 6 cups low-sodium chicken broth
- 2 cups stemmed and finely shredded collard greens or kale (from 3–4 oz unstemmed greens)

---

## Instructions

1. In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot, heat olive oil over medium. Add onion, season with salt and pepper, and cook stirring occasionally until softened and lightly golden, **~8 minutes**. Add garlic and stir until fragrant, **~1 minute**.
2. Add potatoes, the whole sausage link, and broth; season with salt. Bring to a boil over high heat. Cover, reduce heat to medium-low, and cook until potatoes are very soft and crush with no resistance, **~25 minutes**. Transfer sausage to a cutting board to cool.
3. Using a slotted spoon, reserve 1 cup of the potatoes. Purée the remaining soup in a blender (or use an immersion blender) until smooth. Return to pot.
4. Add collard greens and reserved potatoes. Cook over medium-low, stirring and mashing potatoes slightly, until greens are softened and soup is thickened, **~5 minutes**. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Divide soup among bowls. Thinly slice the sausage and divide on top. Drizzle with olive oil and serve warm.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 5 days fridge / 3 months freezer (freeze without sausage slices on top for best texture)
**Reheat:** Stovetop over medium-low with a splash of broth or water to loosen. Microwave works well too.
**Batch size:** Doubles easily — great Sunday meal prep soup for the week
**Substitutions:** Turkey or chicken sausage instead of chouriço cuts fat and cholesterol significantly while keeping the flavor profile
**Notes:** Excellent meal prep candidate — actually gets better after a day in the fridge. The collard greens and garlic make this heart-healthy. Moderate cholesterol, mainly from the sausage — swapping to turkey sausage bumps it up to #low-cholesterol territory.
