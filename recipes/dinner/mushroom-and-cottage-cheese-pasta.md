# Mushroom and Cottage Cheese Pasta

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #vegetarian #quick #comfort-food #high-protein
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~560 | ~18g | ~16g | ~76g | ~5g | ~35mg |
**Health Score:** 6/10

---

## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 1/2 pounds cremini mushrooms, cleaned, trimmed, and roughly chopped (about 6 cups)
- 1 yellow or red onion, roughly chopped (about 2 cups)
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 6 garlic cloves, roughly chopped
- 1 pound short pasta, such as rigatoni or cavatappi
- 1 cup full-fat cottage cheese, plus more for serving
- 1/2 lemon
- 1 teaspoon red-pepper flakes, or to taste
- Handful of parsley leaves, finely chopped

## Instructions

1. Heat a large 12-inch skillet over medium-high. Add the olive oil, mushrooms, onion, 1 teaspoon salt, and a big pinch of pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the mushrooms release their liquid, **4 to 5 minutes**. Add the garlic and cook, stirring, until the mushrooms are soft, browned in spots, and the liquid has cooked away, **7 to 8 minutes**. Turn off the heat.
2. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the pasta according to package instructions. Reserve **3 cups** of the pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta and return it to the pot.
3. Transfer the mushroom mixture to a blender or food processor and add **2 cups** of the pasta cooking water. Blend until silky. Add more pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce.
4. Pour the mushroom sauce over the pasta, add the cottage cheese, and toss to coat thoroughly.
5. Serve with a squeeze of lemon juice, red-pepper flakes, and chopped parsley. Finish with more cottage cheese on top if you want extra richness.

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 4 days refrigerated  
**Reheat:** Warm gently on the stovetop or in the microwave with a splash of water or milk  
**Batch size:** Doubles well; just keep extra pasta water on hand to thin the sauce as needed  
**Notes:** The mushrooms do the heavy lifting here, turning into a deeply savory base once blended. Cottage cheese makes the sauce creamy without needing heavy cream, and the lemon keeps the whole thing from feeling too rich.
