# Slow Cooker BBQ Pulled Pork

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#high-protein` `#meal-prep`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~430 | ~29g | ~24g | ~18g | ~1g | ~95mg |
**Health Score:** 5/10
**Serves:** 8
**Prep Time:** 20 min | **Cook Time:** 6-8 hr | **Total:** ~6 hr 20 min to 8 hr 20 min

---

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon garlic powder
- 1 tablespoon onion powder
- 2 teaspoons hot or sweet smoked paprika
- 2 teaspoons salt, plus more to taste
- 1 teaspoon black pepper, plus more to taste
- 3 to 4 pounds boneless pork shoulder or pork butt, trimmed of most excess fat
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, plus more for greasing
- 1 yellow onion, chopped, optional
- 1 (12-ounce) can dark soda, like Dr Pepper, root beer, cola, or birch beer
- 1 1/2 to 2 cups homemade or store-bought barbecue sauce
- Hot sauce, optional

---

## Instructions

1. In a small bowl, combine the garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper. Rub the spice mixture all over the pork. If you have time, cover and refrigerate for 2 hours or up to overnight. If not, move on.
2. Lightly grease the crock of a slow cooker. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the pork until golden brown on all sides, about 2 minutes per side. Add the onion, if using, to the slow cooker, then set the pork on top. Pour the soda over the pork and cook on low for 6 to 8 hours, until the meat has collapsed and shreds easily.
3. Drain most of the liquid from the slow cooker and shred the meat directly in the pot. Stir in about 1/2 cup barbecue sauce, or more to taste. Serve with extra sauce on the side and hot sauce if you want more heat.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 4 days fridge
- **Reheat:** Warm gently in a skillet or microwave with a splash of extra sauce or cooking liquid
- **Batch size:** Scale to 8 servings for sandwiches, bowls, or meal-prep lunches

---

## Notes

- **Health score: 5/10** - good protein and batch-cook value, but the pork shoulder, barbecue sauce, and soda keep it in the moderate zone
- **Best use case** - pulled pork sandwiches, rice bowls, or loaded baked potatoes
- **Meal-prep win** - this gets better after sitting in the sauce overnight
