# Steak Diane

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #high-protein #keto
**Servings:** 2
**Prep Time:** 20 min | **Cook Time:** 15 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~620 | ~50g | ~44g | ~4g | ~0g | ~185mg |
**Health Score:** 3/10

---

## Ingredients

- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 (12–16 oz) boneless steak — filet mignon or strip steak, ~1-inch thick
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 tbsp neutral oil (grapeseed or safflower)
- 2 tbsp minced shallot or red onion
- 1 tsp tomato paste
- 2 tbsp Cognac or brandy
- ¼ cup heavy cream
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
- ½ tsp fish sauce (optional)
- Minced chives or parsley, for garnish

---

## Instructions

1. Season steak generously with salt and pepper on both sides. Set aside at room temperature for **15–30 minutes**.
2. Melt butter and heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high. Pat steak dry with a paper towel. Increase heat to high, add steak, and sear until well browned, **~2 minutes**. Flip and cook to desired doneness, **1–4 more minutes**.
   - Rare: 125°F | Medium-rare: 135°F | Medium: 145°F
3. Transfer steak to a cutting board and tent with foil or an overturned bowl to keep warm.
4. Add shallot to the pan and sauté over medium-high until golden, **2–3 minutes**.  Stir in tomato paste and cook until deepened in color, **~1 minute**.
5. Turn off heat. Add Cognac to deglaze. Using a long match or stick lighter, carefully ignite the Cognac — stand back. Let flames burn out, then return to medium-high heat and cook until Cognac is almost evaporated, **1–2 minutes**.
6. Stir in cream, mustard, Worcestershire, and fish sauce (if using). Cook until thickened, **1–2 minutes**.
7. Slice steak if desired, drizzle warm sauce over top, and garnish with black pepper and chives or parsley.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 3 days fridge. Sauce stores separately — reheats well in a small saucepan over low.
**Reheat:** Steak in a warm skillet over medium-low 1–2 min per side. Sauce in a small pan over low, add a splash of cream if it tightens up.
**Batch size:** Double the steak and sauce proportionally — use a larger skillet to avoid crowding.
**Safety note:** The Cognac flambé step is dramatic but optional — you can skip igniting and just cook until evaporated. Same flavor result, less theater.
**Notes:** Impressive restaurant-quality dish ready in 35 min. High fat and cholesterol from butter, cream, and steak — firmly in the occasional column. The sauce alone is worth making for other proteins (chicken, pork tenderloin) to stretch the recipe into healthier territory. See `steak-diane-healthier.md` for a lightened version using Greek yogurt sauce and leaner sirloin.
