# Eggplant and Bean Chili

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #heart-healthy #low-cholesterol #meal-prep
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 45 min | **Total:** ~60 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~415 | ~24g | ~8g | ~69g | ~22g | ~0mg |
**Health Score:** 6/10

---

## Ingredients

- ¼ cup neutral oil (safflower or canola)
- 1 lb eggplant (1 medium), peeled and cut into ½-inch cubes (~6 cups)
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 1 small onion, finely chopped (~1 cup)
- 2 small carrots, peeled and cut into ¼-inch dice (~1 cup)
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 3 large garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp pure chile powder (ancho, chipotle, or a mix)
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp dried oregano
- 1 (14 oz) can whole tomatoes and their juices, crushed by hand
- 1 (15 oz) can red kidney beans, rinsed
- 1 (14 oz) can lentils, rinsed
- Thinly sliced scallions
- 1 tbsp lime juice

### Optional Toppings
- Sour cream, grated cheddar, sliced avocado, tortilla chips

---

## Instructions

1. In a large Dutch oven, heat 2 tbsp oil over medium. Add eggplant, season with salt and pepper, and cook stirring occasionally until softened and golden in spots, **~5 minutes**. Transfer to a plate.
2. Reduce heat to medium-low. Add remaining 2 tbsp oil, then onion and carrots. Season with salt and pepper. Cook stirring occasionally until softened, **~5 minutes**.
3. Add tomato paste and garlic. Cook stirring occasionally until tomato paste is lightly caramelized, **~3 minutes**. Stir in chile powder, cumin, and oregano until well blended.
4. Add tomatoes, kidney beans, lentils, reserved eggplant, and 2 cups water. Bring to a boil over medium-high, stirring to release any browned bits from the bottom. Cover, reduce heat to medium-low, and cook undisturbed until eggplant is very tender and flavors have melded, **30 minutes**.
5. Uncover and stir, mashing some of the eggplant to create a creamy texture.
6. Stir in scallions and lime juice. Taste and season with salt and pepper.
7. Divide among bowls, garnish with more scallions. Add optional toppings as desired.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 5 days fridge / 3 months freezer — one of the best freezer meals in the book
**Reheat:** Stovetop over medium-low with a splash of water to loosen. Microwave works well.
**Batch size:** Doubles easily in a large pot — freeze in individual portions for quick weeknight meals
**Toppings note:** Keep toppings separate if meal prepping — add fresh when serving
**Notes:** Zero cholesterol, highest fiber in the book at 16g per serving. Fully plant-based. Protein is moderate at 14g — add a dollop of Greek yogurt instead of sour cream to boost protein while keeping it heart-healthy. One of the best make-ahead dinners in the library — flavors deepen significantly after a day in the fridge.
