# Oyakodon (Chicken and Egg Rice Bowl)

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#quick` `#rice-bowl` `#weeknight`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~520 | ~32g | ~18g | ~54g | ~2g | ~300mg |
**Health Score:** 6/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~30 min

---

## Ingredients

- 2 cups Japanese short-grain rice
- 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, sliced into bite-size pieces
- 1 tablespoon sake
- 1 cup dashi
- 2 tablespoons mirin
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce, or more to taste
- 1 tablespoon granulated sugar, or more to taste
- 1 small onion, thinly sliced
- 4 large eggs
- 2 scallions, sliced, or 3 sprigs mitsuba
- Togarashi, optional, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. Cook rice according to package directions.
2. Meanwhile, stir chicken and sake in a bowl. In a measuring cup or bowl, stir dashi, mirin, soy sauce, and sugar until the sugar dissolves. Add more soy sauce or sugar to taste.
3. In a deep medium frying pan, add the dashi mixture and onion. Bring to a simmer over medium-high, then add the chicken in a single layer, making sure the dashi mixture covers the chicken and onion.
4. Bring the heat down to medium-low. Cook for 2 minutes, then flip each piece of chicken with a pair of chopsticks or tongs. Continue cooking until chicken is just cooked through and onion has softened, about 3 minutes more.
5. In a measuring cup or small bowl, lightly beat eggs. Bring the heat up to medium, and add three-quarters of the eggs in a clockwise pattern over the chicken. Allow the mixture to simmer, swirling the pan to ensure coverage. When the eggs are slightly set, around 1 minute, add the remaining eggs around the pan's edges in a clockwise fashion. Once again, swirl the pan to ensure coverage.
6. Continue to cook the oyakodon to your preferred doneness, then add scallions or mitsuba to the pan.
7. Divide the rice among individual bowls, then slide enough of the oyakodon over the rice to cover. Serve immediately and sprinkle with togarashi if preferred.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** 3 days refrigerated
- **Reheat:** Warm gently in a skillet or microwave just until hot so the eggs stay tender
- **Batch size:** Best made fresh, but the rice and chicken can be prepped ahead to speed up dinner

---

## Notes

- This is a classic Japanese comfort bowl: savory, brothy, and quick.
- The eggs push the cholesterol up, but the dish still delivers solid protein and a comforting balanced carb base.
- If you want it lighter later, serve a little more of the egg-chicken mixture over a smaller rice portion.
