# Crispy Coconut Shrimp and Shallots

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** `#seafood` `#quick` `#weeknight`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~460 | ~28g | ~21g | ~34g | ~2g | ~170mg |
**Health Score:** 5/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 15 min | **Total:** ~30 min

---

## Ingredients

- 5 tablespoons unrefined coconut oil or olive oil, or a mixture of both
- 1 large shallot, sliced into thin rings (about a heaping 1/2 cup)
- 1 cup unsweetened coconut flakes
- Salt and black pepper
- 1 pound large raw shrimp (20 to 25 count), peeled and deveined, patted dry
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch
- 1 cup orange juice
- 1 tablespoon lime juice
- 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
- 2 teaspoons fish sauce
- 1/2 teaspoon red-pepper flakes
- 1/4 cup coarsely chopped cilantro
- Freshly steamed rice, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. Heat 3 tablespoons of coconut oil in a large 10- to 12-inch skillet over medium. Once melted, stir in the shallots and, once they are rapidly sizzling, after about 2 minutes, stir in the coconut flakes so they get coated in oil. Season lightly with salt and pepper and sauté, stirring frequently until both the shallots and coconut are golden brown and crisp, 5 to 7 minutes. Transfer the coconut and shallot to a paper towel-lined plate, set aside, and wipe the pan clean.
2. In the same pan, add the remaining 2 tablespoons coconut oil and heat on high. Once hot, add the shrimp in a single layer, if possible, and cook, turning them over occasionally until cooked through and thoroughly pink, 4 to 5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper and transfer to a plate. Wipe the pan clean and turn heat down to low.
3. Whisk the cornstarch into 2 tablespoons water to make a slurry. Add the orange juice and lime juice to the pan, then the cornstarch slurry, sugar, fish sauce, and red-pepper flakes, and stir to combine and cook over low heat until slightly thickened, about 3 minutes. Season as needed with salt and black pepper. Turn off heat.
4. Serve immediately by first spooning the rice into bowls, then placing the shrimp on top with the sauce generously spooned over. Toss the cilantro with the coconut and shallots and sprinkle on top of the shrimp.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** Best eaten fresh; leftovers keep 1 to 2 days refrigerated
- **Reheat:** Rewarm shrimp gently in a skillet or microwave briefly so they do not overcook
- **Batch size:** Works best as a quick dinner over rice rather than as a make-ahead meal

---

## Notes

- The crisp coconut-and-shallot topping is the signature move here, so add it right before serving to keep the texture.
- This is richer than a plain shrimp bowl because of the coconut oil and rice, but the shrimp keeps the protein strong.
- If you want it lighter later, cut the rice portion first and keep the topping intact.
