# Cheese-Topped Cauliflower Steaks

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #vegetarian #high-protein #comfort-food #vegetable-forward
**Servings:** 6
**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 35 min | **Total:** ~50 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~320 | ~15g | ~24g | ~14g | ~4g | ~55mg |
**Health Score:** 6/10

---

## Ingredients

- 2 medium cauliflower heads (about 2 pounds each), cut into 12 (3/4-inch-thick) slices, trimmings reserved for another purpose
- Extra-virgin olive oil, for roasting
- Salt and black pepper
- 12 slices fresh mozzarella (from one 1-pound package)
- 12 slices provolone (from one 8-ounce package)
- 12 slices Monterey Jack (from one 8-ounce package)
- About 1/2 cup grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano
- Pinch of red-pepper flakes
- 1/2 cup dry, coarse bread crumbs, preferably homemade
- 12 anchovy fillets, rinsed and patted dry (optional)
- Pitted black olives, such as niçoise (optional)
- 2 tablespoons roughly chopped Italian parsley
- 1 teaspoon roughly chopped rosemary

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to **400°F**. Place the cauliflower slices on 2 rimmed baking sheets. Drizzle both sides with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Bake, rotating the pans and switching racks halfway through, until fork-tender and lightly browned, about **20 minutes**.
2. Top each cauliflower steak with a slice of mozzarella, a slice of provolone, and a slice of Monterey Jack. Sprinkle with Parmesan, a tiny bit of red-pepper flakes, and a heaping tablespoon of bread crumbs. Bake until golden and bubbling, **about 15 minutes** more.
3. Top each steak with a torn piece or two of anchovy if using, a few black olives, parsley, and rosemary.

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 3 days refrigerated  
**Reheat:** 375°F oven for 8 to 10 minutes to re-melt the cheese  
**Batch size:** Best baked on 2 sheet pans so the cauliflower browns instead of steaming  
**Notes:** This is basically cauliflower dressed like a loaded pizza-meets-gratin. The anchovies and olives add salt and depth, but it still works without them if you want the dish vegetarian.
