# Spaghetti with Meat Sauce — Healthier Variant

> 💡 **Healthier Variant** of Spaghetti with Meat Sauce — see `spaghetti-meat-sauce.md` for the classic version.

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #high-protein #meal-prep #heart-healthy #low-cholesterol #fat-loss
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 30 min | **Total:** ~40 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~475 | ~50g | ~8g | ~48g | ~12g | ~75mg |
**Health Score:** 7/10

**What changed vs. classic (ground turkey version):** ~100 fewer calories, ~4g less fat, similar protein. Achieves this by:
- Switching to **Banza chickpea spaghetti** — adds 4g fiber per serving, lower glycemic index
- Adding **hidden vegetables** (zucchini, mushrooms, bell pepper) to the sauce — boosts fiber, volume, and micronutrients
- Using **ground chicken or extra-lean ground turkey (93/7)** — leanest option
- **Boosting protein** with a tablespoon of tomato paste and a parmesan rind simmered in the sauce

---

## Ingredients

- 1 lb **Banza chickpea spaghetti** (or chickpea pasta for even more protein)
- 1 lb ground chicken or extra-lean ground turkey (93% lean)
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 1 medium zucchini, finely diced
- 8 oz cremini mushrooms, finely chopped
- 1 red bell pepper, finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 (24 oz) jar low-sodium tomato or marinara sauce
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- ½ tsp dried basil
- ¼ tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1 Parmesan rind (optional — adds body and depth without adding much fat)
- Grated Parmesan, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. Cook Banza chickpea spaghetti in heavily salted boiling water per package directions. **Reserve ½ cup pasta water before draining.** Drain and set aside.
2. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium. Add onion, zucchini, mushrooms, and bell pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, until softened and any moisture from the mushrooms has evaporated, **~8 minutes**.
3. Push vegetables to the sides. Add ground meat and cook, breaking it up, until fully browned, **~5 minutes**. Stir everything together.
4. Add garlic and tomato paste. Cook stirring until tomato paste darkens slightly, **~2 minutes**. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Stir in tomato sauce, oregano, basil, and red pepper flakes. Add Parmesan rind if using. Simmer over medium-low, stirring occasionally, **15–20 minutes**.
6. Remove Parmesan rind. Add a splash of reserved pasta water if sauce is too thick. Taste and adjust seasoning.
7. Toss with cooked spaghetti. Serve topped with grated Parmesan.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** Sauce 5 days fridge / 3 months freezer. Store sauce and pasta separately for best texture.
**Reheat:** Sauce on stovetop over medium-low with a splash of water. Pasta microwaved with a splash of water.
**Batch size:** Doubles easily — make a double batch of sauce and freeze half for future meals
**Notes:** The hidden vegetable technique (zucchini + mushrooms + bell pepper) adds significant volume and fiber to the sauce without changing the eating experience — you barely notice them once everything is simmered together. Whole-wheat pasta adds a pleasant nutty depth that actually pairs well with meat sauce. The Parmesan rind trick adds savory body to the sauce without adding meaningful calories.
