# Baby Back Ribs With Sweet and Sour Glaze

**Category:** Dinner
**Tags:** #high-protein #meal-prep
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 2.5 hrs | **Total:** ~2 hrs 45 min

---

**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~620 | ~42g | ~42g | ~14g | ~0g | ~160mg |

---

## Ingredients
- 1 large rack baby back pork ribs (3 to 3½ pounds)
- 1½ tsp coarse kosher salt
- 1 tsp onion powder
- ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 4 garlic cloves, crushed but left whole
- ¼ cup Concord grape jelly
- 2 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp soy sauce

---

## Instructions

1. Position an oven rack 6 inches from the broiler and another on the bottom of the oven. Heat oven to 325°F.
2. Optional: Remove the membrane from the bony underside of the ribs — use a small knife to release one corner, then pull off with your hands and discard.
3. In a small bowl, stir together salt, onion powder, and black pepper. Season ribs on both sides.
4. Wrap ribs tightly in aluminum foil, place on a sheet pan flesh-side down, and bake on the bottom rack until meat is tender and pulls away easily from bones, **2 to 2½ hours**.
5. Remove ribs from oven, then heat the broiler. Unwrap ribs and transfer flesh-side up to the sheet pan — reserve the foil with its juices.
6. Tip the reserved juices into a large skillet. Add garlic, jelly, vinegar, and soy sauce. Bring to a simmer over medium-high. Cook, whisking occasionally, until syrupy and reduced by half, **5–7 minutes**. Remove from heat.
7. Brush or spoon half the glaze (including garlic cloves) over the ribs. Broil until charred in spots, **1–3 minutes**. Don't step away.
8. Transfer ribs to a cutting board, spoon remaining glaze over top. Cut between bones into riblets and serve.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

**Storage:** 4 days fridge / 3 months freezer
**Reheat:** Wrapped in foil at 300°F for 15–20 min, or microwave covered with a damp paper towel
**Batch size:** Double the rack for 8 servings — glaze scales easily
**Notes:** Ribs can be baked (steps 1–4) up to 2 days ahead and refrigerated. Glaze and broil day-of for best results. High in saturated fat (~14g/serving) and cholesterol — treat as an occasional dinner.
