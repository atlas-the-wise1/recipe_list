## Ingredients

- 6 tablespoons cold butter, cut into small pieces, plus more for greasing pan
- 2 1/2 to 3 pounds rhubarb, trimmed, tough strings removed, and cut into 1 1/2-inch pieces (about 5 to 6 cups)
- 1/4 cup white sugar
- 1 tablespoon orange or lemon juice
- 1 teaspoon orange or lemon zest
- 3/4 cup brown sugar
- 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
- 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon, or to taste
- Pinch salt
- 1/2 cup rolled oats
- 1/2 cup pecans

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Heat oven to 375 degrees. Butter a 9-by-13-inch baking dish or a deep 2-quart baking dish.

**STEP 2:** In a large bowl, toss the rhubarb with the white sugar, citrus juice, and zest. Transfer to the prepared baking dish and spread into an even layer.

**STEP 3:** In a separate bowl, mix the brown sugar, flour, cinnamon, and salt. Add the oats and pecans, then work in the butter with your fingers or a fork until the mixture forms clumps.

**STEP 4:** Sprinkle the topping evenly over the rhubarb. Bake until the fruit is bubbling around the edges and the top is golden brown, 45 to 50 minutes.

**STEP 5:** Let the crisp cool for at least 15 minutes before serving so the juices thicken a bit. Serve warm, plain or with vanilla ice cream.

## Notes

- **Best texture:** serve warm so the topping stays crisp and the rhubarb is still juicy
- **Citrus choice:** lemon makes it brighter; orange makes it a little rounder and sweeter
- **Make-ahead:** bake earlier in the day and rewarm at 325°F for 10 to 15 minutes before serving
- **Storage:** cover and refrigerate leftovers for up to 4 days
