# Lemon Pudding Cakes With Sugared Raspberries

**Category:** S — Snack
**Tags:** `#vegetarian` `#low-cholesterol`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~285 | ~6g | ~10g | ~44g | ~2g | ~110mg |
**Health Score:** 4/10

**Serves:** 6 ramekins
**Prep Time:** 20 min | **Cook Time:** 35–40 min | **Total:** ~75 min

---

## Ingredients

- ¼ cup / 57g unsalted butter (½ stick), melted and cooled, plus more for greasing
- 3 large eggs, separated
- 1 cup / 200g granulated sugar, plus more as needed
- ¾ cup / 180ml buttermilk (or plain yogurt thinned with milk)
- 1 tablespoon finely grated lemon zest (from 1–2 lemons)
- ½ cup / 120ml lemon juice (from 3–4 lemons)
- ¼ teaspoon fine sea salt or table salt
- ⅓ cup / 43g all-purpose flour
- 6 oz / 170g raspberries

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Heat oven to 350°F. Butter 6 (8-oz) ramekins. Place them in a large baking pan where they can sit without touching — a 9×13-inch pan works well.

**STEP 2:** In a large bowl, whisk together butter, egg yolks, ¾ cup sugar, buttermilk, lemon zest and juice, and salt until smooth. Whisk in flour until well combined.

**STEP 3:** Using an electric mixer (handheld or stand mixer with whisk attachment), beat egg whites on medium speed until thick, white, and very foamy — 2 to 4 minutes. Increase to high speed and gradually beat in remaining ¼ cup sugar, continuing until stiff and glossy peaks form — about 2 to 3 minutes more.

**STEP 4:** Using a rubber spatula, gently fold about a third of the egg whites into the lemon mixture, taking care not to deflate them. When no white streaks remain, fold in remaining whites carefully. Stop folding when most of the second batch is mixed in but a few white streaks remain.

**STEP 5:** Spoon batter into ramekins. Transfer pan to oven. Carefully pour enough very hot tap water into the pan to come about halfway up the sides of the ramekins. *Note: The higher the water level, the more pudding and less cake you'll get.*

**STEP 6:** Bake until puddings are puffed and lightly browned on top — 35 to 40 minutes. Do not overbake or there won't be any pudding at the bottom. Carefully transfer ramekins from pan to a rack to cool at least 15 minutes before serving. Use tongs or spatula plus oven mitts.

**STEP 7:** As the cakes bake, mix raspberries with a few pinches of sugar in a bowl, mashing slightly. Let macerate until sugar melts — about 15 minutes. Taste and add more sugar if berries are very tart. Serve cakes with sugared berries and their syrup.

---

## Notes

- **The magic is the bain-marie (water bath)** — don't skip it; it's what creates the self-separating pudding layer at the bottom and cake layer on top
- **Don't overbake** — 35 minutes is the floor; check at 35, pull when just lightly browned and still slightly jiggly
- **Serve warm or room temp** — the pudding layer sets more as it cools; warm = more luscious, room temp = firmer
- **Make-ahead:** Batter can't be made ahead (whites will deflate), but baked cakes keep at room temp 2–3 hours or refrigerated up to 2 days; reheat gently
- **Lighter swap:** Reduce butter to 3 tablespoons and use low-fat buttermilk to drop ~40 cal/serving
- **Seasonal swap:** Sub blueberries or blackberries for raspberries; same maceration technique
