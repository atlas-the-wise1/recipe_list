# Air-Fryer Sweet Potato Fries

**Category:** Snack
**Tags:** `#quick` `#air-fryer` `#vegetarian`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~180 | ~2g | ~10g | ~22g | ~3g | ~0mg |
**Health Score:** 7/10

**Serves:** 2
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 12 min | **Total:** ~22 min

---

## Ingredients

- 1 small sweet potato, about 8 ounces, peeled
- 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 teaspoons ground paprika
- Salt
- Ketchup or mustard, optional, for serving

---

## Instructions

1. Heat the air fryer to 400 F, if preheating is recommended for your model.
2. Cut the sweet potato in half lengthwise, then set each half on its flat surface and slice into 1/4-inch planks. Cut the planks lengthwise into 1/4-inch thick strips. In a large bowl, toss the sweet potato fries with the olive oil and paprika until well-coated; season generously with salt.
3. Transfer the sweet potatoes to the air fryer, piling them haphazardly so they have some height. This helps them cook evenly and crisp in spots.
4. Cook for 5 minutes. Use tongs to give them a quick toss, then continue cooking until the fries are tender on the inside and crisped and darkened in some spots toward the tips, 10 to 12 minutes total, tossing again halfway through.
5. Transfer sweet potatoes to a serving dish and season with more salt if desired. Serve with ketchup or mustard.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** Best fresh; leftovers keep 1 to 2 days refrigerated
- **Reheat:** Air fryer at 375 to 400 F for a few minutes to restore crispness
- **Batch size:** Works best as a small snack or side dish

---

## Notes

- Piling them a bit haphazardly in the basket is intentional and helps the edges crisp instead of steaming flat.
- Paprika gives them a simple savory heat; salt at the end tightens the flavor.
- If you want them lighter later, use slightly less oil and keep the basket from being too crowded.
