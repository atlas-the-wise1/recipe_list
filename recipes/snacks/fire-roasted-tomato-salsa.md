# Fire-Roasted Tomato Salsa

**Category:** S — Snacks / Condiment
**Tags:** `#vegan` `#low-cholesterol` `#heart-healthy` `#fat-loss` `#quick` `#meal-prep` `#keto`

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~22 | ~1g | ~0g | ~5g | ~1g | ~0mg |
**Makes:** ~2 cups (8 servings)
**Prep Time:** 5 min | **Total:** ~5 min

---

**Health Score:** 6/10

## Ingredients

- 1 (14½-ounce) can fire-roasted tomatoes with juice
- 2 tomatoes, cored and seeded
- ½ small red onion, peeled and roughly cut
- 3 cloves garlic, peeled
- 1 chipotle pepper, chopped
- ½ jalapeño pepper
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
- ¼ teaspoon ground cumin
- ¼ cup fresh cilantro leaves
- 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Place all ingredients into the blender pitcher in the order listed above.

**STEP 2:** Pulse 4–5 times until vegetables are roughly chopped or desired consistency is reached. Do not over-blend — chunky is better than smooth for salsa.

---

## Notes

- **Pulse, don't blend** — stop at chunky; over-blending turns this into tomato soup
- **Heat level:** 1 chipotle + ½ jalapeño = medium heat; remove jalapeño seeds for mild, add a full jalapeño for hot
- **Chipotle pepper:** From a can of chipotles in adobo — adds smoky depth; freeze remaining chipotles in ice cube trays for future use
- **Fire-roasted tomatoes** are the flavor backbone — don't sub regular canned tomatoes; the char makes the difference
- **Fridge life:** Keeps refrigerated up to 1 week; flavors deepen overnight — make it the day before if you can
- **Use it for:** Chips, eggs, tacos, grilled chicken, fish, or as a base for shakshuka
