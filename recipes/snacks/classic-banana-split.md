# Classic Banana Split

**Category:** S — Snack
**Tags:** `#vegetarian` `#treat` `#dessert`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~960 | ~14g | ~50g | ~118g | ~7g | ~95mg |
**Health Score:** 1/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 20 min | **Cook Time:** 15 min | **Total:** ~35 min

---

## Ingredients

### For the Walnuts

- 1 cup / 115g walnut halves
- 1/4 cup / 59ml maple syrup
- 1/4 cup / 59ml honey
- Pinch of fine sea salt

### For the Hot Fudge Sauce

- 2/3 cup / 158ml heavy cream
- 1/4 cup / 50g packed light brown sugar
- 3 tablespoons honey
- 1 1/2 tablespoons Dutch-process cocoa powder
- Pinch of fine sea salt
- 3 1/2 ounces / 100g bittersweet chocolate
- 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, cubed
- 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract, or 1/2 tablespoon brandy or rum

### For Assembly

- 2 bananas, halved lengthwise and peeled
- 2 pints ice cream, any flavors
- Whipped cream, for serving
- Cherries, for garnish (optional)

---

## Instructions

1. Heat the oven to 350 F. Spread the walnut halves on a baking sheet and bake for 8 to 12 minutes, until toasted and slightly darker. Transfer to a rack to cool.
2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the maple syrup, honey, and salt. Add the walnuts and stir to coat.
3. In a medium saucepan over medium heat, bring the cream, brown sugar, honey, cocoa, and salt to a simmer, whisking until the sugar dissolves and the mixture thickens slightly, about 5 minutes.
4. Stir in the chocolate and cook until melted, about 2 minutes. Stir in the butter and vanilla or liquor. Cool slightly. If the sauce separates, whisk in 1 tablespoon hot water. The sauce can be made up to 2 weeks ahead and refrigerated; reheat gently before serving.
5. Line a banana split bowl or other bowl with the banana halves. Top with scoops of ice cream, hot fudge, and walnut sauce. Add whipped cream and a cherry if you like. Serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- This is an indulgent dessert, not a daily health-goals recipe.
- The walnuts add some useful fat and texture, but the ice cream, hot fudge, honey, and whipped cream make this a high-calorie treat.
- Best use case is an occasional wedding-weekend dessert rather than a regular meal-planning item.
