# Basil Pesto

**Category:** S — Snacks / Condiment
**Tags:** `#vegetarian` `#quick` `#keto` `#meal-prep` `#heart-healthy`
**Nutrition (per ~3 tablespoon serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~314 | ~6g | ~31g | ~2g | ~0g | ~4mg |
**Serves:** 4–6
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Total:** ~10 min

---

## Ingredients

- ½ cup toasted pine nuts
- 4 large garlic cloves, peeled
- 3 cups tightly packed fresh basil leaves
- ½ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ½ teaspoon pepper
- ½ to ¾ cup extra-virgin olive oil

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Place all ingredients except the olive oil into the blender pitcher. Blend on MEDIUM until smooth.

**STEP 2:** Add the olive oil and continue to blend until all ingredients are combined and smooth. Taste and adjust salt if needed.

---

## Notes

- **Toast the pine nuts** — 3–4 min in a dry skillet over medium heat until golden; adds depth and nuttiness; don't skip this step
- **Oil last** — adding oil after the solids are blended creates a better emulsion and prevents the pesto from turning gummy
- **Start with ½ cup oil** and add more to reach your preferred consistency — thicker for pasta sauce, looser for drizzling
- **Freeze well:** Portion into ice cube trays, freeze, then bag — each cube = ~1 serving; pull out what you need; keeps 3 months
- **Oxidation:** Pesto browns fast — press plastic wrap directly onto the surface before refrigerating; a thin drizzle of olive oil on top also helps
- **Use it for:** Pasta, pizza base, grilled chicken, fish, sandwiches, grain bowls, scrambled eggs, roasted veggies
- **Sub walnuts** for pine nuts to cut cost significantly — different flavor but still excellent
