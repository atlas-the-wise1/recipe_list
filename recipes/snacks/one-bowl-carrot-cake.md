# One-Bowl Carrot Cake

**Category:** S — Snack
**Tags:** `#vegetarian` `#meal-prep`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~380 | ~6g | ~22g | ~42g | ~1g | ~95mg |
**Health Score:** 3/10

**Serves:** 9 squares (8-inch square pan)
**Prep Time:** 20 min | **Cook Time:** 50–60 min | **Total:** ~80 min + cooling

---

## Ingredients

### Topping
- 1 (8-oz / 226g) block cream cheese, softened
- ½ packed cup / 73g confectioners' sugar
- ¼ teaspoon fine sea salt
- 1 large egg
- ½ cup / 120g sour cream

### Cake
- Nonstick cooking spray
- 2 large eggs
- ¾ cup / 168g granulated sugar
- ½ cup / 100g neutral oil (vegetable or canola)
- 1 cup / 135g all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon sweet baking spice (or any combination of ground cinnamon, cardamom, ginger, or nutmeg)
- 1½ teaspoons baking powder
- ½ teaspoon baking soda
- ½ teaspoon fine sea salt
- 2 packed cups / 260g coarsely grated peeled carrots (from 3 medium carrots)

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Heat oven to 375°F. Lightly coat an 8-inch square baking pan with cooking spray. Line the bottom and sides with parchment paper, then spray again.

**STEP 2 — Make the topping:** Whisk cream cheese in a large bowl until smooth. Add confectioners' sugar and salt; stir slowly until no sugar traces remain, then whisk rapidly until smooth. Add egg and whisk until fully incorporated, then whisk in sour cream until smooth. Using a silicone spatula, scrape into a pastry bag fitted with a ½-inch plain tip (or a gallon-size zip-lock bag). Set aside. *Reserve the bowl, whisk, and spatula without washing.*

**STEP 3 — Make the cake batter:** Combine eggs and granulated sugar in the reserved bowl and beat with the whisk until evenly combined. While whisking, add oil in a steady stream and whisk until emulsified. Add flour, spice, baking powder, baking soda, and salt; use spatula to fold into wet ingredients just until dry ingredients disappear. Add carrots and fold until evenly distributed.

**STEP 4:** Scrape batter into the prepared pan and spread evenly. If using a zip-lock bag, snip a ½-inch hole in a lower corner. Pipe the cream cheese topping gently and evenly over the batter to completely cover it. *(It's OK if it looks bumpy — it will even out in the oven.)*

**STEP 5:** Bake until the top is golden brown and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out with bits of topping but no cake batter — 50 to 60 minutes.

**STEP 6:** Cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Cut directly in the pan, or slide out using parchment to cut into squares. Refrigerate in an airtight container up to 5 days. Bring to room temperature before serving.

---

## Notes

- **True one-bowl** — reserve the bowl after making the topping; no extra dishes for the cake batter
- **Meal-prep friendly:** Makes 9 squares, lasts 5 days refrigerated — batch once, snack all week
- **Grate your own carrots** — pre-shredded bag carrots are too dry and coarse; fresh-grated gives moisture and better distribution
- **Topping piped on raw batter** — not a frosting applied after; it bakes into the top and creates a distinct cream cheese layer
- **Lighter swap:** Sub Greek yogurt for the sour cream in the topping (saves ~20 cal/serving, adds protein); use light cream cheese to drop another ~30 cal
- **Occasion vs. everyday:** Higher sugar and fat — treat/snack territory, not a daily health-goals item. The cream cheese topping is non-negotiable for the experience.
- **Spice blend tip:** Equal parts cinnamon and cardamom with a pinch of ginger is the sweet spot if you don't have baking spice
