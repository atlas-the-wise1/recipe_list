# Spinach & Artichoke Dip

**Category:** S — Snacks
**Tags:** `#vegetarian` `#high-protein` `#keto`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~427 | ~9g | ~37g | ~7g | ~4g | ~75mg |
**Health Score:** 4/10

**Serves:** 2–4
**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 20 min | **Total:** ~30 min

---

## Ingredients

- ¼ cup mayonnaise
- ¼ cup sour cream
- 8 ounces cream cheese
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 1 (14-ounce) can artichoke hearts, drained and chopped
- ½ cup low-fat mozzarella cheese, shredded
- ¼ cup Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces or grated
- 2 tablespoons chopped onion
- 1 cup frozen spinach, thawed and chopped *(squeeze out excess liquid)*
- Salt and pepper to taste

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Preheat oven to 350°F. Place all ingredients into the blender pitcher in the order listed above, except the spinach. Blend on MEDIUM for 15–20 seconds until ingredients are just combined — do not over-blend.

**STEP 2:** Add the chopped spinach and pulse until incorporated. Spoon into a heat-resistant serving dish and bake for 20 minutes until hot and bubbly. Serve with sliced French bread.

---

## Notes

- **Squeeze the spinach hard** — excess water will make the dip runny; use a kitchen towel and wring it out thoroughly
- **Don't over-blend** — you want some texture, not a paste; 15–20 seconds on medium and stop
- **Indulgent snack:** At 427 cal and 37g fat this is a party dip, not an everyday snack — great for game day or entertaining
- **Lighter version:** Swap cream cheese for Neufchâtel (⅓ less fat), skip the mayo, double the Greek yogurt — cuts ~150 cal and ~15g fat per serving
- **Serve with:** French bread, tortilla chips, pita, or veggies for a lower-carb option
- **Make ahead:** Assemble, refrigerate unbaked up to 24 hours — bake when ready to serve
