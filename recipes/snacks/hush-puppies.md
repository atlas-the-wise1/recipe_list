# Hush Puppies

---

## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted and cooled
- 1 large egg, at room temperature
- 1/4 cup grated white or yellow onion, from about 1/2 large onion
- 2 tablespoons chopped chives, optional
- 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
- 1 tablespoon brown sugar
- 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more for finishing
- 1 1/2 cups fine yellow cornmeal
- 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
- 1 cup buttermilk
- 1 quart vegetable oil, for frying
- Salted butter, for serving, optional

---

## Instructions

1. Set a rack over a baking sheet. If you do not have a rack, line a baking tray with paper towels.
2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the melted butter and egg until smooth. Add the onion, chives if using, both sugars, baking powder, and salt, then mix until no lumps remain.
3. Stir in the cornmeal and flour. While whisking, stream in the buttermilk and mix until the batter is smooth but not runny. It should be thick enough to scoop and drop into hot oil.
4. In a medium skillet with high sides or a deep cast-iron skillet, heat about 2 inches of oil to 350 F over medium heat. Working in batches, scoop 1-tablespoon balls of batter and carefully drop them into the oil. Fry until one side is golden brown, about 1 minute, then flip and fry another minute until golden brown all over.
5. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the hush puppies to the prepared rack. Sprinkle with a little more salt. Serve hot, with salted butter if you like.

---

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Storage:** Best fresh; 1 day fridge for leftovers
- **Reheat:** Reheat in a 375 F oven or air fryer until crisp again
- **Batch size:** Scale to 6 servings for a side dish or snack spread

---

## Notes

- **Health score: 3/10** - cornmeal and onion are fine, but the deep-frying makes this a treat rather than a daily staple
- **Best use case** - a side for seafood, barbecue, or fried-fish dinners
- **Texture tip** - serve immediately so the outside stays crisp and the middle stays tender
