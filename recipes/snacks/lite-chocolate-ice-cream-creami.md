# Lite Chocolate Ice Cream

**Category:** S — Snacks
**Tags:** `#vegan` `#low-cholesterol` `#heart-healthy` `#fat-loss`
**⚠️ Requires:** Ninja CREAMi machine
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~132 | ~1g | ~9g | ~13g | ~1g | ~0mg |
**Health Score:** 6/10

**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 5–10 min | **Freeze Time:** 24 hours | **Best Within:** 2 weeks

---

## Ingredients

- ¾ cup unsweetened coconut cream
- 3 tablespoons monk fruit sweetener with erythritol
- 2 tablespoons dark cocoa powder
- 1 tablespoon raw agave nectar
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1 cup chocolate oat milk

---

## Tools Needed
- Large bowl + whisk
- Ninja CREAMi pint container with lid

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** In a large bowl, whisk the unsweetened coconut cream until smooth.

**STEP 2:** Add monk fruit sweetener, dark cocoa powder, agave nectar, and vanilla extract. Whisk until well combined and sweetener is fully dissolved. Add chocolate oat milk and whisk to combine.

**STEP 3:** Pour base into an empty CREAMi pint container. Place storage lid on pint and freeze for 24 hours.

**STEP 4:** Remove pint from freezer and remove lid. Process according to your CREAMi's start guide (Ice Cream function). Serve immediately or re-lid and store in freezer.

---

## Notes

- **24-hour freeze is mandatory** — the CREAMi needs a fully frozen solid pint to process correctly; don't shortcut this
- **Monk fruit + erythritol** is zero-calorie — this is what keeps it "lite"; sub regular sugar (3 tbsp) if you don't have it but macros will change (+~144 cal per batch)
- **Coconut cream ≠ coconut milk** — use the thick cream, not the thin milk; it's what gives this an ice cream texture
- **Re-spin:** If texture is crumbly after first process, add 1 tbsp oat milk and re-spin — CREAMi tip for firmer results
- **Chocolate oat milk** adds natural sweetness and chocolate depth; sub regular oat milk + 1 extra tsp cocoa if needed
- **Only 132 cal** for actual ice cream — one of the best macro-friendly desserts in the library
