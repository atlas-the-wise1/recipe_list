## Ingredients

### For the Cake
- 8 tablespoons / 114 grams (1 stick) unsalted butter, cut into pieces, softened
- 8 ounces / 226 grams chocolate, melted
- 6 large eggs, separated
- 1/2 cup / 113 grams superfine sugar
- 1/3 cup / 75 grams superfine sugar
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

### For the Topping
- 1 cup / 240 milliliters heavy cream
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 4 ounces / 113 grams chocolate, melted and cooled
- Approximately 1 cup small candy eggs, like robin's eggs

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Heat oven to 350 degrees. Line the bottom of a 9-inch springform cake pan with parchment paper.

**STEP 2:** For the cake, stir butter into chocolate, reheating the mixture to incorporate the butter if needed, and let cool. In a medium bowl, whisk 4 egg whites until foamy. Gradually add 1/2 cup sugar and whisk until the whites hold their shape but are not too stiff. Reserve.

**STEP 3:** In a large bowl, whisk 2 whole eggs and 4 egg yolks with 1/3 cup sugar and vanilla until combined. Stir in chocolate to mix.

**STEP 4:** In three additions, fold the whites into the chocolate mixture. Pour into the prepared pan and bake for 35 to 40 minutes, or until the cake rises, cracks, and the center is no longer wobbly.

**STEP 5:** Cool the cake on a wire rack; the middle will sink and the sides will crack. Carefully remove the cake from the pan and place it on a serving plate.

**STEP 6:** For the icing, whip cream with vanilla until it is firm but not stiff. Fold in the melted chocolate. Fill the top of the cake with icing, easing it out gently toward the edges. Arrange Easter eggs on top.

---

## Notes

- Best served the day it is assembled so the candy eggs stay crisp
- A 9-inch springform pan is important because the cake is delicate after baking
- The original recipe uses superfine sugar for better whipping and a smoother texture
