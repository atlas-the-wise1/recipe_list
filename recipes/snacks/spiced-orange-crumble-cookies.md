# Spiced Orange Crumble Cookies

**Category:** S — Snack
**Tags:** `#vegetarian`
**Nutrition (per serving):**
| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Fiber | Cholesterol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~320 | ~4g | ~18g | ~38g | ~1g | ~45mg |
**Serves:** 8–10 wedges (one 9-inch springform pan)
**Prep Time:** 20 min | **Cook Time:** ~60 min | **Total:** ~90 min + cooling

---

## Ingredients

- 1½ cups / 115g cold unsalted butter, cut into ½-inch cubes, plus more (room temp) for greasing pan
- 1½ cups / 175g all-purpose flour
- ¾ cup / 80g almond flour
- ¾ cup packed / 160g dark brown sugar
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt (Diamond Crystal)
- ¼ teaspoon ground cloves
- ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- ¼ cup / 57g heavy cream
- 6 tablespoons / 142g orange marmalade
- 1 whole orange (for zest)
- Confectioners' sugar, for dusting

---

## Preparation

**STEP 1:** Position a rack in the middle of the oven and heat to 350°F. Butter a 9-inch springform pan.

**STEP 2:** In a medium bowl, combine flour, almond flour, brown sugar, salt, cloves, and black pepper. Rub the cold butter into the flour mixture using your fingers until small pebbles form. *(Alternatively, pulse dry ingredients with butter in a food processor.)* Pour cream over the mixture, stir just until combined, and squeeze until dough forms ¾-inch clumps. Transfer a little less than half of the crumble (1½ loosely packed cups / 210g) to a small bowl and refrigerate.

**STEP 3:** Transfer remaining dough to the buttered pan. Flatten into an even layer by pressing with the bottom of a glass. Bake on middle rack until edges are golden brown and begin to pull away from the sides — about 22 to 25 minutes. Cool in the pan at least 10 minutes. *Tap the surface — it should sound hollow.*

**STEP 4:** Spread orange marmalade in an even layer over the surface. Sprinkle refrigerated crumble evenly over the top. Return to oven and bake until top is golden brown — about 35 minutes. While still warm, zest the orange directly over the top. Cool completely in the pan, then run a knife along the edges before releasing the springform sides. Dust with confectioners' sugar, cut into wedges, and serve.

---

## Notes

- **Cold butter is essential** — do not soften; the cold butter rubbed into flour is what creates the crumbly, sandy texture
- **The hollow tap test** — after the first bake, tap the surface; if it sounds hollow it's set and ready for the marmalade layer
- **Springform pan is non-negotiable** — you need the removable sides to cleanly release the crumble without breaking it
- **Zest while warm** — fresh orange zest over a warm crumble blooms the citrus oils into the surface; don't skip this step
- **Occasion treat** — high butter and sugar content; not aligned with health goals but great for guests or a special dessert
- **Storage:** Keeps covered at room temp up to 3 days; stays crumbly (that's the point)
- **Marmalade swap:** Apricot jam or raspberry preserves work well as substitutes if you want a different flavor profile
